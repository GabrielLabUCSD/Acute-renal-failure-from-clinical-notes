The patient is a 60-year-old male with a past medical history significant for diabetes mellitus type 2, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia. His surgical procedure is an elective cholecystectomy due to symptomatic cholelithiasis. He has a history of multiple episodes of biliary colic characterized by recurrent right upper quadrant pain radiating to the back.

His preoperative laboratory results revealed a well-controlled fasting blood glucose level of 110 mg/dL, with a glycosylated hemoglobin level of 7.2%. His lipid profile showed a total cholesterol level of 200 mg/dL, LDL cholesterol of 130 mg/dL, HDL cholesterol of 40 mg/dL, and triglyceride level of 150 mg/dL. Other preoperative labs, including liver function tests, were within normal limits.

The patient's physical examination was unremarkable except for mild tenderness in the right upper quadrant. His chest x-ray showed no significant abnormalities. An electrocardiogram was obtained, which revealed normal sinus rhythm.

In terms of medications, he is on metformin for his diabetes, losartan for hypertension, and simvastatin for hyperlipidemia. Preoperatively, he was instructed to hold his metformin on the day of the surgery and resume it the following day, as per the anesthesia team's recommendation.

The patient underwent an uneventful laparoscopic cholecystectomy under general anesthesia. The procedure lasted approximately one hour, during which four small incisions were made and the gallbladder was successfully removed. There were no intraoperative complications.

During his postoperative course, the patient was closely monitored in the recovery room for several hours. He tolerated a clear liquid diet and ambulated without difficulty. His vital signs remained stable, and he received appropriate pain management. A surgical consult was obtained, and the patient was discharged home later that day with detailed discharge instructions.

At the two-week follow-up visit, the patient reported improved postoperative pain and good overall recovery. His surgical incisions were healing well without signs of infection. He was advised to resume his regular diet and gradually increase physical activity. The patient was satisfied with the surgical outcome and had no complaints or complications.

Overall, the patient had a successful cholecystectomy without any acute complications. He had an uneventful postoperative course and showed satisfactory healing of surgical incisions. The patient will continue to be followed up for his chronic medical conditions by his primary care physician.
