The patient is a 45-year-old male who is scheduled to undergo surgery for an inguinal hernia repair. He has a past medical history significant for obesity with a BMI of 35, hypertension, and type 2 diabetes mellitus for the past 10 years, which is currently managed with metformin. The patient does not have a history of any previous surgeries. 

During the preoperative evaluation, the patient was found to have well-controlled blood pressure with a reading of 130/80 mmHg. His fasting blood glucose level was 120 mg/dL. His renal function was within normal limits with a serum creatinine level of 1.0 mg/dL. The patient reported no history of allergies to medications or any complications with anesthesia in the past. 

The patient was advised to discontinue his metformin 48 hours prior to the surgery, and an alternative antidiabetic medication, such as insulin, was recommended to maintain his blood glucose levels. Additionally, he was instructed to have a light meal the evening before the surgery and to avoid food and drink after midnight to prepare for anesthesia.

The surgery went smoothly, and the patient tolerated the procedure well without any intraoperative complications. In the immediate postoperative period, the patient was monitored for pain, wound healing, and any signs of infection. He was started on oral pain medications post-surgery and was able to tolerate a regular diet. 

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and he did not experience any significant complications. His blood pressure remained stable throughout the recovery period, and his blood glucose levels were well controlled. The surgical wound healed without any signs of infection or dehiscence.

The patient was advised to continue his regular follow-up appointments with his primary care physician and to monitor his blood pressure and blood glucose levels closely. He was also instructed to maintain a healthy lifestyle, including a balanced diet, regular exercise, and weight management, to reduce the risk of future complications.

He underwent a successful inguinal hernia repair surgery without any intraoperative or postoperative complications. His blood pressure and blood glucose levels were well controlled throughout the surgical and recovery period.
