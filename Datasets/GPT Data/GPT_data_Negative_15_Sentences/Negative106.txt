He has been on antihypertensive and antidiabetic medications for the past five years. His blood pressure and blood sugar levels have been well controlled with medication. He has a history of smoking but quit three years ago.

The patient has had a recent history of chest pain and underwent a cardiac workup. He had a stress test which was normal, ruling out any coronary artery disease. His echocardiogram also showed normal cardiac function. 

On physical examination, the patient is obese with a body mass index (BMI) of 35. He has a large abdominal pannus and his waist circumference is 42 inches. His lungs are clear on auscultation, and his heart sounds are normal. He has pedal edema bilaterally, but his pulses are palpable. 

Laboratory investigations have shown well-controlled blood pressure with a recent reading of 130/80 mmHg. His fasting blood sugar levels have been in the target range, with a recent reading of 120 mg/dL. His renal function is within normal limits with a serum creatinine level of 0.9 mg/dL. His liver function tests are also within normal limits.

The patient is scheduled for an elective surgery to remove his abdominal pannus. The surgical team has advised preoperative optimization of his comorbidities, including blood pressure and blood sugar control. He has been advised to continue his antihypertensive and antidiabetic medications until the morning of surgery.

Following the surgery, the patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He was started on prophylactic antibiotics to prevent surgical site infection. Pain management was provided with a multimodal approach, including opioid and non-opioid analgesics. The patient was able to ambulate on the same day after surgery and was discharged on the third postoperative day.

The wound healing was satisfactory, with no signs of infection. The patient was advised to continue a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise, a balanced diet, and smoking cessation. He was scheduled for regular follow-up visits to monitor his blood pressure and blood sugar levels.

The patient did not experience any complications during his postoperative course. He did not develop any surgical site infections, wound dehiscence, or hematomas. His blood pressure and blood sugar levels remained within normal limits. His renal function was also unaffected postoperatively. The patient expressed satisfaction with the surgical outcome and reported an overall improvement in his quality of life.
