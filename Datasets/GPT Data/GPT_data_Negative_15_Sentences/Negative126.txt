The patient is a 55-year-old male with a medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hyperlipidemia. He presents to the hospital for elective surgery for a hernia repair. The patient reports a history of well-controlled hypertension, for which he takes amlodipine 5mg daily. His diabetes is also well-managed with metformin 1000mg twice daily, and his last hemoglobin A1c was within the target range at 6.5%. He has a history of high cholesterol and takes atorvastatin 40mg daily, with recent lipid panel showing LDL levels of 100 mg/dL.

Regarding the surgical procedure, the patient has no known allergies, and his preoperative labs, including complete blood count, comprehensive metabolic panel, and coagulation studies, are all within normal limits. Furthermore, his preoperative chest X-ray and electrocardiogram are unremarkable. He has been advised to discontinue his antidiabetic medication on the day of the surgery and to resume it once he is allowed to eat.

In terms of his postoperative course, the patient received general anesthesia without complications and tolerated the procedure well. He was started on oral pain medications and instructed on postoperative wound care. The patient was encouraged to ambulate early to prevent complications such as deep vein thrombosis and pneumonia. There were no signs of wound infection, and his pain was well-controlled. Serial postoperative laboratory assessments showed stable renal function, with a creatinine level within his baseline. 

Despite the uneventful postoperative course, the patient complained of mild nausea on the first day after surgery, which was managed conservatively with antiemetic medications. On the second postoperative day, he began passing flatus and had a bowel movement, indicating return of bowel function. His incision site healed well and demonstrated no signs of dehiscence or infection. He was discharged on the third postoperative day with recommendations for continued pain management and follow-up with the surgeon in 10 days.

In summary, the patient's medical history includes hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hyperlipidemia. He underwent an uneventful hernia repair surgery with no intraoperative complications. Postoperatively, he experienced mild nausea, which resolved with medication, and had a normal recovery in terms of bowel function. No wound infection or dehiscence was observed. The patient was discharged in stable condition with plans for further follow-up.
