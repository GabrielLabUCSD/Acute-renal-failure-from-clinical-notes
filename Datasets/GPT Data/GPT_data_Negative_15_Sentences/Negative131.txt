The patient is a 55-year-old male who is planning to have surgery. He has a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. The patient has been on medications for these conditions, including lisinopril, atorvastatin, and metformin.

In terms of surgical history, the patient had a cholecystectomy 10 years ago due to gallstones. There were no complications during or after the surgery. The patient also had a hernia repair surgery 5 years ago, which was uncomplicated as well.

The patient has a family history of cardiovascular disease, with his father having a history of myocardial infarction. The patient is a non-smoker and does not consume alcohol regularly. He exercises regularly and maintains a healthy weight.

On physical examination, the patient's blood pressure is well-controlled with lisinopril, and his fasting blood glucose levels are within normal limits with metformin. His cardiovascular and respiratory examinations are normal.

Preoperatively, the patient's laboratory tests including complete blood counts, electrolytes, and liver function tests are all within normal limits. His fasting blood glucose levels are also controlled.

The patient is scheduled to have a knee replacement surgery due to severe osteoarthritis. He has been experiencing worsening pain and difficulty walking for the past year. Preoperative imaging showed significant joint degeneration.

Postoperatively, the patient is monitored closely for pain management and infection control. He is started on prophylactic antibiotics and physical therapy is initiated to aid in his recovery. The patient's pain is well-controlled with a combination of opioids and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs.

Complications of the surgery include a deep vein thrombosis (DVT) which was suspected due to swelling and pain in the affected leg. A doppler ultrasound was performed and confirmed the diagnosis. The patient was promptly started on anticoagulant therapy and kept under close monitoring.

The patient's postoperative course is otherwise uneventful. He is compliant with physical therapy and follows up regularly with his surgeon. The patient's pain gradually improves, and he gradually resumes normal activities. He is discharged from the hospital without any further complications.

In conclusion, this 55-year-old male patient with a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus is planning to undergo knee replacement surgery for severe osteoarthritis. He has a history of uncomplicated cholecystectomy and hernia repair surgeries. Despite developing a deep vein thrombosis postoperatively, he makes a satisfactory recovery and is discharged without any other complications.
