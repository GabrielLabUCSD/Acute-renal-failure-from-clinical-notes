The patient is a 55-year-old male scheduled to undergo surgery for a hernia repair. He has a significant medical history including hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. The patient has been managing these conditions with medication and regular follow-up visits with his primary care physician. He has a history of smoking but quit several years ago.

In terms of his surgical history, the patient has previously undergone a cholecystectomy for gallstones and a knee arthroscopy for meniscal tear repair. He did not experience any complications during these procedures.

The patient's preoperative workup revealed well-controlled blood pressure, stable blood glucose levels, and normal lipid profile. However, his body mass index (BMI) is currently 34, which puts him in the obese category. The patient has undergone preoperative counseling regarding the importance of weight loss and has been advised to continue his medications as prescribed.

During the surgical procedure, the patient was placed under general anesthesia and the hernia repair was performed without any complications. The surgery lasted approximately 1 hour, and the patient tolerated it well.

In the immediate postoperative period, the patient experienced mild pain at the surgical site, which was managed with analgesics. He was provided with postoperative care instructions and encouraged to ambulate as tolerated. His vital signs remained stable, and there were no signs of infection or wound complications.

Over the next few days, the patient's pain gradually decreased, and he reported improved mobility. He followed the prescribed postoperative care regimen and maintained proper wound care. His appetite returned to normal, and he was able to tolerate a regular diet.

During the hospital stay, the patient received deep venous thrombosis prophylaxis with intermittent pneumatic compression devices. He was also provided with appropriate pain management to ensure a comfortable recovery.

The patient was discharged on the third postoperative day with instructions for self-care and follow-up visits. He continued to recover well at home, reporting minimal pain and no signs of surgical site infection.

In conclusion, this patient underwent a successful hernia repair surgery with no immediate or postoperative complications. The surgical team ensured appropriate pain management and deep venous thrombosis prophylaxis to promote an optimal recovery process. The patient will need to continue managing his chronic medical conditions and actively work towards weight loss. Follow-up appointments will be scheduled to assess his progress and monitor any potential long-term complications.
