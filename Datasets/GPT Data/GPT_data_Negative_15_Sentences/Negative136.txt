Patient X is a 60-year-old male with a past medical history significant for hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and moderate chronic kidney disease. He is scheduled for a surgical procedure in the near future. The patient's hypertension is well-controlled with a combination of an angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitor and a diuretic. His diabetes is managed with oral hypoglycemic medications, achieving adequate glycemic control. 

He has a 20-pack-year smoking history and quit smoking two years ago after being diagnosed with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). His COPD is stable with the use of bronchodilators, and he denies any recent exacerbations. The patient is compliant with his medications and has no known drug allergies.

Patient X had a previous surgical procedure four years ago to remove a benign prostate hyperplasia. The surgery was uneventful, and the patient's recovery was uncomplicated. He had no prior history of anesthesia-related complications.

His recent laboratory tests show stable kidney function with an estimated glomerular filtration rate of 60 ml/min/1.73 m². His hemoglobin A1c level is 7.2%, indicating moderate glycemic control. The patient's electrocardiogram shows sinus rhythm without any significant abnormalities. 

His physical examination is notable for well-controlled blood pressure (130/80 mmHg) and a body mass index of 27 kg/m². He has no peripheral edema and no evidence of organomegaly. His lung sounds are clear to auscultation bilaterally, and there are no other significant findings on examination.

Postoperatively, Patient X underwent surgery without any complications. He was monitored closely in the recovery room and had stable vital signs throughout the immediate postoperative period. He received appropriate pain management and respiratory care to prevent complications.

During his hospital stay, the patient followed the prescribed activity and diet modifications. He was started on prophylactic antibiotics to prevent surgical site infection. The surgical wound healed well without any signs of infection or dehiscence. 

Patient X had an uneventful postoperative course without any significant complications. His blood pressure remained stable, and there were no signs of electrolyte imbalances or exacerbation of his chronic illnesses. His kidney function remained steady, with no evidence of acute kidney injury. 

In summary, Patient X, a 60-year-old male with a history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and moderate chronic kidney disease, underwent surgery without any complications. He had a smooth postoperative course with appropriate wound healing and maintenance of stable vital signs. Thus, there were no acute renal failure complications observed in this patient.
