The patient is a 50-year-old male scheduled for surgery due to a herniated disc in the lumbar spine causing severe back pain and radiating leg pain.
He has a past medical history significant for hypertension, which is well-controlled with medication.
The patient also has a history of smoking, but quit five years ago.
He has no known drug or food allergies.
His preoperative laboratory studies including complete blood count, basic metabolic profile, and coagulation profile were within normal limits.
An EKG was performed and showed sinus rhythm with no significant abnormalities.
The patient underwent a preoperative chest x-ray, which revealed clear lung fields.
His vital signs are stable, with blood pressure consistently averaging around 120/80 mmHg and heart rate around 70 beats per minute.
The patient has been educated about the surgical procedure, its risks, and the expected postoperative course.
He expressed understanding and consented to the surgery.
The patient has been advised to stop taking nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and other medications that may increase bleeding risk.
He was instructed to fast for at least 8 hours before surgery.
Postoperatively, the patient was monitored in the recovery room until he regained consciousness and stable vital signs.
He was started on intravenous pain medication as ordered and closely monitored for any signs of complications.
Fortunately, the patient had a smooth postoperative course and did not experience any complications such as surgical site infection, deep vein thrombosis, or wound dehiscence.
