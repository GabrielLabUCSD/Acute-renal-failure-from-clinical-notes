The patient is a 65-year-old male with a past medical history significant for hypertension, dyslipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. He is scheduled to undergo an elective surgery for the removal of a benign tumor in his colon. The patient has been on medication for his hypertension and dyslipidemia for the past five years. His blood pressure has been well controlled on an angiotensin receptor blocker and a diuretic. His cholesterol levels have also been within the target range with the use of a statin.

The patient's diabetes is managed with lifestyle modifications and an oral hypoglycemic agent. He monitors his blood glucose levels regularly and maintains a stable glycemic control. He does not have any history of diabetic complications such as neuropathy or retinopathy.

Apart from his chronic medical conditions, the patient has a history of smoking for 20 years but quit 10 years ago. He denies any respiratory symptoms or chronic lung disease. His lung function tests are within normal limits.

On examination, the patient is well-nourished and in good general condition. His vital signs are stable with a blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg, heart rate of 70 beats per minute, respiratory rate of 16 breaths per minute, and oxygen saturation of 98% on room air. The patient's cardiopulmonary examination is unremarkable, and he does not have any significant findings on abdominal examination.

Preoperatively, the patient underwent a thorough workup including blood tests, chest X-ray, electrocardiogram, and an anesthetic evaluation. All the results were within normal limits, and he was cleared for surgery.

The surgery was performed successfully using a minimally invasive approach, and the tumor was completely excised. The patient tolerated the procedure well and did not experience any intraoperative complications. He was then transferred to the postoperative care unit for monitoring.

In the postoperative period, the patient's vital signs remained stable. He was started on a clear liquid diet and gradually advanced to a regular diet as tolerated. Pain control was achieved with a patient-controlled analgesia pump, and he reported minimal discomfort.

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, with no signs of infection or wound complications. His bowel function gradually returned, and he passed flatus on postoperative day one and had a bowel movement on postoperative day three. His incision site remained clean and dry, without any signs of redness, swelling, or discharge.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgery with no immediate postoperative complications. He was discharged home on postoperative day four with clear instructions for wound care, pain management, and follow-up appointments. The patient was advised to continue his regular medications and to monitor his blood glucose levels closely in the coming weeks to ensure stable glycemic control.
