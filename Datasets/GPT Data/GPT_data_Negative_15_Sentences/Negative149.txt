The patient has a past medical history of type 2 diabetes, which is managed with diet and exercise alone. His latest HbA1c level was 6.2%, indicating good glycemic control.

He denies any previous surgeries or major medical procedures. He has no known drug allergies.

Laboratory investigations showed a complete blood count within normal limits, as well as normal liver and kidney function tests. His fasting blood glucose level was 110 mg/dL.

No immediate postoperative complications were noted, and the patient was discharged home on the third day following surgery.

The patient was advised to continue taking his antihypertensive and lipid-lowering medications as prescribed. He was also counseled on smoking cessation and referred to a smoking cessation program.
