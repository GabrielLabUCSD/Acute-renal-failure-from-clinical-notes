The patient is a 45-year-old female who is planning to undergo surgery. She has a past medical history significant for hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hyperlipidemia. She was diagnosed with diabetes 10 years ago and has been managing it with oral medications. Her hypertension is controlled with antihypertensive medications.

Her surgical history includes a cholecystectomy 5 years ago and an appendectomy at the age of 25. The patient had an uneventful recovery from both surgeries. She has no known drug allergies.

The patient's social history reveals that she is a non-smoker and consumes alcohol occasionally. She lives with her husband and two children and works as a teacher. She denies any history of illicit drug use.

On review of systems, the patient reports occasional episodes of heartburn and occasional joint pain but denies any chest pain, shortness of breath, or palpitations. She has been compliant with her medications and has not required any recent hospitalizations.

Her family history is notable for hypertension and diabetes in her parents. There is no history of any cardiac conditions or cancer.

Preoperatively, routine lab work was performed, which included a complete blood count, comprehensive metabolic panel, and coagulation studies. All results were within normal limits. An EKG was also performed, showing sinus rhythm without any significant abnormalities.

The patient was given preoperative instructions to discontinue all antiplatelet medications, including aspirin, one week before surgery. She was also instructed to have nothing to eat or drink for at least 8 hours prior to her scheduled surgery time.

Postoperatively, she had an uneventful recovery with no signs of infection or wound complications. Her vital signs remained stable, and she was able to tolerate a regular diet without any issues. The patient received adequate pain management and was discharged home on postoperative day 2.

She was provided with detailed discharge instructions, including wound care and signs of infection. The patient was scheduled for a follow-up appointment in two weeks to ensure proper healing and address any concerns.

The patient did not have any complications postoperatively, including acute renal failure. She recovered well and returned to her usual activities without any limitations. Closely monitoring her blood sugar levels allowed her diabetes to remain under control during the surgical process.
