The patient is a 58-year-old male with a past medical history significant for hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and obesity. He presents to the hospital for surgery as he has been experiencing progressive knee pain and difficulty walking. 

His hypertension is well-controlled on amlodipine 5 mg daily. He was diagnosed with diabetes mellitus type 2 ten years ago and is managed with metformin 1000 mg twice daily. The patient's HbA1c is 7.2%. He has a history of obesity with a body mass index (BMI) of 34.3 kg/m2. 

The patient reports no known drug allergies. He is a non-smoker and does not consume alcohol. His family history is significant for coronary artery disease in his father and mother. He underwent a stress echocardiogram six months ago which was normal. His preoperative EKG is unremarkable.

In terms of surgical history, the patient had an appendectomy at the age of 25. He also underwent a cholecystectomy five years ago due to symptomatic gallstones. There were no complications during either surgery.

On physical examination, the patient is obese with a blood pressure of 138/82 mmHg and a heart rate of 78 beats per minute. He has crepitus with knee movement and tenderness over the medial joint line. Otherwise, the examination is unremarkable.

After surgery, the patient's postoperative course is uneventful. He receives prophylactic antibiotics perioperatively and is started on a postoperative pain management regimen. Physical therapy is initiated on the first postoperative day to facilitate early mobilization. The patient is monitored closely for wound healing and signs of infection.

The patient does not experience any complications during his recovery. He is discharged home on postoperative day three with instructions for wound care and continuation of pain medications. Physical therapy is scheduled as an outpatient to optimize his knee function and range of motion. The patient is advised to follow up with his primary care physician for routine monitoring of his chronic conditions.

In summary, this 58-year-old male with a history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and obesity undergoes surgery for progressive knee pain. His preoperative evaluation demonstrates well-controlled hypertension and diabetes. He has no known drug allergies and no significant surgical history complications. His postoperative course is uneventful, and he is discharged home on postoperative day three.
