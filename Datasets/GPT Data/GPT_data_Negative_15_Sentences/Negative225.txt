The patient is a 65-year-old male who is planning to have surgery for an inguinal hernia repair. He has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. The patient has been on antihypertensive medication for the past 10 years, statins for the past 5 years, and metformin for the past 2 years.

In terms of his surgical history, the patient had a cholecystectomy 10 years ago due to symptomatic gallstones. He had an uneventful postoperative course with no complications. The patient's preoperative laboratory values show a fasting blood glucose of 150 mg/dL, total cholesterol of 220 mg/dL, and blood pressure of 140/90 mmHg.

During the preoperative evaluation, the patient reported no known drug allergies or adverse reactions to anesthesia. He denies smoking or alcohol use. The patient's physical examination revealed an overweight BMI of 30, but otherwise, normal findings were noted. His cardiopulmonary examination was unremarkable, and his lung function tests showed no evidence of any obstructive or restrictive lung disease.

The patient was evaluated by the anesthesiology team and deemed suitable for surgery. He was cleared for surgery with a recommendation to continue his regular medications preoperatively. The patient was instructed to undergo a preoperative fasting period of 8 hours prior to surgery and to receive an antibiotic prophylaxis.

Postoperatively, the patient had a smooth recovery. He was monitored closely for any signs of infection or wound complications. The patient's vital signs remained stable, and he maintained good pain control with orally administered analgesics. He was encouraged to ambulate as tolerated and received appropriate physical therapy.

The patient's postoperative laboratory values showed a postprandial blood glucose level of 180 mg/dL, a total cholesterol level of 200 mg/dL, and blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg. No additional medications were required for glycemic control. The patient did not experience any major postoperative complications such as wound infection, hematoma, or seroma formation.

In conclusion, this 65-year-old male with a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus underwent surgery for an inguinal hernia repair. His preoperative evaluation and laboratory values were within acceptable ranges. The patient had an uneventful perioperative and postoperative course, and he did not experience any major complications following surgery.
