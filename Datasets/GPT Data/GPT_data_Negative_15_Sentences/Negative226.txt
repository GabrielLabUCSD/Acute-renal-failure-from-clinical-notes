The patient is a 60-year-old male who is scheduled to undergo surgery for a hernia repair. He has a past medical history significant for hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. He takes metoprolol, atorvastatin, and metformin for these conditions and reports compliance with his medication regimen.

Regarding his surgical history, the patient previously had a cholecystectomy for gallstones with an unremarkable postoperative course. He did not experience any complications such as wound infection, bleeding, or incisional hernia postoperatively.

The patient denies any known drug allergies but mentions a history of tobacco use for 20 years, which he quit 10 years ago. He also admits to occasional alcohol consumption, social drinker, with no history of alcohol abuse.

On physical examination, the patient's vital signs are within normal limits. He has a body mass index (BMI) of 28, which classifies him as overweight. Auscultation reveals regular heart sounds, normal breath sounds, and no abdominal bruits or murmurs. His hernia is reducible and causes intermittent discomfort on physical exertion.

Laboratory investigations indicate well-controlled diabetes, with a HbA1c level of 6.5%. He has a total cholesterol of 180 mg/dL, low-density lipoprotein (LDL) of 110 mg/dL, and blood pressure readings consistently around 130/80 mmHg. His liver function tests, renal function tests, and complete blood count are all within normal limits.

The patient has been adequately counseled by the surgical team regarding the risks and benefits of the procedure, including potential complications such as infection, bleeding, and hernia recurrence. He understands the need for preoperative fasting and the importance of smoking cessation prior to surgery.

Postoperatively, the patient had an uneventful course. He was monitored closely for pain control and wound healing. He received prophylactic antibiotics, and the surgical incision was noted to be clean, dry, and intact. The patient was able to tolerate a regular diet and ambulate without difficulty. He was discharged home on postoperative day 2 with appropriate pain medications and wound care instructions.

There were no complications noted during his follow-up visits. The patient's incision site healed well, and he did not experience any wound infections or seromas. He has been advised to continue regular follow-up with his primary care physician to monitor his chronic medical conditions.
