Patient is a 55-year-old male with a medical history significant for hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes, who is planning to have surgery for a hernia repair. His renal function was within normal limits, with a serum creatinine level of 0.9 mg/dL. He was also found to have a normal electrocardiogram and echocardiogram.

In the postoperative period, the patient was closely monitored for pain control and wound healing. He was given appropriate analgesic medications and reported adequate pain relief. The wound was inspected daily for signs of infection or dehiscence, but no abnormalities were noted.

The patient's blood pressure and blood glucose levels were closely monitored, and no significant changes or abnormalities were observed. He continued to take his regular medications, including metoprolol, atorvastatin, and metformin, without any adverse effects.

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and he was discharged from the hospital two days after the surgery. He was advised to follow up with his primary care physician and surgeon for routine postoperative care. Instructions were given regarding wound care, activity restrictions, and medication management.

No complications, such as surgical site infection, wound dehiscence, or cardiovascular events, were reported during the patient's postoperative period. The patient did not experience any delays in wound healing or any issues related to anesthesia.
