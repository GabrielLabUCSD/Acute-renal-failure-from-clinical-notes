The patient was diagnosed with hypertension 10 years ago and has been on antihypertensive medication since then. His blood pressure has been well controlled with the current medication regimen.

He was diagnosed with hyperlipidemia 6 years ago and has been on statin therapy to control his cholesterol levels. The patient's lipid profile has shown improvement with this treatment.

Type 2 diabetes mellitus was diagnosed in the patient 8 years ago. He has been managing his blood glucose levels with oral antidiabetic medication and diet modifications. HbA1c levels have been within the target range.

The patient has no known drug allergies and does not smoke. He consumes alcohol occasionally, within moderate limits.

In terms of surgical history, the patient underwent an appendectomy 20 years ago without any complications. He has not had any major surgeries since then.

On physical examination, the patient is in good general health. His vital signs are stable, and there are no significant abnormalities noted.

Laboratory investigations show a normal complete blood count, liver and renal function tests within normal limits, and blood glucose levels under control.

During the postoperative course, the patient recovered well with no immediate complications. He experienced mild pain at the surgical site, which was managed effectively with analgesics.

The patient's incision site was monitored for signs of infection, and dressing changes were done regularly to promote healing.

He was started on a clear liquid diet initially, which was transitioned to a regular diet as tolerated. The patient did not have any issues with digestion or bowel movements.

Daily ambulation was encouraged to prevent any postoperative complications such as deep vein thrombosis.

The patient's postoperative recovery period went smoothly without any significant complications, and he was discharged from the hospital after 3 days.

Upon follow-up, the patient had reported a satisfactory recovery and was comfortable with the surgical outcome. He did not have any postoperative complications such as wound infection, seroma, or hernia recurrence.
