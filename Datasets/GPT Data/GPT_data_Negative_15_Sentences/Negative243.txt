The patient is a 45-year-old male who is planning to have surgery. He has a past medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hyperlipidemia. He has been on antihypertensive medications, oral hypoglycemic agents, and lipid-lowering medications for the past 5 years. His blood pressure and blood sugar levels have been well controlled with these medications.

The patient's surgical history includes an appendectomy at the age of 30 and a hernia repair at the age of 35. Both surgeries were uneventful, and he recovered well without any complications. The patient does not have any known drug allergies.

On physical examination, the patient is found to be overweight with a body mass index (BMI) of 31, indicating obesity. He has no signs of edema or peripheral vascular disease. His cardiovascular examination reveals no murmurs, and his lung sounds are clear. The patient has good pulses in all extremities.

Laboratory investigations show a normal complete blood count, kidney function tests, and liver function tests. His fasting blood sugar levels are well controlled, with a value of 110 mg/dL. Lipid profile shows mildly elevated levels of total cholesterol and low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol, for which he is already on medication.

The patient has been counseled regarding the surgery, its benefits, risks, and potential complications. He has understood the importance of preoperative fasting and other instructions provided to him. He has provided informed consent for the procedure.

Postoperatively, the patient is closely monitored in the recovery room. He is maintained on intravenous fluids and given analgesics for pain control. His vital signs remain stable, with blood pressure within normal limits. He is able to tolerate oral medications and is started on antibiotics to prevent surgical site infection.

The patient's postoperative course is unremarkable. He does not experience any major complications such as wound dehiscence, infection, or hematoma. He is able to ambulate and perform activities of daily living without difficulty. The patient is discharged from the hospital on the third postoperative day, following a routine check-up by the surgical team.

In summary, this 45-year-old male patient with a past medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hyperlipidemia is planning to have surgery. His preoperative evaluation and laboratory investigations have been performed, showing well-controlled blood pressure, blood sugar levels, and lipid profile. He has received counseling and provided informed consent for the surgery. Postoperatively, his recovery is uneventful, and he is discharged from the hospital without any complications related to the surgery.
