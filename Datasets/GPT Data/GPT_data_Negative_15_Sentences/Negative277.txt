In the postoperative course, the patient is initially kept on intravenous pain medication and is advanced to oral pain medication as tolerated. He is encouraged to ambulate and participate in breathing exercises to prevent postoperative complications.

The patient's wound heals well, with no signs of infection or dehiscence. His pain is well controlled, and he does not require any additional intervention. There are no signs of surgical site infection or wound complications.

The patient is discharged on postoperative day 2 with appropriate instructions for wound care, pain medication, and follow-up. He is advised to continue his regular medications and to schedule a follow-up appointment in two weeks.

Overall, the patient's postoperative course was uneventful, with no complications observed. He recovered well, experienced no renal complications, and was discharged in a satisfactory condition.
