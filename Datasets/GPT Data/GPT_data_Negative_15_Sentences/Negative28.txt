The patient is a 65-year-old male, Mr. Smith, who is planning to have surgery. His past medical history includes hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. He has been on antihypertensive medications, statins, and oral hypoglycemic agents for several years.

He has a history of smoking for 30 years but quit 10 years ago. He does not drink alcohol or use illicit drugs. He has no known drug allergies.

Mr. Smith also has a family history of cardiovascular diseases, with his father having a myocardial infarction at the age of 55. He has been compliant with his medications and has regular follow-up visits with his primary care physician.

His surgical history includes a cholecystectomy 15 years ago. He has not had any major surgeries since then. Mr. Smith has experienced no complications during or after the cholecystectomy procedure.

Physical examination reveals a well-nourished patient with a body mass index of 28. His blood pressure is well-controlled at 130/80 mmHg. Auscultation of the heart and lungs is unremarkable. His abdomen is soft and non-tender without any palpable masses or organomegaly. Neurological examination is intact, and there are no signs of peripheral artery disease.

Preoperative laboratory investigations show a fasting blood glucose of 150 mg/dL, HbA1c of 7.2%, total cholesterol of 230 mg/dL, LDL cholesterol of 140 mg/dL, HDL cholesterol of 50 mg/dL, and triglycerides of 160 mg/dL. Renal function tests are within normal limits.

Mr. Smith has been advised to stop his oral hypoglycemic medication the night before the surgery and to fast for 8 hours prior to the procedure. He will be kept on his antihypertensive and statin medications. Preoperative counseling has been provided regarding the procedure, expected outcomes, potential complications, and risks associated with anesthesia.

Postoperatively, Mr. Smith had an uneventful course with no major complications. He had adequate pain control and was able to ambulate on the first postoperative day. His blood glucose levels were well-controlled with insulin therapy. His incision site remained clean and showed no signs of infection. No signs of thromboembolic events or respiratory complications were observed. The patient was discharged on the third postoperative day with appropriate instructions for wound care and follow-up.
