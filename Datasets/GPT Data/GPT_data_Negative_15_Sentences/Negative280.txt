The patient is a 50-year-old male with a history of hypertension and diabetes mellitus who is planning to have elective surgery. He has been taking antihypertensive medication for the past 10 years and has his blood pressure well controlled. His diabetes is managed with oral hypoglycemic agents, and his blood glucose levels are within the target range. The patient has no known drug allergies.

In terms of surgical history, the patient underwent an appendectomy 15 years ago without any complications. He has no history of any other major surgeries or anesthesia-related complications. He does not smoke and consumes alcohol in moderation.

During preoperative assessment, the patient's physical examination was unremarkable with stable vital signs. His blood work, including complete blood count, renal function tests, liver function tests, and coagulation profile, were all within normal limits. His electrocardiogram showed sinus rhythm without any significant abnormalities. A chest X-ray revealed no acute cardiopulmonary abnormalities.

In terms of medication management, the patient was advised to discontinue any blood-thinning medications, such as aspirin and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, at least one week before surgery. He was also instructed to continue taking his antihypertensive and hypoglycemic medications on the day of surgery with a sip of water.

The surgical procedure was performed under general anesthesia without any complications. The patient was closely monitored during the operation, and his vital signs remained stable throughout the procedure. Postoperative pain was managed with a combination of intravenous opioids and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs. The patient was encouraged to ambulate and deep breathe to prevent postoperative complications.

The patient's postoperative recovery was uneventful, with no immediate complications such as wound infection or dehiscence. He was able to tolerate a regular diet and was gradually weaned off the intravenous fluids. His pain was well controlled, and he did not require any additional analgesics. The patient's vital signs remained stable, and his blood glucose levels were within the target range.

During the postoperative period, the patient remained in the hospital for 3 days under close observation. He was discharged home with appropriate postoperative care instructions and follow-up scheduled with his primary care physician. The patient was advised to continue his antihypertensive and hypoglycemic medications as usual to maintain optimal control.

In summary, this patient with a history of hypertension and diabetes mellitus underwent elective surgery without complications. He had no significant past medical or surgical history and had well-controlled blood pressure and blood glucose levels prior to the procedure. The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, with prompt recovery and stable vital signs. He did not experience any immediate complications and was discharged home with appropriate instructions for postoperative care and follow-up.
