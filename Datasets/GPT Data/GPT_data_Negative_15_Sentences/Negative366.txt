Postoperatively, the patient's recovery is uneventful. He is monitored closely for any signs of complications, such as infection or hematoma formation at the surgical site. The surgical wound heals well and shows no signs of infection.

The patient's pain is adequately managed with analgesics, and he is encouraged to ambulate and engage in breathing exercises to prevent postoperative complications such as atelectasis and deep vein thrombosis.

No complications are noted during the patient's hospital stay. He is provided with discharge instructions, including wound care, activity limitations, and when to resume his regular medications.

In conclusion, the patient is a middle-aged male with a past medical history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, but no known cardiac disease. He is undergoing surgery for a hernia repair and has an uneventful postoperative course without any complications.
