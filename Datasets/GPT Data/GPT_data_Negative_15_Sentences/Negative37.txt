Regarding the patient's past surgical history, he underwent an appendectomy 10 years ago without any complications. He has no known allergies to medications or latex. Additionally, the patient has a history of smoking but quit five years ago. He denies any regular alcohol or illicit substance use.

In terms of his family history, there is a positive family history of heart disease, with his father having suffered a myocardial infarction at the age of 60. The patient's mother is alive and well with no significant medical history.

The patient's preoperative laboratory results are within normal limits. His complete blood count shows no signs of anemia or infection. His liver and renal function tests are also normal. His fasting blood glucose level is well-controlled. His lipid profile reveals mildly elevated LDL cholesterol levels.

Considering the patient's postoperative course, he recovers well initially and is discharged home on postoperative day three. He reports minimal pain, which is well-controlled with oral analgesics. He is advised to resume his regular medications, including his antihypertensive, antidiabetic, and cholesterol-lowering medications.

During his postoperative follow-up visit at two weeks, the patient is noted to have a small surgical wound infection, which is successfully managed with antibiotics and wound care. Apart from this, he does not have any other complications.

In conclusion, this 55-year-old male patient, with a history of hypertension, type 2 diabetes, and hyperlipidemia, is planning to have surgery. His preoperative evaluations are reassuring, and he has an uneventful postoperative course except for a minor wound infection, which is promptly addressed.
