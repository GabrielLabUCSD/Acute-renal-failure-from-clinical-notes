In terms of his respiratory health, the patient has COPD and uses albuterol as needed for symptom relief. He has had a few exacerbations in the past but has not required hospitalization. The patient has undergone pulmonary function tests which showed mild to moderate airflow limitation.

The patient will be undergoing surgery for a hernia repair. He has been thoroughly evaluated prior to the procedure, including a complete blood count, comprehensive metabolic panel, coagulation studies, and an electrocardiogram (EKG) which were all within normal limits. His pulmonary function tests were also reviewed and showed no significant changes.

Postoperatively, the patient experienced a smooth course and his pain was well managed with analgesics. He followed all postoperative instructions including wound care and was able to resume regular activities gradually. There were no signs of infection or wound complications noted during the follow-up visits.

The patient's diabetes management remained stable during the postoperative period. His blood glucose levels were monitored closely, and they remained within the target range. No adjustments to his medication regimen were required.

Additionally, his blood pressure remained well controlled, and no changes to his antihypertensive medication were necessary. He did not experience any significant respiratory complications after surgery and did not require any additional treatment for his COPD.

In summary, this 65-year-old male patient with a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, type 2 diabetes mellitus, coronary artery disease, and COPD underwent surgery for a hernia repair without any complications. His chronic medical conditions were well managed during the perioperative period, and his blood glucose levels and blood pressure remained within target ranges.
