The patient is a 55-year-old female who has a medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hypothyroidism. She presents to the hospital for surgery, which involves the removal of a benign ovarian cyst. She is planning to have the surgery due to chronic abdominal pain and intermittent urinary symptoms.

During the preoperative evaluation, her vital signs are stable, and she has no evidence of an active infection. Her laboratory values indicate well-controlled blood glucose levels, with a hemoglobin A1C of 7%. She has a normal lipid profile and thyroid function studies within the target range for her hypothyroidism.

The patient has a previous surgical history, including cholecystectomy and a uterine fibroid removal. She has a known allergy to penicillin, which is documented in her medical records. Otherwise, her surgical history has been unremarkable, with no previous complications.

In terms of medications, she takes lisinopril for hypertension, metformin for diabetes, and levothyroxine for her hypothyroidism. She is compliant with her medications and has had no recent changes in her regimen.

As for family history, her mother had hypertension and her father had type 2 diabetes. Her siblings are healthy and have had no surgical procedures. She denies any history of blood clotting disorders or bleeding tendencies in her family.

The patient is otherwise healthy, with no recent weight loss or gain, no significant exercise limitations, and no respiratory symptoms. She does not smoke and has a social history that includes occasional alcohol consumption.

Following surgery, the patient had an uneventful postoperative course. She woke up from surgery without any immediate complications and experienced a smooth recovery in the post-anesthesia care unit. Her pain was adequately controlled with intravenous analgesics and transitioned to oral pain medications before discharge.

The patient did not experience any respiratory complications or signs of infection during her hospital stay. Her surgical incision site healed well without any signs of infection or dehiscence. She was provided with appropriate postoperative instructions regarding wound care, activity restrictions, and when to seek medical attention if needed.

Throughout her hospital stay, the patient's blood glucose levels were closely monitored to ensure optimal glycemic control. Her blood pressure remained stable, and there were no episodes of hypotension or hypertension.

In summary, the patient is a 55-year-old female with a medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hypothyroidism, who underwent a surgery for the removal of a benign ovarian cyst. She had a previous surgical history including cholecystectomy and uterine fibroid removal, with no complications in the past. Her postoperative course was uneventful, without any respiratory complications or signs of infection. She had adequate pain control, and her surgical incision healed well. Blood glucose levels were closely monitored, and her blood pressure remained stable. The patient was discharged with appropriate postoperative instructions.
