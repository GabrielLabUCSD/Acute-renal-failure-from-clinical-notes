He was diagnosed with hypertension 10 years ago and is currently on a combination of antihypertensive medications, which include amlodipine and lisinopril. His blood pressure has been well controlled with these medications. He was diagnosed with hyperlipidemia 5 years ago and is currently on a statin, which has effectively lowered his cholesterol levels.

Additionally, patient X was diagnosed with type 2 diabetes mellitus 6 years ago. His diabetes is managed with lifestyle modifications and metformin. He maintains good glycemic control with a hemoglobin A1c consistently below 7%. Patient X has been advised to continue taking metformin until the day before surgery, following which he is instructed to hold the medication.

The patient had a previous surgical procedure 3 years ago for the removal of his gallbladder due to chronic cholecystitis. The surgery was uneventful, and he recovered without any complications.

Patient X does not have a history of acute renal failure. He has normal kidney function with an estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) of 80 mL/min/1.73m^2. His serum creatinine consistently measures within the normal range.

During the preoperative assessment, it was noted that patient X has no known drug allergies and has never had any adverse reactions to anesthesia. He has been fully informed about the procedure he is scheduled for, as well as its potential risks and benefits. The patient has given his consent for the surgery and has received appropriate preoperative instructions.

Postoperatively, patient X had a smooth recovery and was transferred to the surgical ward. He experienced minimal pain, which was managed with oral analgesics. The patient's vital signs remained stable, and he did not develop any signs of infection. Regular monitoring of his blood glucose levels was performed, and his diabetes was managed with insulin therapy to maintain glycemic control.

There were no significant complications during the patient's postoperative course. He was encouraged to ambulate early and received physiotherapy to prevent any potential complications, such as deep vein thrombosis. Patient X was discharged on the third postoperative day with appropriate instructions for wound care and pain management.

In conclusion, patient X, a 45-year-old male with a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus, underwent surgery without any complications. The patient's postoperative course was unremarkable, and he was discharged home in stable condition.
