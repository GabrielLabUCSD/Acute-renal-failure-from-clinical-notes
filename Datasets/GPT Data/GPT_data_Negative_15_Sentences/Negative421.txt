His hypertension is well controlled with lisinopril 10 mg daily, and his hyperlipidemia is managed with atorvastatin 20 mg daily. His diabetes is controlled with metformin 1000 mg twice daily.

The patient's surgical history includes an appendectomy performed 10 years ago. The surgery was uncomplicated, and the patient had an uneventful recovery period. He has no known drug allergies.

Routine preoperative lab work was performed, which revealed a normal complete blood count and comprehensive metabolic panel. His blood glucose levels were within the target range, and his HbA1c was 6.8%.

The patient's physical examination was unremarkable except for the presence of an umbilical hernia. He has a body mass index (BMI) of 29, indicating he is overweight.

During the surgery, general anesthesia was administered, and the patient remained stable throughout the procedure. The hernia was successfully repaired, and there were no intraoperative complications.

Postoperatively, the patient's pain was managed with analgesics, and he was closely monitored for any signs of infection or bleeding. He was able to ambulate and tolerate a regular diet on the first postoperative day. The surgeon documented good wound healing, and the patient was discharged home on the second postoperative day.

During follow-up visits, the patient reported a gradual improvement in his pain and overall well-being. He was advised to maintain a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and a balanced diet. No postoperative complications were reported.

In conclusion, this 45-year-old male with a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes underwent an uneventful umbilical hernia repair without any intraoperative or postoperative complications.
