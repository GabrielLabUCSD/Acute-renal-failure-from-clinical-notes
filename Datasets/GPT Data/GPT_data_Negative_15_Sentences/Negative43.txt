Patient X is a 55-year-old male who is planning to undergo surgery. His medical history includes hypertension, type 2 diabetes mellitus, hyperlipidemia, and a past smoking history of 30 pack-years. He has been taking metoprolol and lisinopril for his blood pressure control, metformin for diabetes management, and atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia.

The patient has a BMI of 32, indicating obesity, and has a sedentary lifestyle. He reports occasional episodes of exertional chest pain but denies any recent cardiac events. He has never been diagnosed with coronary artery disease and has a normal EKG.

On further evaluation, his cardiac workup with a stress test and echocardiogram showed no evidence of significant coronary stenosis or any other structural cardiac abnormalities.

Patient X is also on a multivitamin supplement and takes occasional over-the-counter pain relievers for joint pain due to osteoarthritis.

His preoperative laboratory tests were within normal limits, including complete blood count, liver function tests, and electrolyte levels. He has a normal creatinine level, ruling out impaired renal function.

During the postoperative course, the patient experienced no major complications. His vital signs remained stable throughout the recovery period. He was closely monitored for any signs of infection, thrombosis, or respiratory distress, but none were noted. He had a normal wound healing process without signs of dehiscence or infection.

Patient X was able to tolerate a clear liquid diet on the first postoperative day, followed by a regular diet on the second day. His pain was well-managed with opioid and non-opioid analgesics, and he reported minimal discomfort.

The patient's recovery was uneventful, and he was discharged home on the fourth postoperative day. He was advised to continue his regular medications and follow up with his primary care provider. Additional recommendations included smoking cessation, weight loss, and initiation of a structured exercise program to improve his overall health.

In conclusion, Patient X underwent surgery without any complications and had a smooth postoperative course. His medical history, including hypertension, diabetes, and hyperlipidemia, was well-managed prior to surgery. No acute renal failure was observed, and the patient was discharged home in stable condition.
