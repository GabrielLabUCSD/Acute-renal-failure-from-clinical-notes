The patient has a history of a previous surgery, as he underwent a cholecystectomy five years ago without any complications. He reported an unremarkable postoperative recovery during that time, with no issues of infection, wound complications, or deep vein thrombosis. 

He currently takes aspirin 81 mg daily for cardiovascular prophylaxis but is willing to pause it prior to surgery as per the surgeon's advice. He reports no known allergies to medications or latex. Preoperative laboratory tests reveal normal kidney function, with a serum creatinine level of 0.9 mg/dL. 

The patient's surgical team ensured appropriate preoperative education and counseling regarding the surgery, including potential risks, benefits, and alternatives. The patient expressed understanding and provided informed consent. 

Postoperatively, the patient had an uneventful course. He received appropriate pain management and deep vein thrombosis prophylaxis. His vital signs were stable throughout his hospital stay, with no signs of infection or wound complications. He was able to ambulate without difficulty and had no issues with bowel movements. 

The patient was discharged home three days after surgery with clear postoperative instructions. He was advised to continue his regular medications and to follow up with his primary care physician and surgeon as scheduled.

The patient was discharged home with appropriate instructions and follow-up plans.
