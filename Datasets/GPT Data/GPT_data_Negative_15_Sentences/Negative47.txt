The patient is a 65-year-old male planning to undergo surgery for the removal of a benign tumor in his colon. He has a significant medical history, including hypertension and hyperlipidemia, for which he takes medications regularly. He has a prior surgical history of a cholecystectomy and arthroscopic knee surgery. Additionally, he has a known allergy to penicillin.

The patient's preoperative workup includes routine blood tests, which revealed normal kidney function, liver function, and electrolyte levels. He had an electrocardiogram which showed sinus rhythm and no significant abnormalities. Chest X-ray demonstrated clear lung fields. The patient's overall health is considered good, with stable vital signs and no evidence of active infection or inflammation.

During the postoperative course, the patient was closely monitored in the recovery unit for signs of infection, bleeding, and any other surgical complications. He required intravenous pain medication initially, which was transitioned to oral pain medications as his pain improved. The patient was able to tolerate a regular diet and had no issues with bowel movements after surgery.

A few days after the surgery, the patient developed a fever and increased abdominal pain. Further evaluation revealed an infection at the surgical site, which was managed with antibiotics and wound care. The patient's condition improved with treatment, and he was discharged home with a course of oral antibiotics. Close follow-up was arranged to ensure proper healing of the surgical site.

In the postoperative period, the patient did not experience any complications related to his hypertension or hyperlipidemia. His blood pressure was well controlled with his usual antihypertensive medications, and his lipid profile remained stable. The patient was advised to continue taking these medications as prescribed.

Overall, while the patient experienced a postoperative infection at the surgical site, he did not develop any other major complications. His preoperative optimization and postoperative management played a crucial role in his recovery. The patient was satisfied with the surgical outcome and was pleased to have the tumor successfully removed.
