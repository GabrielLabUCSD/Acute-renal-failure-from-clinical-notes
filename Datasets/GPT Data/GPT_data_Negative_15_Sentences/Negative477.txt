The patient is a 45-year-old male who is planning to undergo surgery for a hernia repair. He has a past medical history significant for hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. In terms of surgical history, the patient has not had any prior surgeries. He does not have any known drug allergies and denies any history of adverse reactions to anesthesia. He is a non-smoker and does not consume alcohol.

The patient's preoperative labs were significant for elevated total cholesterol levels at 270 mg/dL and triglyceride levels of 200 mg/dL. His blood pressure was controlled with medication, with a reading of 130/80 mmHg. His renal function, as assessed by serum creatinine levels, was within normal limits.

The patient was advised to stop taking his blood thinner, aspirin, one week prior to surgery and advised to continue taking his other regular medications with a sip of water on the day of surgery. He was also instructed to fast from solid foods and clear liquids for eight hours before the procedure.

Following surgery, the patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He was closely monitored in the recovery room for about an hour and then transferred to the surgical ward. The patient was instructed to ambulate as tolerated and use incentive spirometry to prevent postoperative complications such as pneumonia. He was given pain medication as needed and started on a regular diet once his bowel function returned to normal.

During his hospital stay, the patient did not experience any significant complications such as wound infections, bleeding, or deep vein thrombosis. He was discharged on postoperative day two in stable condition. The surgical site was healing well, and the patient was advised to follow up with his primary care physician for routine postoperative care.

In conclusion, the patient, a 45-year-old male with hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity, successfully underwent hernia repair surgery. He had an uneventful postoperative course without any significant complications. The patient was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day two and was advised to continue with his regular medications and follow up with his primary care physician.
