The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a medical history significant for hypertension, type 2 diabetes, and hyperlipidemia. He is scheduled for surgery for a hernia repair. He has been taking antihypertensive medication, including lisinopril, for the past 5 years and has his blood pressure well controlled. His diabetes is well managed with metformin, and his last hemoglobin A1c was 6.8%, indicating good glucose control. He takes atorvastatin to manage his hyperlipidemia, and his cholesterol levels have been within normal range.

The patient has a history of smoking, with a 30-pack year history, but quit smoking one year ago. He reports no current symptoms of dyspnea or chronic cough. He denies any history of respiratory or cardiac problems. He has no previous surgical history and has not required any hospitalization in the past.

On physical examination, the patient appears in overall good health. Vital signs are stable, with blood pressure 128/80 mmHg, heart rate 78 beats per minute, respiratory rate 16 breaths per minute, and temperature 98.6°F. He has a body mass index (BMI) of 26 kg/m², placing him in the overweight category. Auscultation of the heart reveals regular rate and rhythm, with no murmurs, rubs, or gallops. Lung examination is clear with good air entry bilaterally. Abdominal examination reveals a reducible hernia with no signs of incarceration or strangulation.

The patient's preoperative laboratory studies are within normal limits. His complete blood count shows no signs of anemia or infection. His renal function is normal, with a serum creatinine of 0.9 mg/dL. Liver function tests and coagulation profile are also within normal range.

The patient's surgery goes smoothly, without any intraoperative complications. He undergoes general anesthesia and the hernia repair is performed using mesh. The operative time is approximately 1 hour, and there is minimal blood loss. The patient is monitored closely postoperatively in the recovery room and demonstrates stable vital signs. He is started on regular oral diet soon after surgery and tolerates it well. Pain control is managed with opioid analgesics and the patient's pain is well controlled. He is instructed on postoperative wound care and activity restrictions.

The patient's postoperative course is uneventful, and he recovers well from the surgery. He is discharged home on the second postoperative day. He is advised to follow-up with his primary care physician in 2 weeks for a wound check and evaluation of any persistent symptoms. The patient does not experience any complications during his recovery period, such as wound infection, bleeding, or hernia recurrence. Overall, he has a successful surgical outcome and is satisfied with the results.
