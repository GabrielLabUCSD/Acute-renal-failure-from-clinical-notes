The patient, a 56-year-old male, has a medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hyperlipidemia. He is scheduled to have surgery for a hernia repair. His blood pressure has been well-controlled on a combination of antihypertensive medications, including lisinopril and amlodipine. His diabetes is managed with metformin and he has good glycemic control with a HbA1c of 6.5%. His lipid profile is within normal limits with the use of atorvastatin.

The patient has a history of smoking but quit 10 years ago, reducing the risk of complications related to smoking. He denies any other past surgical intervention. There is no family history of clotting disorders or any known allergies or adverse drug reactions. He does not consume alcohol and denies any illicit drug use.

Preoperatively, the patient underwent a comprehensive medical evaluation, including an electrocardiogram and chest X-ray, both of which were unremarkable. Laboratory tests, including complete blood count, renal and liver function tests, and coagulation panel, were within normal limits. He received preoperative antibiotic prophylaxis and was informed about the importance of ambulation and deep breathing exercises postoperatively.

The patient was taken to the operating room for a hernia repair under general anesthesia. Intraoperatively, there were no significant complications, and the surgery proceeded smoothly. The patient was extubated without complications and transferred to the recovery room in stable condition.

In the postoperative period, the patient was closely monitored for pain control and wound healing. He received intravenous fluids and analgesics as needed. Early ambulation was encouraged to prevent complications such as deep vein thrombosis. The patient tolerated a regular diet and had adequate urinary output.

During the postoperative course, the patient did not develop any signs of infection, such as fever, redness, or discharge at the surgical site. His vital signs remained stable, and he did not experience any cardiovascular or respiratory complications. Regular wound care was provided, ensuring proper healing and preventing any postoperative wound-related complications.

The patient's postoperative recovery was uneventful, and he was discharged home on the fifth postoperative day in good condition. He was instructed to continue taking his regular medications and to follow up with his primary care physician in two weeks. He was also educated about potential signs of surgical site infection and advised to seek medical attention if they occurred.

In summary, the patient has a medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hyperlipidemia. He underwent an uneventful surgery for a hernia repair and had a smooth postoperative course without any complications noted. The patient was discharged home in stable condition and provided with appropriate postoperative care instructions.
