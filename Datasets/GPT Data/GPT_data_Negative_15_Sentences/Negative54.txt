The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and obesity. He has been managing his hypertension with a combination of antihypertensive medications including angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors and diuretics. His diabetes has been controlled through lifestyle modification and oral antidiabetic medications, and his obesity is managed with diet and exercise.

The patient is scheduled to undergo surgery for a hernia repair. He has been advised to discontinue his antihypertensive medications for 24 hours before the surgery, under the supervision of his primary care physician. He is also instructed to discontinue his antidiabetic medications on the day of surgery and have a preoperative fasting blood glucose level check.

Following the surgery, the patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He was monitored for postoperative pain, wound healing, and signs of infection. His pain was managed with analgesics and he was instructed on proper wound care. No signs of infection or wound dehiscence were observed.

The patient was able to ambulate on the day of surgery and was advised to continue light activities to prevent postoperative complications such as deep vein thrombosis. He was also instructed to maintain a high-fiber, low-fat diet to minimize postoperative constipation.

During the postoperative follow-up visit, the patient reported no significant pain or complications. His wound was healing well with no signs of infection. The patient's blood pressure and blood glucose levels were within normal range. He was advised to resume his antihypertensive and antidiabetic medications as recommended by his primary care physician.

In conclusion, the patient's medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and obesity were managed well prior to the surgery. The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, with no complications observed. Proper management of pain, wound care, and early mobilization contributed to the successful outcome. The patient's hypertension and diabetes control were maintained during the postoperative period, and no acute renal failure was reported.
