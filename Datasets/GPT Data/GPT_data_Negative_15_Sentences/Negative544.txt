The patient is a 55-year-old male who is scheduled to undergo surgery. He has a medical history significant for hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. His blood pressure has been well-controlled on a combination of lisinopril and amlodipine. His diabetes is managed with metformin and he maintains good glycemic control with a HbA1c level of 6.5%. His cholesterol levels are maintained at a normal range with atorvastatin.

The patient has a body mass index (BMI) of 32 and his weight has been stable for the past few years. He does not have any history of alcohol or tobacco use. His exercise tolerance is limited due to obesity and he regularly experiences shortness of breath during physical activity. He has been advised to work on weight reduction and increase physical activity to improve his overall health status.

His surgical procedure involves an elective cholecystectomy due to symptomatic cholelithiasis. Preoperatively, the patient was assessed by a cardiologist who stated that his cardiac status was stable and deemed him fit for surgery. The anesthesiologist also evaluated the patient and found no contraindications to anesthesia.

Postoperatively, the patient was transferred to the surgical ward. He was ambulatory on the first postoperative day and his pain was well-controlled with regular analgesia. He started a clear liquid diet and tolerated it without any issues. On the second postoperative day, he advanced to a regular diet and was able to tolerate it well. His incision site remained clean and dry, with no signs of infection. The patient was regularly monitored for vital signs and remained stable throughout the postoperative course.

The patient received appropriate deep vein thrombosis prophylaxis in the form of sequential compression devices and subcutaneous heparin injections. He was also encouraged to do regular breathing exercises to prevent atelectasis and deep breathing exercises to aid in pain control. The patient's surgical wound was assessed daily for any signs of infection or dehiscence.

There were no complications evident during the patient's postoperative course. He was discharged on the fourth postoperative day with instructions for wound care and pain management. The patient was advised to follow up with his primary care physician for further monitoring of his chronic medical conditions. He was also given advice regarding lifestyle modifications to improve his overall health, specifically focusing on weight reduction and regular exercise. The patient was informed about potential complications related to surgery, but none were experienced during his hospital stay.
