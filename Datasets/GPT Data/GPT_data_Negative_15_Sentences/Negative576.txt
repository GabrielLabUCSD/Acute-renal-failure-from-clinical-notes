The patient's preoperative evaluation reveals well-controlled blood pressure and blood glucose levels. His body mass index (BMI) is 35, indicating obesity. He has been counseled on the importance of weight management and lifestyle modifications to improve his overall health.

During the surgery, the patient was placed under general anesthesia and the hernia repair was performed laparoscopically. The procedure went smoothly without any intraoperative complications. The surgeon noted a small inguinal hernia that was repaired with mesh.

He experienced some postoperative pain, which was managed with analgesic medications. The nursing staff assisted him with early ambulation and taught him deep breathing exercises to prevent pulmonary complications.

The surgical wound was assessed for signs of infection, but no abnormalities were noted. The patient was instructed to keep the incision clean and dry, and to report any signs of redness, swelling, or discharge.

There were no complications directly related to the surgery, such as wound dehiscence or infection. The patient's blood pressure and blood glucose were closely monitored and remained within normal limits throughout the hospital stay. The surgical team closely collaborated with the patient's primary care physician to manage his chronic conditions during the perioperative period.

Prior to discharge, the patient was provided with postoperative care instructions, including limitations on physical activity, wound care, and medication management. He was scheduled for a follow-up visit with the surgeon to assess the incision site and overall recovery progress.
