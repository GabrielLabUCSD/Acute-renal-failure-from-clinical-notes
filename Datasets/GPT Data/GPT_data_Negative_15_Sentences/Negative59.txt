Patient X is a 60-year-old male who presented with a medical history significant for hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. He was scheduled for surgery due to a ruptured appendicitis. He has no known drug allergies.

His hypertension has been well-controlled on a combination of lisinopril and hydrochlorothiazide, with his blood pressure consistently below 130/80 mmHg. His hyperlipidemia is managed with a statin, resulting in LDL cholesterol levels below 100 mg/dL. His type 2 diabetes is controlled through diet and exercise, with glycosylated hemoglobin (HbA1c) levels consistently below 7%. He has normal renal function, with no evidence of chronic kidney disease.

His preoperative laboratory workup revealed normal complete blood count, blood chemistry panel, and coagulation profile. His fasting blood glucose level was 110 mg/dL and his random blood glucose level was 140 mg/dL. He had normal liver function tests and lipid profile. His electrocardiogram showed sinus rhythm with no significant abnormalities.

Patient X underwent surgery under general anesthesia. The surgery was uneventful, with no intraoperative complications reported. His vital signs remained stable throughout the procedure. Postoperatively, he was carefully monitored in the recovery room and was transitioned to the surgical ward on the same day.

During his postoperative course, the patient experienced mild postoperative pain, which was effectively managed with analgesics. He was encouraged to ambulate, perform deep breathing exercises, and use incentive spirometry to prevent complications such as pneumonia or deep vein thrombosis. His bowel function returned within the expected timeframe, and he tolerated a regular diet well.

No postoperative complications were reported, including wound infections, urinary tract infections, or respiratory complications. Patient X was discharged home on postoperative day 3 with a prescription for a short course of oral analgesics and instructions for wound care. He was scheduled for a follow-up appointment in one week.

In summary, Patient X, a 60-year-old male with a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus, underwent successful surgery for a ruptured appendicitis. His preoperative evaluation revealed well-controlled comorbidities and normal laboratory results. He had an uneventful intraoperative period and a smooth postoperative course, without the occurrence of any complications. Patient X was discharged home with appropriate pain management and follow-up plans.
