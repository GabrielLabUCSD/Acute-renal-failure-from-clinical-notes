The patient is a 45-year-old male with a past medical history significant for hypertension and type 2 diabetes. He has been controlled on an angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor and metformin. His blood pressure has been well controlled for the past year. He has no significant family history of surgical complications or adverse reactions to anesthesia.

The patient presented with a recent diagnosis of gallstones causing severe abdominal pain. After further evaluation, it was determined that the patient required cholecystectomy surgery to remove the gallbladder. Preoperative labs revealed normal kidney function with a serum creatinine level of 1.0 mg/dL. The patient was continued on his regular medications, including the ACE inhibitor and metformin.

The patient underwent the cholecystectomy surgery without any complications. Anesthesia was induced using general anesthesia, and the procedure was completed laparoscopically. Intraoperatively, there were no unexpected findings, and the entire gallbladder with stones was successfully removed. Estimated blood loss was minimal. The patient tolerated the surgery well without any intraoperative hemodynamic or respiratory complications.

Postoperatively, the patient was monitored closely for any signs of infection or bleeding. He received postoperative pain management with opioids and was maintained on his preoperative medications. The patient had an uneventful recovery and was able to ambulate and tolerate a regular diet the day after surgery. His blood pressure remained stable throughout the hospital stay. There were no signs of wound infection or dehiscence, and his postoperative labs, including kidney function, remained within normal limits.

The patient was discharged on postoperative day 2 with appropriate pain medications and instructions on wound care. He was advised to slowly resume his regular activities and to follow up with his primary care physician in two weeks. The patient was given a hotline number to contact in case of any concerns or complications.

At the two-week follow-up appointment, the patient reported no significant issues or discomfort. His surgical wound had healed well without any signs of infection. The patient's blood pressure remained well controlled, and his kidney function was stable. He was advised to continue his regular medications and to follow up as needed.

In conclusion, the patient had an uncomplicated surgical course for cholecystectomy. His postoperative recovery was smooth, with no immediate or delayed complications observed. The patient's kidney function remained stable throughout the perioperative period. He was able to resume his regular activities within a reasonable timeframe without any limitations.
