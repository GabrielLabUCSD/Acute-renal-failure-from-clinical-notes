The patient is a 65-year-old male with a past medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. He is currently on medications including lisinopril, atorvastatin, and metformin. The patient is planning to undergo elective surgery for the removal of a benign skin lesion. 

In terms of surgical history, the patient had a cholecystectomy 10 years ago due to symptomatic gallstones. He tolerated the procedure well and did not experience any postoperative complications. He has no known drug allergies.

The patient's preoperative evaluation revealed well-controlled blood pressure and blood glucose levels. His last HbA1c was 6.8%, indicating good glycemic control. His laboratory results also showed normal renal function with a serum creatinine level of 0.9 mg/dL.

During the preoperative assessment, the patient reported no difficulty breathing or any other respiratory symptoms. He denied any cardiac symptoms, such as chest pain or shortness of breath. His physical examination was unremarkable, with normal heart and lung sounds.

The patient was instructed to discontinue his metformin for 48 hours prior to surgery, as per the surgeon's recommendation. He was advised to take his other medications with a sip of water on the morning of the surgery.

Postoperatively, the patient tolerated the procedure well and was able to ambulate on the same day. He experienced mild pain at the surgical site, which was managed with analgesic medications. His vital signs remained stable, and he had no signs of infection or wound complications.

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and he was discharged home the following day. He was provided with detailed instructions regarding wound care, activity limitations, and the resumption of his regular diabetic medications.

No complications, such as wound infection, hematoma, or seroma formation, were reported during the patient's follow-up visits. The surgical site healed well, without any signs of dehiscence or delayed wound healing.

Overall, the patient's surgical experience was successful, without any significant complications. He maintained appropriate blood pressure and blood glucose control throughout the perioperative period. The patient was advised to continue his regular follow-up care with his primary care physician and surgical team to monitor his ongoing recovery.
