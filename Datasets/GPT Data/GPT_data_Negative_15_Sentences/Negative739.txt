The patient is a 60-year-old male who has a medical history significant for hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hyperlipidemia. He presents to the hospital for surgery. He has a history of well-controlled hypertension on lisinopril 10 mg daily and hydrochlorothiazide 25 mg daily. His diabetes is managed with glipizide 5 mg twice daily and metformin 1000 mg twice daily. His hyperlipidemia is treated with atorvastatin 40 mg daily.

The patient's preoperative laboratory results show a fasting blood glucose level of 130 mg/dL, HbA1c of 7.5%, total cholesterol of 200 mg/dL, LDL cholesterol of 120 mg/dL, and HDL cholesterol of 40 mg/dL. His renal function is within normal limits with a serum creatinine of 0.9 mg/dL.

The patient has previously undergone a cholecystectomy for symptomatic cholelithiasis and has had no complications during or after the surgery. He has a history of uneventful colonoscopies and is up to date with his screenings. He denies any allergies to medications or latex.

During the preoperative evaluation, the patient's vital signs are stable, and he is generally in good health. He does not report any recent illnesses or infections. He does not smoke and denies any alcohol or illicit drug use.

The patient's current medications are continued into the perioperative period, with the exception of metformin, which is held on the day of surgery. He is given clear instructions on when to stop eating and drinking before surgery, and he complies accordingly. The surgical procedure is uneventful, and the patient is monitored closely during and after the operation. No complications are encountered during the postoperative period, and the patient is closely monitored for pain control, wound healing, and mobility.

The patient's postoperative course is generally uncomplicated. He is started on a clear liquid diet on the day of surgery and is advanced to a regular diet as tolerated over the next few days. His vital signs remain stable, and no signs of infection or wound complications are noted. The patient is discharged home on postoperative day 3 with appropriate pain medications and postoperative care instructions.

In summary, the 60-year-old male patient with a history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and hyperlipidemia undergoes surgery without complications. His preoperative laboratory results are within normal limits, and his renal function is normal. The patient has a history of a previous cholecystectomy with no complications. He is discharged home on postoperative day 3 with an uneventful recovery and appropriate postoperative care.
