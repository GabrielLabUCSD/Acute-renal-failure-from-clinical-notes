Preoperative laboratory investigations show that the patient has a normal complete blood count, electrolyte levels, liver function tests, and coagulation profile. His fasting blood glucose levels are within the target range, and his glycosylated hemoglobin (HbA1c) level is well controlled.

During the postoperative period, the patient has an uneventful recovery. He is closely monitored for vital signs and pain management. However, long-term follow-up will be essential to monitor for any potential complications that may arise in the future.
