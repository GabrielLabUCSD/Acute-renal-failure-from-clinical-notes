The patient was diagnosed with osteoarthritis of the right hip, which has significantly impacted his mobility and quality of life. After conservative management failed to alleviate his symptoms, the decision was made to proceed with total hip replacement surgery. The patient underwent preoperative investigations, including blood tests, an electrocardiogram, and a chest x-ray, which did not reveal any significant abnormalities.

The patient was admitted for surgery and received prophylactic antibiotics prior to the procedure. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient tolerated it well. The orthopedic surgeon successfully replaced the patient's right hip joint with a prosthesis without any complications.

Postoperatively, the patient was closely monitored in the recovery room and then transferred to the orthopedic ward. Pain control was managed with regular administration of acetaminophen and non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs. The patient also received physical therapy to aid in his rehabilitation. His vital signs remained stable throughout his hospital stay, with no signs of infection or wound complications.

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and he showed good progress in his mobility. He was able to walk with the support of a walker on the second postoperative day and gradually transitioned to using a cane. Physical therapy sessions were continued on an outpatient basis following his discharge. The patient was educated about wound care and signs of infection to watch out for at home.

He mentioned that he had experienced some occasional mild discomfort but was overall satisfied with the outcome of the surgery. The patient was advised to continue with his regular medications as well as implement lifestyle modifications to maintain his overall health.

In summary, this patient with a history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and coronary artery disease underwent successful total hip replacement surgery for osteoarthritis.
