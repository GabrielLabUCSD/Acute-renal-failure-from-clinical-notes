The patient is a 55-year-old woman who is planning to undergo surgery for the removal of a benign breast lump. She has a medical history of hypertension, controlled with medication, and hyperlipidemia. The patient does not smoke but consumes alcohol occasionally.

Her surgical history includes a laparoscopic appendectomy 10 years ago and a tonsillectomy during childhood. The patient is not allergic to any medications or known substances. She takes a daily multivitamin and over-the-counter ibuprofen occasionally for pain relief.

In terms of chronic medications, the patient takes lisinopril 10 mg daily for hypertension and simvastatin 20 mg daily for hyperlipidemia. She has been compliant with her medications and has not experienced any adverse reactions.

Preoperatively, the patient had routine blood work, which revealed normal complete blood count, electrolytes, and renal function tests. Her preoperative electrocardiogram and chest X-ray were unremarkable.

The patient was provided with preoperative instructions including fasting from midnight before the surgery, and she complied accordingly. On the day of surgery, she received appropriate anesthesia and underwent an uneventful procedure lasting approximately 1 hour.

Following surgery, the patient was transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) for monitoring and recovery. She received intravenous fluids for hydration and pain medication for postoperative discomfort. The patient's vital signs and incision site were monitored regularly, and she tolerated the procedure well.

During her postoperative course, the patient experienced minimal pain, which was effectively managed with oral analgesics. She was encouraged to ambulate and perform breathing exercises to prevent complications such as deep vein thrombosis and atelectasis.

The patient's surgical wound healed without any signs of infection or dehiscence. She was discharged home on postoperative day 2 with appropriate wound care instructions and pain medication for home use. A follow-up appointment was scheduled for the removal of sutures and further assessment of her surgical site.

There were no immediate postoperative complications, such as wound infection or bleeding. The patient maintained good hydration and was advised to continue taking her chronic medications as prescribed.

In conclusion, the patient's surgery for benign breast lump removal went smoothly, and her postoperative course was uneventful without any complications. She followed instructions and was cooperative throughout the process. All necessary postoperative measures were taken to ensure her safety and overall well-being.
