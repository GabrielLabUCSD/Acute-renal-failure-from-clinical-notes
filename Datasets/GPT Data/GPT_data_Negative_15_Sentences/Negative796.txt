The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of hypertension, type 2 diabetes mellitus, and obesity. He has been on antihypertensive medications for the past 10 years and takes oral hypoglycemic agents to control his blood sugar levels. His body mass index is 35, indicating obesity.

The patient has a past surgical history significant for cholecystectomy and hernia repair 5 years ago. He has a history of smoking for 20 years but quit 2 years ago. He reports occasional alcohol use but denies any illicit drug use. His family history is significant for cardiovascular disease, with his father having a myocardial infarction at age 60.

The patient is scheduled to undergo a laparoscopic sleeve gastrectomy for weight loss. Preoperatively, he has been counseled on the risks and benefits of the surgery. He has undergone preoperative evaluations including blood work, chest X-ray, and electrocardiogram, all of which were within normal limits.

The surgery was performed without any intraoperative complications. The patient tolerated the procedure well and was transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit for recovery. Postoperatively, he was started on a clear liquid diet and advanced as tolerated. He was ambulating on the day of surgery and had adequate pain control with oral medications.

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, with no signs of infection or wound complications noted. His blood sugar levels were closely monitored and remained stable throughout his hospital stay. He was discharged home on postoperative day 3 with instructions for diet, activity, and follow-up appointments.

At his follow-up visit one week later, the patient reported feeling well and had lost 5 pounds since surgery. His surgical incisions were healing well, with no signs of infection. His blood pressure and blood sugar levels were well-controlled, and he was advised to continue his regular medications.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgery with no major complications. He was pleased with the outcome and motivated to continue his lifestyle changes to achieve his weight loss goals. The multidisciplinary team will continue to follow up with him to ensure optimal recovery and long-term success.
