The patient is a 56-year-old female with a medical history significant for hypertension, diabetes, and obesity, who is scheduled to undergo elective bariatric surgery to address her obesity. She has a history of well-controlled hypertension on a combination of Lisinopril and Amlodipine and her diabetes is managed with Metformin and Glipizide. The patient has been compliant with her medications and has regular follow-ups with her primary care physician for monitoring of her conditions.

She has a history of previous surgeries including a cholecystectomy and a total abdominal hysterectomy without any complications. The patient is a non-smoker and does not consume alcohol. She does not have any known drug allergies and her family history is negative for any significant medical conditions. The patient has undergone all preoperative testing including labs, EKG, and chest X-ray which all came back within normal limits.

During the surgery, the patient tolerated the procedure well without any intraoperative complications. Postoperatively, she was started on a clear liquid diet and advanced to a regular diet as tolerated. The patient was encouraged to ambulate early to prevent complications such as deep vein thrombosis and pneumonia. She was monitored closely for signs of infection at the surgical site and was given prophylactic antibiotics as per protocol.

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and she was discharged home on postoperative day three with instructions for wound care and a follow-up appointment in two weeks. She was advised to continue her medications as prescribed and to monitor her blood sugar levels closely. The patient was also referred to a nutritionist for dietary counseling to aid in her weight loss journey.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgery and an uncomplicated recovery. She was pleased with the care she received during her hospital stay and reported feeling motivated to continue making positive lifestyle changes to improve her health.
