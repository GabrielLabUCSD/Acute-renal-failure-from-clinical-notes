The patient is a 65-year-old male with a significant medical history of hypertension, type 2 diabetes, and coronary artery disease. He also has a history of smoking for 30 years but quit 10 years ago. His current medications include metformin, lisinopril, atorvastatin, and aspirin.

The patient has been experiencing worsening hip pain due to osteoarthritis, for which he is scheduled to undergo a total hip replacement surgery. He has met with the orthopedic surgeon and completed all preoperative workup including blood tests, imaging studies, and cardiac clearance.

During the surgery, the patient tolerated general anesthesia well and the procedure was successful without any complications. Postoperatively, he received adequate pain management with a combination of medications including opioids and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs.

The patient was mobilized with the help of physical therapy on the first day after surgery and was able to bear weight on the operated hip. He followed the prescribed postoperative care plan including deep breathing exercises, leg exercises, and wound care.

On the third day after surgery, the patient developed a low-grade fever which was managed with acetaminophen. Wound inspection revealed no signs of infection or dehiscence, and the fever resolved within 24 hours.

The patient's postoperative course was otherwise uneventful, with good pain control and gradual improvement in mobility and function. He was discharged home on postoperative day 5 with instructions for outpatient physical therapy and a follow-up appointment with the surgeon in two weeks.

The patient was advised on signs and symptoms of potential complications such as infection, blood clots, or wound issues and instructed to seek medical attention if any concerning symptoms arise. He was also counseled on the importance of adherence to medications, activity restrictions, and lifestyle modifications to promote healing and reduce the risk of complications.

Overall, the patient's recovery from the total hip replacement surgery was successful, and he reported satisfaction with the outcome and improvement in his quality of life with decreased hip pain and improved mobility.
