Mr. Smith has a past surgical history of a cholecystectomy 10 years ago without any complications. He is a non-smoker and does not drink alcohol. His family history is significant for cardiovascular disease, with his father having a heart attack at the age of 60. 

On physical examination, Mr. Smith is overweight with a BMI of 30. His vital signs are stable, and he has no signs of infection at the surgical site. Preoperative labs reveal well-controlled blood sugar levels and normal kidney function. 

The surgery is performed without complications, and Mr. Smith is monitored closely in the postoperative period. He is started on pain medications and prophylactic antibiotics. His blood pressure and blood sugar levels are closely monitored and remain stable. 

Mr. Smith is encouraged to ambulate early and perform breathing exercises to prevent postoperative complications. He is seen by physical therapy for mobility assistance during his hospital stay. A clear liquid diet is initiated and advanced as tolerated. 

In the postoperative period, Mr. Smith experiences some mild nausea and vomiting, which is managed with medication. He also develops a low-grade fever, likely due to the body's response to surgery. His wound site is closely monitored for signs of infection, but remains clean and intact. 

Overall, Mr. Smith's postoperative course is uneventful, and he is discharged home on postoperative day 3 with instructions for wound care and pain management. He is advised to follow up with his primary care physician for further monitoring of his blood pressure and blood sugar levels. 

At his follow-up appointment, Mr. Smith's wound has healed well, and he is gradually resuming his regular activities. His blood pressure and blood sugar levels remain stable, and there are no signs of infection or other complications related to the surgery. 

In conclusion, Mr. Smith successfully underwent surgery for a hernia repair with a smooth postoperative course. With appropriate preoperative optimization and close postoperative monitoring, he was able to recover well without any significant complications.
