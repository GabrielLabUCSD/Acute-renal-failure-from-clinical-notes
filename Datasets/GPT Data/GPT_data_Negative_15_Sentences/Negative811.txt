Mr. Roberts is a 55-year-old male with a history of hypertension for 10 years.
He was diagnosed with type 2 diabetes 5 years ago and has been on metformin since.
He had a heart attack 2 years ago and underwent cardiac stent placement.
Mr. Roberts also has a history of osteoarthritis in his hips, which has been causing him significant pain and limited mobility.
He is scheduled to undergo a total hip replacement surgery due to severe degeneration of the joint.
Prior to surgery, he underwent a thorough preoperative evaluation which included cardiac clearance.
Mr. Roberts was advised to discontinue his metformin a week before surgery to reduce the risk of complications.
The surgery was performed successfully, and Mr. Roberts was closely monitored in the immediate postoperative period for pain control and infection.
He was started on physical therapy the day after surgery to aid in his recovery and rehabilitation.
Mr. Roberts experienced some postoperative complications in the form of a wound infection which was promptly treated with antibiotics.
Despite the setback, his medical team closely monitored his recovery and ensured proper wound care.
He was discharged home with instructions for wound care, pain management, and a plan for outpatient physical therapy.
Follow-up appointments were scheduled to monitor his progress and address any concerns.
Overall, Mr. Roberts is expected to have a favorable outcome from the surgery once the infection is fully resolved and he completes his rehabilitation.
