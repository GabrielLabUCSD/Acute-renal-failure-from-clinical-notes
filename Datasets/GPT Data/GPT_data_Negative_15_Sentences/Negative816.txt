Patient is a 60-year-old male with a history of well-controlled hypertension and type 2 diabetes, both managed with medication. He has a past surgical history of a cholecystectomy 10 years ago without complications. He also has a family history of cardiovascular disease, with his father having had a myocardial infarction in his 50s.

The patient smokes occasionally and drinks alcohol socially. He leads a sedentary lifestyle but has recently been trying to incorporate more physical activity into his routine. He denies any history of allergies to medications or anesthesia.

The patient is scheduled for an elective knee replacement surgery due to severe osteoarthritis. Pre-operative evaluation revealed no significant abnormalities in his lab work, ECG, or chest X-ray. He was advised to continue his antihypertensive and antidiabetic medications up to the day of surgery.

The surgery went smoothly without any intraoperative complications. The patient was monitored closely for postoperative pain, infection, and mobility. He was started on a prophylactic course of antibiotics to prevent surgical site infections.

In the postoperative period, the patient experienced some expected pain and swelling, which were managed with pain medications and physical therapy. He was encouraged to mobilize as soon as possible to prevent complications such as deep vein thrombosis.

The patient's recovery progressed well, and he was discharged home after three days in the hospital. He was provided with instructions for wound care, medication management, and follow-up appointments. He was advised to continue with physical therapy and gradually increase his activity level.

During his follow-up visits, the patient showed good progress in his range of motion and strength. He reported minimal pain and was able to perform daily activities with ease. His surgical site healed well without any signs of infection.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgical outcome with no major complications. He was satisfied with the results of the surgery and expressed gratitude for the care received from the healthcare team. The patient was counseled on the importance of maintaining a healthy lifestyle, including regular exercise and a balanced diet, to support his recovery and prevent future health issues.
