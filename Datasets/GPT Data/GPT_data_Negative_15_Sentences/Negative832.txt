The patient is a 45-year-old female with a history of hypertension, diabetes, and obesity, who is scheduled to undergo surgery for a hysterectomy due to benign uterine fibroids. She has a past surgical history of a cesarean section 10 years ago. Her current medications include metformin for diabetes, lisinopril for hypertension, and a daily multivitamin.

The patient's preoperative workup showed well-controlled blood pressure and blood sugar levels. She has no known drug allergies. The patient reported being a non-smoker and does not consume alcohol. Her physical examination was unremarkable, aside from an elevated BMI of 32.

Following the surgery, the patient had an uneventful postoperative course with adequate pain control and early ambulation. She was started on a clear liquid diet and gradually advanced to a regular diet as tolerated. The patient's blood pressure and blood sugar levels remained stable during the postoperative period.

She was discharged home on postoperative day 3 with instructions for wound care and a plan for follow-up with her primary care physician in one week. The patient was advised to resume her regular medications postoperatively. There were no signs of surgical site infection or other immediate postoperative complications.

At the follow-up appointment, the patient reported a satisfactory recovery with minimal discomfort at the surgical site. Her vital signs were within normal limits, and her blood sugar levels were well-controlled. The patient was encouraged to continue her healthy lifestyle modifications to optimize her overall health and reduce the risk of future complications related to her medical conditions.
