The patient is a 55-year-old male with a history of hypertension, type 2 diabetes, and coronary artery disease. He had a myocardial infarction two years ago and underwent a successful coronary artery stenting procedure.

He has a past surgical history of cholecystectomy and appendectomy. His current medications include metformin, lisinopril, atorvastatin, and aspirin. The patient is a non-smoker and does not consume alcohol.

He presents with worsening angina symptoms and is scheduled for elective coronary artery bypass grafting surgery. Preoperative testing shows well-controlled blood pressure and blood glucose levels.

The patient undergoes surgery without any intraoperative complications. Postoperatively, he is monitored closely in the intensive care unit for the first 24 hours. He is successfully weaned off mechanical ventilation and extubated on postoperative day 1.

His recovery progresses well, with satisfactory pain control and early mobilization. The patient is started on cardiac rehabilitation exercises and closely monitored for signs of infection or bleeding.

On postoperative day 3, he develops a low-grade fever which resolves with antibiotics. His chest wound is clean, dry, and intact without any signs of infection.

The patient is discharged home on postoperative day 7 with a plan for outpatient cardiac rehabilitation and close follow-up with his cardiologist. He is counselled on medication compliance, dietary modifications, and lifestyle changes to optimize his cardiac health.

During his follow-up appointments, the patient reports feeling better and stronger with improved exercise tolerance. He is adherent to his medications and has adopted a heart-healthy diet.

Overall, the patient's postoperative course was uncomplicated, and he did not experience any major surgical complications. He continues to make progress in his recovery and remains motivated to maintain a healthy lifestyle to optimize his cardiac health.
