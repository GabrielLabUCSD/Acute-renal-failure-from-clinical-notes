The patient is a 55-year-old male with a history of hypertension, diabetes, and obesity. He also has a previous heart attack 3 years ago for which he underwent a stent placement.

His medications include metformin, lisinopril, and atorvastatin. He has a history of smoking but quit 10 years ago. The patient has had knee pain for the past year due to osteoarthritis and has been recommended for a total knee replacement surgery.

The patient underwent preoperative clearance with cardiology and endocrinology, and both specialists cleared him for surgery. His preoperative labs were within normal limits except for mild anemia.

The patient underwent total knee replacement surgery without any intraoperative complications. Postoperatively, he received adequate pain control with a combination of acetaminophen and opioids as needed. Physical therapy was initiated on postoperative day 1 to help with early mobilization.

The patient experienced some mild swelling and bruising around the surgical site, which improved with elevation and icing. He developed a superficial wound infection which was treated with antibiotics and resolved without any further complications.

Despite his medical history, the patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and he was able to successfully complete his physical therapy sessions and was discharged home on postoperative day 4. He was advised to continue with his medications and follow-up with his primary care physician and orthopedic surgeon for further monitoring.

Overall, the patient's surgery was successful and he was satisfied with the outcome. He reported improvement in his knee pain and function following the surgery. The patient was counseled on the importance of adhering to a healthy lifestyle to optimize his recovery and prevent future complications.
