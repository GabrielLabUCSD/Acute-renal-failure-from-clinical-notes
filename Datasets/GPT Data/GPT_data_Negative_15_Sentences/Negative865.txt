The patient reports a family history of heart disease, with both parents having a history of coronary artery disease.

He is scheduled to undergo elective surgery for a total knee replacement due to severe osteoarthritis that has been affecting his mobility and quality of life. The patient has been experiencing significant knee pain and stiffness for the last 5 years, despite conservative management with physical therapy and medications.

During the preoperative assessment, the patient's blood pressure was well controlled with a reading of 130/80 mmHg and his blood glucose levels were within normal limits. He had a routine electrocardiogram which showed normal sinus rhythm.

The surgical procedure was performed without any complications, and the patient was closely monitored in the post-anesthesia care unit. He received appropriate pain management and physical therapy to aid in his recovery. The patient was started on prophylactic antibiotics to prevent surgical site infections.

In the immediate postoperative period, the patient was able to bear weight on his new knee with the help of a physical therapist. He was closely monitored for signs of infection, deep vein thrombosis, and other postoperative complications. The patient's blood pressure and blood glucose levels remained stable throughout his hospital stay.

The patient did not develop any complications during his hospital stay and was discharged home with a plan for outpatient physical therapy and follow-up with his primary care physician. He was instructed on wound care, signs of infection, and the importance of adherence to his medication regimen.

At his follow-up appointment 2 weeks post-surgery, the patient reported a significant improvement in his knee pain and mobility. His surgical incision was healing well without any signs of infection. The patient was advised to continue with physical therapy and to gradually increase his activity levels under the supervision of his healthcare team.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgical procedure and a smooth postoperative course. He was able to return to his activities of daily living with improved mobility and quality of life.
