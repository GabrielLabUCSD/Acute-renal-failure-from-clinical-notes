Mr. Smith is a 65-year-old male with a medical history of hypertension, type 2 diabetes, and obesity. He has a history of smoking for 30 years but quit 10 years ago. He also has a family history of cardiovascular disease. His medications include metformin for diabetes and lisinopril for hypertension.

In preparation for surgery, Mr. Smith underwent preoperative evaluation where his blood pressure, blood sugar levels, and kidney function were assessed. His surgery was scheduled for a total knee replacement due to severe osteoarthritis that was affecting his mobility and quality of life.

During the surgery, Mr. Smith was closely monitored by the anesthesia team, and the procedure went smoothly without any complications. Postoperatively, he was started on pain medications and physical therapy to aid in his recovery and rehabilitation.

For postoperative care, Mr. Smith was closely monitored for signs of infection, blood clots, and wound healing. He was encouraged to move around to prevent complications such as pneumonia and deep vein thrombosis.

Fortunately, Mr. Smith had a smooth postoperative course with no complications. He was able to begin walking with the help of a physical therapist and gradually regained strength in his knee. He also had adequate pain control and was able to resume his daily activities with ease.

Mr. Smith was discharged home with instructions for wound care, pain management, and a follow-up appointment with his surgeon. He was advised to continue his medications for diabetes and hypertension as prescribed and to maintain a healthy lifestyle to prevent future health issues.

Overall, Mr. Smith's surgery was successful, and he experienced a positive outcome with no complications. His commitment to his recovery and adherence to medical advice played a significant role in his smooth postoperative course.
