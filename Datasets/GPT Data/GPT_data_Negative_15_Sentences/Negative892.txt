The patient is a 55-year-old male with a history of hypertension, diabetes, and obesity. He has been on medications for his conditions for the past 10 years. He has a history of smoking for 20 years but quit 5 years ago. The patient had a previous surgery for appendicitis without any complications. 

His preoperative evaluation revealed well-controlled hypertension and diabetes. His physical examination was unremarkable, except for a body mass index of 32. The patient's renal function tests showed a baseline creatinine of 1.0 mg/dL. He had a chest x-ray and electrocardiogram, which were normal. The patient had a consultation with the anesthesiologist and was deemed fit for surgery.

Postoperatively, the patient was monitored closely in the recovery room for any signs of complications. He was started on appropriate pain management and fluid resuscitation. The patient was encouraged to ambulate early to prevent deep vein thrombosis. He had regular assessments of his vital signs and urine output. The patient was started on a clear liquid diet and was gradually advanced to solid foods as tolerated.

The patient did not have any immediate postoperative complications such as wound infection, deep vein thrombosis, or respiratory issues. His renal function was closely monitored, and his creatinine remained stable at 1.0 mg/dL. The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 3 with instructions for wound care and follow-up. He was advised to continue his medications for hypertension and diabetes as prescribed.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgery with no major complications. He was satisfied with the care provided by the healthcare team and was recovering well at home.
