The patient is a 50-year-old male who is planning to have surgery for an inguinal hernia repair. He has a past medical history significant for hypertension and hyperlipidemia, both of which are adequately controlled with medications. He has no known drug allergies. The patient does not smoke but occasionally drinks alcohol on social occasions.

In terms of surgical history, the patient underwent an appendectomy 10 years ago without any complications. He also had a cholecystectomy five years ago for symptomatic gallstones.

The patient currently denies any symptoms such as chest pain, shortness of breath, or palpitations. His physical examination reveals normal vital signs and there are no abnormalities noted on auscultation of the heart or lungs.

Lab work shows all values within normal limits, including a complete blood count, comprehensive metabolic panel, and coagulation profile. His renal function is normal, with a serum creatinine level of 0.9 mg/dL. Urinalysis does not reveal any significant abnormalities.

The patient has been informed about the surgery and its risks, benefits, and alternatives. He has provided informed consent and is prepared for the procedure. An anesthesiologist has evaluated him preoperatively and deemed him fit for surgery.

The surgery is performed without any complications. The patient is closely monitored during the procedure, and vital signs remain stable throughout. The surgical team successfully repairs the hernia, and no issues arise during the surgery.

Postoperatively, the patient is taken to the recovery room and closely observed. He awakens without any issues and reports minimal pain, which is well-controlled with oral analgesics. Intravenous fluids are administered to maintain hydration.

The patient is monitored for any signs of complications, such as infection, bleeding, or wound dehiscence. However, there are no abnormal findings during the postoperative period. He is able to tolerate a regular diet by the next morning and has regular bowel movements.

Following a satisfactory postoperative recovery, the patient is discharged with instructions for activity restrictions, wound care, and medications. He is scheduled for a follow-up appointment in two weeks to assess the wound healing and ensure appropriate recovery.

Overall, the patient has a smooth postoperative course without any complications, including acute renal failure. The surgical team notes that the patient's controlled hypertension and hyperlipidemia did not pose any increased risk during the procedure, and the patient is pleased with the outcome of the surgery.
