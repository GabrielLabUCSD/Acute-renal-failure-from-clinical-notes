The patient is a 65-year-old male who is planning to have surgery.
He has a past medical history significant for hypertension, type 2 diabetes mellitus, and coronary artery disease.
His hypertension has been well-controlled on metoprolol 50 mg daily.
He has been managing his diabetes with diet and exercise but occasionally requires metformin 1000 mg twice daily.
His coronary artery disease is managed with aspirin 81 mg daily and atorvastatin 40 mg daily.
He had a myocardial infarction 5 years ago, for which he underwent stent placement in the left anterior descending artery.
He had an echocardiogram 2 years ago that revealed preserved systolic function.
The patient is a non-smoker and does not consume alcohol.
His surgical procedure is for an elective hernia repair.
Preoperatively, his blood pressure is within normal limits, and his diabetes is well-controlled.
He is instructed to withhold his metformin for 24 hours before the surgery.
The surgical procedure goes smoothly without any immediate complications.
Postoperatively, the patient complains of mild pain and is managed with oral analgesics.
He is closely monitored for any signs of infection.
His blood pressure remains stable throughout the postoperative period.
He is able to tolerate solid food and is discharged home on postoperative day 2 without any complications.
