The surgery was performed under general anesthesia. The patient was intubated and monitored throughout the procedure. The surgical team successfully completed the procedure without any intraoperative complications. The patient was closely observed during the immediate postoperative period in the recovery room.

In the postoperative course, the patient was initially monitored in the intensive care unit (ICU) for the first 24 hours following surgery. His vital signs remained stable, and pain control was achieved with intravenous analgesics. The patient was able to tolerate a clear liquid diet and was encouraged to ambulate under supervision.

In terms of complications, the patient did not experience any immediate postoperative complications such as infection, bleeding, or wound dehiscence. He had an unremarkable recovery and was transferred to the general surgical ward on the second postoperative day.

In the subsequent days, the patient continued to progress well. He was gradually transitioned to oral pain medications and his diet was advanced to a regular solid diet. He had no issues with bowel movement and his incision site was clean and healing well.

The patient was provided with postoperative instructions regarding wound care, medication management, and follow-up appointments. He was advised to gradually increase his activity levels and to avoid heavy lifting or strenuous exercises for a few weeks.

During the follow-up visit, the patient reported no significant issues or complications. His wound had completely healed without any signs of infection or inflammation. The surgical incision site was clean and intact. The patient was discharged from further surgical follow-up with instructions to continue his regular medications and to monitor his blood pressure and blood sugar levels closely.
