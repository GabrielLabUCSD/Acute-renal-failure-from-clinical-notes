He denies any respiratory symptoms or cough.

The patient's surgical history includes an appendectomy ten years ago and an inguinal hernia repair five years ago. Both surgeries were uncomplicated. He has no known drug allergies.

In terms of family history, the patient's father had a history of coronary artery disease and underwent a coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery at the age of 60. His mother has a history of breast cancer but is currently in remission. There is no significant family history of anesthesia-related complications or blood clotting disorders.

The patient's preoperative workup revealed a normal complete blood count (CBC), basic metabolic panel (BMP), and coagulation profile. His electrocardiogram (EKG) showed occasional premature ventricular contractions (PVCs) but was otherwise normal. A chest X-ray did not show any acute abnormalities.

He had normal cardiovascular and respiratory examinations, with blood pressure measuring at 132/80 mmHg and heart rate at 78 beats per minute. Lung auscultation was clear, and he had no signs of peripheral edema. His abdomen was soft and non-tender, with no evidence of hernias or masses.

The patient was counseled on the importance of not smoking in the perioperative period and was referred to a smoking cessation program for further support. He was also advised to continue taking his antihypertensive and lipid-lowering medications until the day of surgery, with the exception of the morning of the procedure.

The surgery performed was a laparoscopic cholecystectomy due to symptomatic gallstones. The procedure was successful, with no intraoperative complications noted. The patient tolerated the anesthesia well and did not require any intraoperative blood transfusions. He was extubated at the end of the procedure and transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) for recovery.

In the PACU, the patient's vital signs remained stable, and he was able to tolerate clear liquids without any signs of nausea or vomiting. His pain was well controlled with a combination of opioids and non-opioid analgesics. The surgical site was inspected and found to be clean and dry, with minimal drainage.

The patient was discharged home on the same day after meeting the criteria for discharge, which included stable vital signs, ability to tolerate oral intake, adequate pain control, and ambulation without difficulty. He was given instructions on wound care and pain management and was provided with a prescription for postoperative pain medications.

During the postoperative period, the patient did not experience any complications such as wound infection, bleeding, or bile leak. He followed up with the surgeon two weeks after the surgery, and his incision site was healing well. The patient reported minimal pain and resumed his normal activities without any limitations.

In summary, the patient is a 45-year-old male with a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity, who presented with symptomatic gallstones requiring laparoscopic cholecystectomy. The surgery was successful, and the patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He did not experience any complications and had a satisfactory recovery.
