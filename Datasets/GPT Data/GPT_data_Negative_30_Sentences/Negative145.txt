The patient is a 62-year-old male who presented with severe hip pain due to osteoarthritis. He has a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. He has been on medications for these conditions, including lisinopril, atorvastatin, and metformin. He has no known drug allergies.

The patient underwent a total hip replacement surgery under general anesthesia. Preoperative labs were within normal limits, including a complete blood count, basic metabolic panel, and coagulation profile. His preoperative electrocardiogram showed no significant abnormalities.

During the surgery, the patient tolerated the procedure well. There were no intraoperative complications, and the total operating time was approximately 2 hours. The surgeon used a cementless implant, and the wound was closed with absorbable sutures.

Following the surgery, the patient was admitted to the orthopedic ward. Pain control was achieved with intravenous morphine immediately postoperatively, and then transitioned to oral oxycodone as needed. The patient received deep vein thrombosis prophylaxis with subcutaneous heparin for 2 weeks.

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful. He began physical therapy on the first day after surgery and was able to ambulate with the assistance of a walker. Daily wound assessments were performed, and there were no signs of infection. His vital signs remained stable throughout the hospital stay.

On postoperative day 2, the patient's labs were repeated, and they showed no significant abnormalities. His pain was well controlled, and he reported being satisfied with his surgical outcome. The patient was discharged on postoperative day 3 with instructions to continue his pain medication as needed and to follow up with his primary care physician in 2 weeks.

At the follow-up appointment, the patient reported continued improvement in hip pain and mobility. The surgical wound was clean and healing well. His oral pain medication was gradually tapered off. The patient was referred for outpatient physical therapy to continue his rehabilitation and regain strength.

The patient did not experience any postoperative complications such as wound infection, deep vein thrombosis, or pulmonary embolism. His blood glucose levels remained stable throughout the hospital stay. There were no signs of surgical site complications like dehiscence or hematoma formation.

Due to the patient's diligent adherence to postoperative care, he had a successful recovery. He regained functional mobility and was able to resume his normal activities. His pain was well controlled, and there were no long-term complications related to the surgery. The patient expressed satisfaction with the surgical outcome and reported significant improvement in his quality of life after the hip replacement surgery.
