His last lipid panel showed LDL cholesterol levels below 100 mg/dL.

He has been managing the condition with physical therapy and over-the-counter pain medications.
The patient does not have any known drug allergies.
His family history is significant for coronary artery disease, as his father had a heart attack in his mid-60s.
The patient is a non-smoker and does not consume alcohol excessively.
He exercises regularly by walking for 30 minutes every day.

The surgical procedure was completed successfully without any intraoperative complications.
Postoperatively, the patient was closely monitored in the recovery room for vital signs and pain control.
He was started on prophylactic antibiotics to prevent surgical site infections.
The patient was mobilized the same day of surgery with the assistance of physical therapy.

His vital signs remained stable and his renal function was within normal limits.
The patient did not experience any adverse events during his hospital stay.
He was discharged on postoperative day 4, following an uneventful recovery.
