The patient is a 50-year-old male with a previous medical history significant for hypertension and type 2 diabetes mellitus.
He has been controlled on metoprolol and metformin respectively.
He presents to the hospital with symptoms of recurrent abdominal pain and has been diagnosed with gallstones on ultrasound.
The patient has decided to undergo cholecystectomy due to the frequency and intensity of his symptoms.
In preparation for surgery, the patient underwent preoperative testing including a complete blood count, basic metabolic panel, and coagulation studies.
All laboratory results were within normal limits.
He was also evaluated by the anesthesiologist prior to surgery and deemed to be a suitable candidate for general anesthesia.
The surgery was performed laparoscopically without any intraoperative complications.
The patient tolerated the procedure well and was transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) for recovery.
In the PACU, the patient received appropriate pain management and monitoring.
He was started on clear liquids and advanced to a regular diet as tolerated.
The patient's vital signs remained stable throughout his hospital stay.
His postoperative course was unremarkable, with no signs of infection or wound dehiscence.
He was able to ambulate without difficulty and his pain was well-controlled with oral analgesics.
The patient was discharged on postoperative day 2 with instructions for wound care and a follow-up appointment with the surgeon in two weeks.
At the follow-up appointment, the patient reported feeling well and was satisfied with the outcome of the surgery.
He mentioned some mild residual abdominal discomfort, which was expected at this stage of recovery.
The surgeon assessed the surgical site, which appeared to be healing without any signs of infection or complications.
The patient's vital signs were within normal limits, and there were no abnormalities on physical examination.
He was advised to continue his regular medications and to gradually increase his activity level.
The patient's postoperative course was overall uneventful, and he did not experience any major complications.
He maintained regular follow-up visits with the surgeon over the next few months to ensure complete recovery and resolution of his symptoms.
At the last follow-up appointment six months after surgery, the patient reported no further abdominal pain or discomfort.
He had returned to his normal activities and had resumed a regular diet without any restrictions.
Overall, the patient's experience with surgery was successful, and he achieved the desired outcome of relief from gallstone-related symptoms.
