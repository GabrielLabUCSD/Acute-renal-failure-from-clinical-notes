His blood pressure is well controlled on a combination of lisinopril and hydrochlorothiazide, with readings averaging around 130/80 mmHg.

On physical examination, the patient is in no acute distress. His vital signs are stable, with a heart rate of 80 beats per minute and blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg. His cardiovascular exam is unremarkable. Abdominal examination reveals no tenderness, organomegaly, or masses. On auscultation, his lungs are clear to auscultation bilaterally with good air entry.

All results were within normal limits. He has been counseled on smoking cessation and instructed to abstain from smoking for at least two weeks prior to surgery to minimize complications.

The patient understands the risks and benefits of surgery and has provided informed consent. He will be placed under general anesthesia, aided by endotracheal intubation. The anesthesia team has been informed of his medical history and will closely monitor his vitals during the procedure.

In terms of the postoperative course, the patient was transferred to the intensive care unit (ICU) for close monitoring for the first 24 hours. Vital signs remained stable, and he was weaned off the respirator successfully in the morning following surgery. The patient was transferred to the surgical floor later that day.

Over the next few days, the patient's pain was well controlled with opioid pain medications, and he was encouraged to ambulate and perform breathing exercises to prevent complications such as deep vein thrombosis and pneumonia. His surgical incision appeared clean with no signs of infection.

On the third postoperative day, the patient started experiencing mild postoperative complications in the form of a low-grade fever, which resolved spontaneously within 48 hours without any intervention. The fever was attributed to the surgical stress response.

Throughout his hospital stay, the patient received prophylactic antibiotic therapy as per the surgical team's protocol to minimize the risk of surgical site infections. Additionally, his diabetes, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia were managed in conjunction with the medical team to maintain adequate control and prevent any postoperative complications related to these comorbidities.

The patient was discharged on the sixth postoperative day in stable condition with appropriate pain control. He was advised to continue his regular medications and follow up with his primary care physician in two weeks for further evaluation of his recovery process. He was also instructed to complete a course of home-based physical therapy to regain his strength and mobility.

In summary, this 45-year-old male with a complex medical history underwent surgery successfully without any major complications.
