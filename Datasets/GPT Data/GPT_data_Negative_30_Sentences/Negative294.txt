The patient is a 50-year-old female who presents with a medical history including hypertension, diabetes, and obesity. She has been experiencing increasing knee pain and mobility issues, warranting surgical intervention. Preoperative evaluations reveal well-controlled blood pressure and blood glucose levels. The patient is currently on antihypertensive and antidiabetic medications. 

Her obesity is a significant concern and may increase the risk of surgical complications, particularly related to wound healing and infection. Therefore, the patient's BMI is carefully monitored, and weight management strategies are implemented preoperatively. No other significant comorbidities are identified, and the patient does not report any known drug allergies.

The patient undergoes a comprehensive preoperative assessment, including a physical examination, routine laboratory tests, and imaging studies. Blood work results show well-controlled blood glucose levels within the target range. Imaging studies reveal severe osteoarthritis in the affected knee, confirming the need for surgery.

The surgical procedure chosen for the patient is total knee replacement. The patient is thoroughly educated about the procedure, including the benefits, risks, and complications associated with surgery. Informed consent is obtained, and the patient's questions and concerns are addressed.

In the postoperative period, the patient experiences expected pain and swelling at the surgical site. Analgesic medications are administered to manage pain adequately. Physical therapy is initiated immediately to promote early mobilization, prevent joint stiffness, and aid in the recovery process.

The patient's wound healing process is carefully monitored, and appropriate wound care techniques are provided to minimize the risk of infection. Prophylactic antibiotics are administered as per protocol to further decrease the chances of surgical site infection.

The patient's vital signs are regularly monitored, and she is closely observed for signs of complications. These include venous thromboembolism (VTE), deep vein thrombosis (DVT), pulmonary embolism (PE), wound infection, or dehiscence. Measures such as compression stockings, pneumatic compression devices, and anticoagulant medications are implemented to prevent VTE.

The patient's blood glucose levels are closely managed during the postoperative period to promote wound healing and prevent complications related to diabetes. Blood glucose monitoring, insulin therapy, and dietary modifications are utilized as necessary.

Early mobilization and physical therapy sessions are continued to optimize the patient's functional recovery. Occupational therapy is also involved to assist the patient in regaining her activities of daily living. The patient is encouraged to perform exercises and adhere to rehabilitation instructions as prescribed.

The patient's postoperative course is uneventful, with no major complications observed. She demonstrates significant improvement in knee pain, range of motion, and functional abilities. 

Regular follow-up visits are scheduled to monitor the patient's progress and address any concerns that may arise. As part of the long-term care plan, the patient is advised to continue with regular physical activity, weight management strategies, and adherence to antihypertensive and antidiabetic medications.
