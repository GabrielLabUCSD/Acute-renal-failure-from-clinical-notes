He reports no symptoms of dysphagia or other gastrointestinal issues. His surgical team plans to administer perioperative proton pump inhibitors to minimize the risk of aspiration.

In terms of anesthesia, the patient reports no previous complications or adverse reactions. He has no history of difficult intubation or problems with anesthesia emergence. The patient expresses anxiety regarding anesthesia and is reassured by the anesthesiologist, who explains the safety measures in place.

Surgery is scheduled to be performed under general anesthesia, and the patient is informed of the potential risks and benefits. He is educated on postsurgical pain management and the importance of early ambulation to prevent complications such as deep vein thrombosis.

He is extubated successfully and transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) in stable condition. The patient's postoperative course is uneventful, and he is able to tolerate a regular diet without any issues. He is discharged home on the second postoperative day with appropriate wound care instructions and pain medications. The patient is advised to follow up with his primary care physician and surgeon in one week.

No complications, such as surgical site infection, wound dehiscence, or respiratory distress, are reported during the patient's postoperative course. The patient continues to manage his chronic medical conditions, and there are no immediate concerns.
