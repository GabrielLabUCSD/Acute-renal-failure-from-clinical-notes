The patient is a 45-year-old male with a past medical history significant for hypertension, diabetes mellitus type 2, and coronary artery disease.
He has been on medications including metoprolol, lisinopril, metformin, and atorvastatin.
The patient has a family history of heart disease, with his father having had a myocardial infarction at the age of 55.
He presents with symptoms of exertional chest pain and shortness of breath, prompting a cardiac catheterization which revealed severe coronary artery disease involving multiple vessels.
Based on the results, the patient is scheduled for coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) surgery.
In preparation for surgery, the patient underwent a preoperative evaluation including blood work, an EKG, and a chest X-ray.
His blood work showed normal renal function and electrolyte levels.
The patient was counseled on the risks and benefits of the surgery and provided informed consent.
On the day of surgery, the patient was admitted to the hospital and underwent CABG with a total of four grafts.
