The patient is a 50-year-old male who has a medical history of hypertension and diabetes, both have been well-controlled with medications. He is now planning to have elective surgery for a hernia repair. The patient has been on antihypertensive medications for the past 10 years and his blood pressure has been consistently within normal range. His diabetes is also well-managed with oral medications and diet modification.

In terms of his surgical history, the patient has never had any previous surgeries. He has never had any complications or adverse reactions to anesthesia in the past. The patient is a non-smoker and does not drink alcohol. He has been following a healthy lifestyle and maintaining a stable weight.

The patient's preoperative laboratory investigations revealed fasting blood glucose levels within the normal range and his hemoglobin A1c was at an acceptable level, indicating good glycemic control. His renal function tests, including serum creatinine and estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR), were within normal limits.

During the surgical procedure, the patient received general anesthesia without any issues. The surgery was uneventful, lasting approximately 2 hours, and the hernia was successfully repaired. The patient's vital signs remained stable throughout the procedure, with minimal intraoperative bleeding.

In the immediate postoperative period, the patient was transferred to the recovery room where he received appropriate pain management and close monitoring. He was able to tolerate a regular diet shortly after the surgery and started ambulating within a few hours. The patient was discharged home the following day with postoperative instructions and medications.

During the postoperative course, the patient did not experience any complications such as wound infection, bleeding, or hernia recurrence. He continued to follow-up with his surgeon for routine wound care and was advised to gradually resume his regular activities over the next few weeks. The patient's pain was well-managed with prescribed pain medications, and he reported minimal discomfort.

The patient was instructed to monitor his blood pressure and blood sugar levels closely in the postoperative period, and there were no significant changes in either. He was advised to continue his antihypertensive and antidiabetic medications as prescribed. The patient's surgical incision healed well without any signs of infection or wound dehiscence. He did not report any adverse reactions or side effects from the prescribed medications.

In summary, this 50-year-old male patient with a medical history of hypertension and diabetes underwent an elective hernia repair surgery with no complications. He had a smooth postoperative course, following all instructions and experiencing proper wound healing. The patient's blood pressure and blood glucose remained controlled, confirming the stability of his medical conditions during the surgical and recovery period. Overall, the patient's surgery was successful, and he recovered well without any complications.
