He also has hyperlipidemia and has been on a statin medication for the past 5 years. His diabetes was diagnosed 3 years ago, and he manages it with oral hypoglycemic agents.

The patient's preoperative lab results revealed a well-controlled blood pressure and lipid profile. His fasting blood glucose was within normal range, indicating good diabetes control. His hemoglobin A1c level was 6.5%, suggesting satisfactory long-term blood sugar management. The patient's EKG showed no abnormalities.

During the surgery, the patient received general anesthesia and the hernia repair was successfully completed without any complications. The patient tolerated the surgery well and had no intraoperative complications.

In the postoperative period, the patient was closely monitored for any signs of infection or complications. He remained in the hospital for 24 hours for observation. The patient reported mild pain at the incision site, which was managed with pain medication. He was encouraged to ambulate and perform deep breathing exercises to prevent complications such as atelectasis or deep vein thrombosis.

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful overall. He did not develop any surgical site infections or wound complications. His pain gradually subsided over the next few days, and he was able to resume regular oral intake without any issues. The patient's vital signs remained stable throughout his hospital stay.

On the day of discharge, the patient was reviewed by the surgical team, who ensured that he understood the postoperative instructions and provided him with a prescription for pain medication for use at home. The patient was advised to follow up with his primary care physician within two weeks for assessment and removal of sutures.

During the post-discharge follow-up appointment, the patient's incision site was examined and found to be healing well with no signs of infection. The patient reported that his pain had resolved completely. His blood pressure and blood glucose levels were within target ranges, and his lipid profile remained controlled. The patient did not experience any complications related to his surgery during the postoperative period.
