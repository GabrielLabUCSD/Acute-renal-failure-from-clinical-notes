In preparation for surgery, the patient has undergone preoperative evaluations, including an electrical conduction study to assess his cardiac function and clearance for anesthesia. The results of these tests were unremarkable, and the patient was deemed suitable for surgery.

Regular monitoring of the patient's vital signs, oxygen saturation levels, and pain scores was conducted to ensure appropriate management and detect any potential complications. The patient's surgical wound was closely monitored for signs of infection or delayed healing.

Despite the patient's complex medical history, there were no immediate postoperative complications. The patient's vital signs remained stable, and he did not develop any signs of surgical site infection.
