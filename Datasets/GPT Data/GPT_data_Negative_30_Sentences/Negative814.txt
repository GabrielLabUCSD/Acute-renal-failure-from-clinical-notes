His diabetes is managed with diet and exercise, and his last HbA1c level was within the target range.

He does not smoke and reports occasional alcohol consumption.

His preoperative chest X-ray showed no abnormalities.

He was able to tolerate a regular diet and was encouraged to ambulate to prevent postoperative complications.
The surgical wound was assessed daily and showed no signs of infection or dehiscence.

During his follow-up visits, the patient was asymptomatic and reported good compliance with his medications and dietary recommendations.
His surgical wound continued to heal without complications or signs of infection.
Over the next few weeks, the patient gradually resumed his daily activities without any limitations.
He had no postoperative complications, including acute renal failure.
