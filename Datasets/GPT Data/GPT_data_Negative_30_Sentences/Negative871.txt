The patient was closely monitored for any signs of infection or other complications, including fever, increased drainage from the incision site, or elevated white blood cell count. No such complications were observed.

The patient was encouraged to quit smoking prior to the surgery to improve his overall lung function and reduce the risk of postoperative complications. He was provided with smoking cessation resources and support. However, the patient expressed reluctance to quit at this time.

Given the patient's history of COPD and smoking, his risk of developing postoperative pneumonia or exacerbation of his underlying lung disease was carefully assessed. The patient was closely monitored for any signs of respiratory distress, including increased work of breathing, decreased oxygen saturation, or changes in lung sounds. He was provided with deep breathing exercises and incentive spirometry to promote optimal lung expansion and prevent atelectasis.

Regarding complications, the patient did not experience any acute renal failure; however, it is important to note that this complication is not included in this summary. The patient successfully recovered from the surgery and was discharged home on postoperative day 5 with instructions to follow up with his primary care physician and pulmonologist.
