The patient is now planning to have a knee replacement surgery due to severe osteoarthritis in his left knee. He has been experiencing progressive pain and limited mobility in that knee for the past year.

In preparation for the surgery, the patient underwent a series of preoperative tests which included blood work, chest X-ray, and an electrocardiogram. These tests revealed no abnormalities, and the patient was cleared for surgery.

During the surgery, the patient was placed under general anesthesia and the orthopedic surgeon performed the knee replacement procedure. The procedure went smoothly, and the patient did not experience any complications during or immediately after the surgery.

Following the surgery, the patient was closely monitored in the recovery room before being transferred to a regular hospital room. He was given intravenous pain medications and was encouraged to start moving his knee as tolerated under the guidance of a physical therapist.

Over the next few days, the patient gradually increased his mobility and was able to bear weight on his operated leg with the assistance of a walker. He continued to take pain medications to manage postoperative pain.

On the fourth day post-surgery, the patient developed a low-grade fever which was attributed to the normal postoperative inflammatory response. He was started on antibiotics as a precaution and his fever resolved within 48 hours.

The patient was discharged from the hospital on the sixth day post-surgery with a prescription for oral pain medications and instructions for physical therapy exercises to be done at home. He was also advised to follow up with his primary care physician in two weeks to assess wound healing and monitor his overall recovery.

During the follow-up visit, the patient had no complaints of pain or fever. His wound was healing well, and there were no signs of infection or other complications. The patient was instructed to continue his physical therapy and gradually increase his activities as tolerated.

Throughout the postoperative course, the patient diligently followed the instructions provided by his healthcare team. He continued to take his regular medications for hypertension and type 2 diabetes mellitus as prescribed.

At the six-week postoperative follow-up appointment, the patient's knee function had significantly improved, and he reported minimal pain while performing activities of daily living. His physical therapist noted good range of motion and strength in the operated knee.

The patient did not experience any complications during his postoperative course, and his recovery was considered successful. He was advised to continue with regular exercise and weight management to maintain the durability of the knee replacement.
