The patient is a 55-year-old male who is planning to have surgery. He has a past medical history significant for hypertension and diabetes mellitus type 2. His blood pressure has been well-controlled on medication, and his last HbA1c was within the target range. He denies any symptoms related to his hypertension and diabetes.

Upon further history taking, the patient reports occasional episodes of chest pain, which are usually relieved by rest and nitroglycerin. He denies any other symptoms of cardiovascular disease such as shortness of breath or palpitations. He has not had a recent stress test or cardiac imaging.

The patient has a surgical history significant for a cholecystectomy performed five years ago due to symptomatic cholelithiasis. He tolerated the procedure well and had an uneventful recovery.

His current medications include lisinopril for hypertension and metformin for diabetes. He also takes atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia. He denies any medication allergies.

Socially, the patient is married and lives with his wife. He is a non-smoker and does not consume alcohol. He is not currently employed but has a supportive family network.

On physical examination, the patient appears well-nourished and in no acute distress. His blood pressure is within normal limits, and his heart rate is regular. Cardiovascular examination reveals normal heart sounds without murmurs or gallops. Lungs are clear on auscultation, and there is no lower extremity edema.

Laboratory investigations reveal a complete blood count within normal limits. His electrolytes, liver function tests, and renal function tests are all within the normal ranges. His fasting blood glucose and HbA1c are also within target levels.

Preoperative imaging includes a chest x-ray, which shows no acute abnormalities. An electrocardiogram (ECG) reveals normal sinus rhythm. The patient is scheduled for surgery under general anesthesia.

In the postoperative setting, the patient's recovery is generally uneventful. His vital signs remain stable, and he is able to tolerate oral intake. Pain is adequately controlled with analgesics, and there are no signs of infection at the surgical site. The patient ambulates without difficulty and occasionally requires assistance from nursing staff.

He is started on prophylactic antibiotics to prevent surgical site infection. Venous thromboembolism prophylaxis is also initiated, including sequential compression devices and subcutaneous heparin. The patient is educated on the signs and symptoms of deep vein thrombosis and pulmonary embolism.

Postoperative laboratory tests, including complete blood count and electrolytes, continue to remain within normal limits. His blood glucose levels are closely monitored, and insulin therapy is adjusted accordingly to maintain glycemic control.

During the postoperative course, the patient remains hemodynamically stable without any evidence of cardiovascular complications. His chest pain has not recurred since the surgery. There are no signs of surgical complications, such as wound dehiscence or abscess formation.

The patient receives appropriate postoperative care, including early ambulation, respiratory exercises, and regular assessments of pain and wound healing. He is discharged home on postoperative day 3 with a plan for outpatient follow-up.

In summary, this 55-year-old male patient with a history of hypertension and diabetes mellitus type 2 is planning to undergo surgery. His preoperative workup reveals well-controlled chronic medical conditions and stable vital signs. He has no significant surgical history apart from a cholecystectomy. His postoperative course is uneventful, with stable vital signs, good pain control, and no signs of infection or other complications. He is discharged home in a satisfactory condition for continued recovery and follow-up.
