The patient also has a history of COPD, which he manages with bronchodilators and inhaled corticosteroids. He occasionally experiences exacerbations requiring short courses of oral corticosteroids and antibiotics. Despite this, he has maintained relatively good lung function, with a forced expiratory volume in one second (FEV1) of 70% predicted.

In addition to his chronic medical conditions, the patient has a history of a previous surgery (inguinal hernia repair) without any significant complications. He recovered well and had an uneventful postoperative course. This is relevant as his previous surgery may impact the type of anesthesia and postoperative pain management for the upcoming procedure.

Close monitoring of blood pressure and blood glucose levels will be essential throughout the perioperative period. The use of bronchodilators for his COPD should be continued, aiming to optimize his lung function preoperatively.

Postoperatively, the patient will be closely monitored for any signs of complications, especially related to his chronic conditions. Given his medical history, wound healing and infection should be closely monitored. Additionally, pulmonary complications such as postoperative pneumonia or exacerbation of COPD should be carefully assessed. Early ambulation and incentive spirometry will be encouraged to minimize the risk of postoperative respiratory complications.
