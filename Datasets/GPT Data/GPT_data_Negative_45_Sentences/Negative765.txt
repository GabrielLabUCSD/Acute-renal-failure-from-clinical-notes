He tolerated the surgery well and did not experience any immediate complications such as bleeding, infection, or issues with anesthesia. Pain management was provided with intravenous opioids initially, followed by transition to oral analgesics as tolerated.

No complications such as wound infection, deep venous thrombosis, or pulmonary embolism were noted. The patient received appropriate preventive measures including early ambulation, deep breathing exercises, and pharmacologic thromboprophylaxis with subcutaneous enoxaparin.

The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 2. He was given a prescription for oral analgesics to manage his pain at home and was advised to monitor his surgical wound for signs of infection. Instructions were also provided regarding gradual resumption of regular activities and restrictions, if any.

A follow-up appointment was scheduled for two weeks post-discharge to assess the surgical wound and ensure optimal recovery. The patient was also instructed to continue his regular medications for hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus.

No complications were noted during his postoperative course, and he was discharged home on postoperative day 2 in stable condition.
