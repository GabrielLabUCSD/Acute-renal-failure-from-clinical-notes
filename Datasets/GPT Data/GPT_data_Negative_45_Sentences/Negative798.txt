The patient's vital signs and physical examination were unremarkable, with a body mass index of 28. She has no known drug allergies.

During her hospital stay, Patient X received appropriate education regarding wound care and signs of infection. She was provided with written instructions and contact numbers to seek immediate medical attention if needed.

Patient X reported feeling better overall and no longer experienced any dizziness or fatigue. Her pain had resolved completely, and she was able to resume her normal activities without limitations.

No complications such as wound infection, bleeding, or deep vein thrombosis were observed in Patient X. Her postoperative course was smooth, and she was satisfied with the surgical outcome.
