The patient is a 58-year-old male who is scheduled to undergo surgery for a hernia repair. He has a significant medical history including hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus. He has been managing these conditions with medications, including lisinopril, atorvastatin, and metformin.

The patient has a history of smoking, with a 30 pack-year history, but he quit smoking 5 years ago. He does not have any known allergies and denies any history of previous surgeries.

His preoperative assessments and lab results are within normal limits, including a normal complete blood count, coagulation profile, and electrolyte levels. His fasting blood glucose level is well controlled at 110 mg/dL. His blood pressure is well managed with lisinopril, averaging around 130/80 mmHg. His cholesterol levels are also within target range due to atorvastatin therapy.

In terms of surgical risk factors, the patient is overweight, with a body mass index (BMI) of 30, but he does not have any other significant risk factors such as heart disease or chronic kidney disease. His exercise tolerance is good, reporting no limitations in his activities of daily living.

During the surgical procedure, the patient is given general anesthesia, and the surgery is performed without any complications. The hernia is successfully repaired, and the patient is monitored closely during the postoperative period for any signs of infection or bleeding.

In terms of the postoperative course, the patient's vital signs are stable, and he recovers well from the anesthesia. He is started on clear fluids soon after the surgery and is able to tolerate them without any issues. His pain is managed with opioids initially, but he is transitioned to non-opioid analgesics as soon as possible.

The patient is ambulating on the same day of surgery and is encouraged to do breathing exercises to prevent lung infections. He is started on prophylactic antibiotics to prevent surgical site infections.

The patient's glucose levels are monitored closely, and his diabetes medication regimen is adjusted according to his blood glucose readings. His blood pressure remains stable throughout the postoperative period.

Complications-wise, the patient does not experience any significant postoperative complications such as wound infections, bleeding, or respiratory issues. His surgical incision heals well without any signs of dehiscence or infection.

The patient is discharged home on postoperative day 2 with instructions for wound care and pain management. He is advised to gradually increase his activity level but to avoid heavy lifting for the next few weeks.

A follow-up appointment is scheduled for him in two weeks to monitor his surgical incision and review his medication regimen. He is also referred to a physical therapist for further guidance on exercises to strengthen his abdominal muscles.

In conclusion, this patient with a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and type 2 diabetes mellitus underwent surgery for a hernia repair successfully. He had a smooth postoperative course without any complications. The patient's chronic conditions were well managed during the perioperative period, and he was discharged home in satisfactory condition with appropriate instructions for follow-up care.
