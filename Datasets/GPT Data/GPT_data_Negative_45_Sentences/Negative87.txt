The patient is a middle-aged male who is planning to have surgery. He has a medical history of hypertension, diabetes, and high cholesterol. His blood pressure is controlled with medication, and his diabetes is managed with diet and exercise. The patient currently takes metformin for diabetes and a statin for high cholesterol.

In terms of surgical history, the patient has previously undergone appendectomy and hernia repair without complications. He has no known drug allergies. The patient does not smoke but occasionally drinks alcohol in moderation.

Laboratory tests were performed prior to surgery. The patient's blood glucose levels were within normal limits and HbA1c was well-controlled. His lipid profile indicated slightly elevated total cholesterol and LDL cholesterol, but his HDL cholesterol was within the target range. Renal function tests showed no signs of acute renal failure, with creatinine and blood urea nitrogen (BUN) levels within normal limits.

A preoperative ECG was done, which revealed normal sinus rhythm. His chest X-ray was unremarkable. The patient has no history of bleeding disorders or abnormal coagulation. He does not take any antiplatelet or anticoagulant medications.

During the surgery, the patient was under general anesthesia, and the procedure went smoothly with no complications reported in the operative notes. The surgery lasted for several hours.

In the immediate postoperative period, the patient was transferred to the recovery room and monitored closely. Vital signs were stable, and pain was managed with IV opioid analgesics. The patient received prophylactic antibiotics to prevent infection. Incentive spirometry was initiated to prevent postoperative atelectasis.

On the first postoperative day, the patient was mobilized, and clear liquid diet was initiated. His pain was adequately controlled with oral analgesics. The surgical wound was clean and dry without any signs of infection. The patient's vital signs remained stable, and he did not complain of any respiratory or cardiovascular symptoms.

Over the next few days, the patient's diet was advanced to regular food, and he was able to tolerate it well. He continued to ambulate regularly. Wound care was maintained, and the surgical site continued to show no signs of infection. His pain gradually decreased, and oral analgesics were gradually tapered.

During the postoperative course, the patient did not experience any surgical complications such as wound dehiscence, hematoma, or seroma formation. There were no signs of postoperative bleeding or infection. His urinary output remained within normal limits, indicating preserved renal function.

The patient was discharged home on the fifth postoperative day. Instructions were given regarding wound care, medications, activity restrictions, and follow-up appointments. He was advised to monitor his blood pressure, blood glucose levels, and cholesterol regularly. The patient was educated on signs of infection, deep vein thrombosis, and when to seek medical attention.

Upon follow-up, the patient's wound had healed well without any issues. His blood pressure, blood glucose levels, and cholesterol were within target ranges. The patient did not report any postoperative complications during the follow-up period, including acute renal failure. He was satisfied with the surgical outcome and expressed gratitude for the care provided by the medical team.
