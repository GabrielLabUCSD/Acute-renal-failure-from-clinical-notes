In rare cases, patients may develop acute renal failure after surgery. Acute renal failure is the sudden loss of kidney function, leading to a buildup of waste products and fluids in the body. This can occur as a result of various factors, including decreased blood flow to the kidneys, exposure to potentially harmful substances during surgery, or the use of certain medications.

Symptoms of acute renal failure may include decreased urine output, fluid retention, electrolyte imbalances, and fatigue. The condition is typically diagnosed through blood tests and imaging studies.

Treatment for acute renal failure may involve fluid and electrolyte management, medication adjustments, and dialysis in severe cases. The patient would be closely monitored by a nephrologist and the surgical team to manage the condition effectively.

Preventative measures, such as optimizing hydration, avoiding nephrotoxic medications, and closely monitoring blood pressure during surgery, can help reduce the risk of acute renal failure.
