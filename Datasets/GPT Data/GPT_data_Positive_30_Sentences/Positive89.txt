His physical examination reveals a well-nourished male with no signs of acute illness.
His heart sounds are regular and clear, with no murmurs or abnormal sounds.

Laboratory tests show normal complete blood count, liver function tests, and kidney function tests.

He has been instructed to avoid eating or drinking for a specified period before the surgery.

In the post-operative period, the patient develops acute renal failure.
This may be due to intraoperative hypotension and decreased renal perfusion.
Signs of acute renal failure include decreased urine output, elevated creatinine and BUN levels, and electrolyte imbalances.
The patient's fluid status will be closely monitored, and he may require intravenous fluids or diuretics to improve kidney function.
Consultation with a nephrologist may be necessary for further management.
The patient will be educated about the importance of medication adherence, regular follow-up visits, and lifestyle modifications to prevent further complications.
