He does not have a history of any surgical procedures.

He denies any drug allergies or adverse reactions to medication.
The patient reports no family history of significant medical conditions.

The etiology of the acute renal failure is unclear, but it may be related to intraoperative hypotension or exposure to nephrotoxic agents.
The patient is closely monitored for electrolyte imbalances and fluid overload.
He is placed on strict fluid management to optimize renal function.

The patient's urine output is monitored hourly to assess renal function.

The patient is started on broad-spectrum antibiotics to treat the infection.
He is also given a nasogastric tube to decompress the bowel and facilitate bowel function.
The patient's renal function gradually improves with appropriate management.
His urine output increases, and laboratory tests show a decrease in creatinine and blood urea nitrogen levels.
The patient is eventually discharged from the hospital with close follow-up with the nephrologist.
He is instructed to continue taking his antihypertensive medication as prescribed.
The patient is advised to avoid any nephrotoxic medications or substances.
He is counseled on the importance of maintaining a healthy lifestyle and managing his hypertension to prevent future complications.
