One potential complication that Mr. Smith and his healthcare team should be aware of is acute renal failure. This can occur as a result of decreased blood flow to the kidneys during surgery, leading to kidney dysfunction and impaired filtering of waste products from the blood. Risk factors for developing acute renal failure include advanced age, pre-existing kidney disease, and certain medications or procedures that can be nephrotoxic.

To minimize the risk of acute renal failure, it is important for Mr. Smith's surgical team to closely monitor his fluid status, ensuring he remains adequately hydrated throughout the perioperative period. They should also be cautious with certain medications, such as nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and radiocontrast agents, that have the potential to further compromise kidney function.

Postoperatively, Mr. Smith should be closely monitored for signs and symptoms of acute renal failure, including decreased urine output, fluid overload, electrolyte imbalances, and elevated creatinine and BUN levels. Prompt intervention and close monitoring can help prevent further kidney damage and promote optimal recovery.
