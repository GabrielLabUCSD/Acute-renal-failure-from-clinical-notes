A nephrology consult was obtained, and the patient was started on medication to support his kidney function, including diuretics and ACE inhibitors.
The patient's fluid intake and output were closely monitored, and his urine output gradually improved over the next few days.
Renal function tests were repeated every 24 hours to assess his progress, and the results showed a steady improvement.
During his hospital stay, the patient also received antibiotics to prevent any potential infection that could further damage his kidneys.
The patient's surgical wound was examined daily, and there were no signs of infection or wound dehiscence.
Physical therapy was initiated to promote early mobilization and prevent complications such as deep vein thrombosis.
