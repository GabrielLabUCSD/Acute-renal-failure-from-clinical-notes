The patient's urine output gradually improves, and his serum creatinine and blood urea nitrogen levels decrease.
The patient's acute renal failure is determined to be post-renal in nature, most likely due to obstruction caused by surgical complications.
After appropriate management and intervention, the patient's renal function stabilizes, and he does not require long-term renal replacement therapy.
The patient's perioperative acute renal failure is determined to be a temporary and reversible complication.
The patient is closely monitored throughout his recovery and receives appropriate follow-up care to ensure his renal function continues to improve.
He is started on medication to prevent future episodes of renal failure, such as angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors or angiotensin receptor blockers.
