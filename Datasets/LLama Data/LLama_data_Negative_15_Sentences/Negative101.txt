Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents for elective surgery. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, for which he takes medication regularly. He had a myocardial infarction 10 years ago and has been compliant with his cardiac medications since then. He also has a history of non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus, which is well-controlled with diet and oral medications. He has no history of bleeding disorders or allergies to anesthesia. His last surgical procedure was a cholecystectomy 15 years ago, which went uneventfully. He is currently taking aspirin and clopidogrel for his cardiac condition, but will discontinue them preoperatively as instructed.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful surgery and was transferred to the recovery room in stable condition. He had some nausea and vomiting in the immediate postoperative period but responded well to antiemetic medication. He had a patent airway and stable vital signs throughout the recovery period. He was able to tolerate sips of water and clear liquids without any issues. He was monitored closely for signs of bleeding or infection, but none were observed.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during his postoperative course. He had some mild discomfort and pain, which were managed effectively with pain medication. He also had a mild episode of postoperative hypotension, which responded well to fluid replacement. There were no signs of wound infection or bleeding. He was able to resume his medications as prescribed by his physician without any issues. He was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 3 in good health and was followed up with in the clinic a week later.
