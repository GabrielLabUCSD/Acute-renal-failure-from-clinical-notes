Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has been diagnosed with spondylolisthesis and degenerative disc disease. The patient has tried physical therapy and medication, but has not experienced adequate relief. He is currently smoking 1 pack per day and has a history of hypertension. The patient is allergic to morphine and has a history of constipation.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative workup, including blood work, electrocardiogram, and imaging studies. The patient's blood work revealed a mild anemia and elevated blood urea nitrogen (BUN) and creatinine levels. The electrocardiogram showed a normal sinus rhythm. The imaging studies demonstrated severe spondylolisthesis and degenerative disc disease at L4-L5 and L5-S1.

Operative Course:

The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 and L5-S1 fusion with instrumentation. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately 3 hours to complete. The patient tolerated the procedure well and did not experience any intraoperative complications.

Postoperative Course:

The patient was transferred to the postanesthesia care unit (PACU) for recovery. He experienced some nausea and vomiting immediately postoperatively, which was managed with antiemetics. The patient's pain was well controlled with a patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) pump. He was able to ambulate and void without difficulty.

On postoperative day 2, the patient developed a fever of 101.3°F (38.5°C) and complained of increased pain in his lower back. The patient was evaluated by the surgical team and was found to have a mild wound infection. The infection was treated with antibiotics and the patient's fever resolved. The patient's pain was managed with adjustments to his PCA pump.

On postoperative day 4, the patient experienced some constipation, which was managed with laxatives and stool softeners. The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 5 with a prescription for pain medication and instructions for wound care.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during his postoperative course. However, he did develop a mild wound infection on postoperative day 2, which was treated with antibiotics. The patient also experienced constipation on postoperative day 4, which was managed with medication.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 and L5-S1 fusion with instrumentation. He experienced a mild wound infection and constipation during his postoperative course, but these were managed effectively with antibiotics and laxatives, respectively. The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 5 and will follow up with the surgical team in 2 weeks for further evaluation.
