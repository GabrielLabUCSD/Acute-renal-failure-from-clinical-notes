Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents for elective surgery. He has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus (NIDDM). The patient had a myocardial infarction (MI) five years ago and has been compliant with his medications since then. He has also been a smoker for the past 20 years but has been advised to quit smoking preoperatively. His surgery is planned for a hernia repair, and the anesthesiologist has recommended a general anesthesia with endotracheal intubation. Airway management was uneventful. The patient had a stable intraoperative course, and the procedure was completed without any complications.

Postoperatively, the patient had some nausea and vomiting, which was managed with ondansetron. He also had some mild pain, which was controlled with fentanyl. He was extubated and transferred to the recovery room (PACU) in stable condition. He remained in the PACU for 4 hours for close monitoring before being transferred to the surgical ward. The patient had an uneventful postoperative course and was discharged from the hospital after 3 days. No complications were encountered during his hospital stay.
