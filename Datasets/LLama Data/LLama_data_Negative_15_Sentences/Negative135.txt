Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he is a current smoker. He denies any history of bleeding disorders or allergies to anesthesia.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery, which was successful in relieving his back pain and radiculopathy. His postoperative course was uneventful, and he was discharged home on the fifth postoperative day.

The patient experienced some minor postoperative discomfort, including nausea and vomiting, which were managed with medication. He also developed a mild wound infection, which was treated with antibiotics. However, he did not experience any significant complications, such as pulmonary embolism or pneumonia.

The patient followed up with his surgeon and primary care physician regularly and had a smooth recovery. He was able to return to work and resume his normal activities within 6 weeks after surgery.

The patient expressed satisfaction with the surgical outcome and reported significant improvement in his quality of life. He was advised to continue with physical therapy and maintain a healthy lifestyle to prevent future recurrences of back pain.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgery and an uneventful postoperative course, with minor complications that were effectively managed.
