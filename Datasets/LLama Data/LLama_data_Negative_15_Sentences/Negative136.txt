Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic low back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant improvement. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he is a current smoker. He denies any significant cardiovascular or respiratory history.

The patient underwent a lumbar decompression and fusion surgery. The surgery was completed without any intraoperative complications.

Postoperatively, the patient had a painful wound site and required frequent doses of narcotics for pain control. He also experienced some postoperative nausea and vomiting.

On the second postoperative day, the patient developed a fever and tachycardia, concerning for surgical site infection. Blood cultures were drawn, and the patient was started on broad-spectrum antibiotics.

The patient's fever and tachycardia resolved within 24 hours, and he continued to recover from his surgery. He was discharged home on postoperative day 4 with a wound vac and instructions for wound care.

At his 2-week follow-up appointment, the patient's incision site looked well-healed, and he reported significant improvement in his back pain. He continued to have some numbness and weakness in his left leg, but it had improved from his preoperative status.

At 6 weeks postoperative, the patient had a significant increase in his back pain and was found to have a pseudarthrosis on imaging studies. He underwent a second surgery for revision of the fusion.

The patient had a difficult postoperative course after the second surgery. He developed a wound infection, which required antibiotics and wound dressing changes. He also experienced respiratory failure and required a brief period of mechanical ventilation.

After a prolonged hospital course, the patient was discharged home with a wound vac and oxygen therapy. He continued to recover and had a follow-up appointment scheduled for 6 weeks.

At his 6-week follow-up appointment, the patient reported significant improvement in his back pain and left leg symptoms. His wound had fully healed, and he was able to discontinue his wound vac.

The patient was satisfied with the outcome of his surgery and felt that it had significantly improved his quality of life. He was grateful for the care provided by his surgical team and promised to follow up with his primary care physician for ongoing management of his medical conditions.

The patient's postoperative course was complicated by a wound infection and respiratory failure, but he ultimately made a full recovery and was satisfied with the outcome of his surgery.
