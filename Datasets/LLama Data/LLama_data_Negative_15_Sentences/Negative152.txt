Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and Left hip pain. The pain started after a fall 10 years ago and has been worsening over time. The patient has tried physical therapy, chiropractic care, and pain medication without significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and smoking. He had a myocardial infarction 5 years ago and has been compliant with his medications since then. He denies any respiratory or gastrointestinal symptoms. He is allergic to sulfa medications. The patient had a previous surgery (inguinal hernia repair) 10 years ago without any complications.

Postoperative Course:

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery. The surgery was uneventful, and the patient tolerated it well. He was transferred to the recovery room in stable condition and was monitored closely for any signs of complications. He was given adequate pain medication and was encouraged to move around and deep breathe to prevent respiratory complications. He was discharged from the hospital on the second postoperative day without any complications.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any acute renal failure or respiratory complications during his postoperative course. However, he did develop a wound infection that was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some numbness and weakness in his legs after surgery, which resolved gradually over time.

Follow-up:

The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon 2 weeks after discharge. He reported some persistent back pain, but the surgeon was satisfied with his progress. The patient was advised to continue with pain medication and physical therapy. The patient also had a follow-up appointment with his primary care physician 4 weeks after discharge, where he reported improved pain control and improved mobility.

Overall, the patient tolerated the surgery well and had an uneventful postoperative course. Although he experienced some complications, they were managed effectively with appropriate treatment. The patient showed good progress during his follow-up appointments and was able to return to his normal activities without any significant limitations.
