Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and decreased mobility. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and medication without significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and is currently taking medication for both conditions. He is a current smoker and has a history of alcohol use. The patient has no known allergies.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative workup, which included a physical examination, laboratory tests, and imaging studies. His blood work showed mild anemia and mild elevations in liver enzymes. His electrocardiogram showed no evidence of ischemia. A chest X-ray was normal.

Operative Course:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion surgery. The procedure was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately 3 hours to complete. The patient tolerated the procedure well and did not experience any intraoperative complications.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a uneventful postoperative course. He experienced some expected postoperative pain, which was managed effectively with narcotic medication. He was able to ambulate independently on the first postoperative day and was discharged home on postoperative day 3.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any acute complications during his hospital stay. However, he did develop a wound infection on postoperative day 7, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some postoperative nausea and vomiting, which was managed with antiemetic medication.

Conclusion:

Overall, the patient had a successful surgical outcome and did not experience any major complications during his hospital stay. However, he did develop a wound infection and some postoperative nausea and vomiting, which were effectively managed. The patient will require ongoing follow-up care to monitor his back pain and mobility, as well as to manage his chronic medical conditions.
