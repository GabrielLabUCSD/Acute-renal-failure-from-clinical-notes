Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has tried physical therapy and conservative management without significant relief. He is a candidate for lumbar fusion surgery.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. He is a non-smoker and drinks alcohol in moderation.

The patient has no known allergies and has not had any previous surgeries.

Preoperative lab work shows normal kidney function and a low risk of bleeding complications.

The patient is scheduled for a lumbar fusion surgery, which includes thoracolumbar approach, discectomy, and interbody fusion.

The surgery is expected to take approximately 4 hours and will be performed under general anesthesia.

The postoperative course is expected to include a 2-day hospital stay for observation and pain management.

The patient will be discharged home with a lumbar brace and will need to follow a regiment of physical therapy to ensure proper healing.

One week postoperatively, the patient is doing well and reports minimal pain. He is ambulating independently and following his physical therapy regimen as prescribed.

At two weeks postoperative, the patient reports some improvement in his back pain, but it is not fully resolved. He is still following his physical therapy regimen.

At four weeks postoperative, the patient reports continued improvement in his back pain and is able to resume some of his normal daily activities.

At six weeks postoperative, the patient reports significant improvement in his back pain and is able to resume most of his normal daily activities. He is still following his physical therapy regimen.
