Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and pain management, without significant relief. The patient is a candidate for lumbar fusion surgery to alleviate his symptoms.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. He is a non-smoker and drinks alcohol occasionally. He has no known allergies to medications and has not had any previous surgeries.

The patient's physical examination reveals limited mobility in the lower back and left leg, with positive straight leg raising and crossed leg raising tests. His neurological examination is normal, with no signs of motor or sensory deficits.

Preoperative workup includes an MRI of the lumbar spine, which shows degenerative disc disease at L4-L5 and L5-S1, with mild spinal canal stenosis. The patient's blood work is normal, with no evidence of kidney or liver dysfunction.

The patient underwent lumbar fusion surgery without any intraoperative complications. His postoperative course was uneventful, with adequate pain management and mobilization. He was discharged home on postoperative day 4 with a plan for continued physical therapy and pain management.

At the 2-week follow-up appointment, the patient reported mild pain and discomfort at the surgical site, which was managed with analgesics. He was able to ambulate independently and showed improvement in his mobility.

At the 6-week follow-up appointment, the patient reported significant improvement in his back pain and mobility. He was able to return to his normal activities, including work and exercise.

At the 12-week follow-up appointment, the patient reported continued improvement in his back pain and mobility. He was able to walk 2 miles without any discomfort and had full range of motion in his lower back.

Overall, the patient had a successful outcome from his lumbar fusion surgery, with significant improvement in his back pain and mobility. He was able to return to his normal activities and had no complications during his postoperative course.
