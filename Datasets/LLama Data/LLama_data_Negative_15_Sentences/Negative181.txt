Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents for elective surgery. He has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes mellitus type 2. The patient has a history of smoking but quit 10 years ago. He denies any significant cardiovascular or respiratory medical history. He has no known allergies to medications and has no history of bleeding disorders. The patient"s surgical history includes a cholecystectomy 15 years ago and a hernia repair 10 years ago. He has been taking aspirin and metformin for his medical conditions. The patient"s lab work shows mild anemia and thrombocytopenia, but all other values are within normal limits. The patient"s ECG shows normal sinus rhythm with no ST segment changes or arrhythmias.

The patient had an uneventful intraoperative course and surgery was completed without any complications. He was extubated in the operating room and transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) in stable condition. He was able to take sips of water and had stable vital signs. He was started on a PCA (patient-controlled analgesia) pump for pain control. The patient had a urinary catheter placed during surgery which was removed on post-operative day 2.

The patient did not have any complications during his post-operative course. He had a minor issue with constipation but it was resolved with laxatives and stool softeners.
