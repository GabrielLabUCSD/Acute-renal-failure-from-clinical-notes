Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain andleft knee instability. He has a history of smoking and has been diagnosed with hypertension.

Preoperative medications include ibuprofen 800mg PO Q8H and alprazolam 0.5mg PO Q8H.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery 5 years ago and has since been experiencing increased pain and discomfort in his left knee.
He has tried physical therapy and bracing without significant improvement.

The patient is allergic to penicillin and has a history of hypercoagulability.

Intraoperative course was uneventful, without any complications.

POSTOPERATIVE COURSE:

Day 1:

* Patient had a rough night with significant nausea and vomiting.
* He had a BP of 180/100 and a HR of 120.
* He was given IV fluids and antiemetics and his symptoms improved by the next morning.

Day 2:

* Patient complained of increased pain in his left knee and was given an additional dose of fentanyl.
* His BP and HR remained stable.

Day 3:

* Patient was able to ambulate with assistance and had a decrease in pain.
* He was discharged home with a walker and instructed to continue physical therapy.

Complications:

* None

Follow-up appointment was set for 2 weeks post-discharge to monitor his progress and manage any potential complications.
