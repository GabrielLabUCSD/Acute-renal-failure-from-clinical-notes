Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents for elective surgery. He has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus (NIDDM). His surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair. He has no known allergies and takes no medications.

The patient's family history reveals a positive history of coronary artery disease and stroke. He is a current smoker and drinks alcohol occasionally. His review of systems is positive for shortness of breath and peripheral neuropathy.

On physical examination, the patient's vital signs are within normal limits. His cardiovascular and respiratory examinations reveal no abnormalities. His neurological examination shows decreased sensation in his feet.

Laboratory studies reveal elevated blood urea nitrogen (BUN) and creatinine levels, consistent with chronic kidney disease (CKD). His electrolyte levels are within normal limits.

The patient underwent a successful surgical procedure, and his postoperative course was uncomplicated. He did not experience any respiratory or cardiovascular complications.

However, on the second postoperative day, the patient developed a fever and tachycardia, concerning for infection. Blood cultures were drawn, and broad-spectrum antibiotics were initiated.

The patient's fever and tachycardia resolved within 24 hours, and he was afebrile and hemodynamically stable on the third postoperative day. He was discharged home on postoperative day 5 with a 7-day course of antibiotics.

Follow-up appointment was scheduled for 2 weeks after discharge to monitor his wound healing and review his lab results.

Overall, the patient had an uneventful postoperative course, except for a brief episode of fever and tachycardia that was treated effectively. His chronic medical conditions were well-controlled during his hospital stay.
