Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and pain medication, without significant relief. The patient is a candidate for spinal fusion surgery to address his chronic pain and improve his quality of life.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative workup, which included a physical examination, laboratory tests, and imaging studies. His blood work revealed mild anemia and elevated inflammatory markers, which were addressed with iron supplements and anti-inflammatory medication. The patient's imaging studies showed significant spinal stenosis and degenerative changes at L4-L5 and L5-S1.

Surgical Procedure:

The patient underwent a successful spinal fusion surgery, where a posterior lumbar interbody fusion was performed at L4-L5 and L5-S1. The surgery involved the removal of the degenerative disc and the insertion of a stabilizing implant.

Postoperative Course:

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and he was able to recover smoothly. He experienced some expected postoperative pain and discomfort, which was managed with pain medication. The patient was able to ambulate independently within the first 24 hours and was discharged home on the third postoperative day.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during his postoperative course. However, he did develop a mild wound infection, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some minor numbness in his lower extremities, which resolved with time.

Conclusion:

Overall, the patient's surgical outcome was successful, and he experienced significant improvement in his chronic back pain. The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and he was able to recover smoothly. The patient will continue to follow up with his surgeon and primary care physician to monitor his progress and address any ongoing concerns.
