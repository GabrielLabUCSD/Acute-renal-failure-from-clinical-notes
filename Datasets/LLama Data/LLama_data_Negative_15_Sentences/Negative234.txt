Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and is currently taking medication for both conditions. He is a nonsmoker and drinks alcohol occasionally.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery six months ago, which provided temporary relief, but the pain has since recurred. He has been experiencing worsening left leg weakness and numbness, and has difficulty walking long distances. The patient's most recent MRI showed significant stenosis at L4-L5 and L5-S1.

Preoperative examination reveals normal heart sounds, clear lungs, and stable vital signs. The patient's blood work is within normal limits, except for a slightly elevated white blood cell count.

Intraoperatively, the patient tolerated the procedure well, with no significant hemodynamic changes. The surgical site was noted to be clean and dry, with no signs of infection.

Postoperatively, the patient experienced some expected discomfort, which was managed with pain medication. He was able to ambulate independently within the first 24 hours and had a steady improvement in his pain and mobility over the first week.

However, on the seventh postoperative day, the patient developed a fever of 101.3°F (38.5°C) and reported increased pain at the surgical site. He was evaluated with a CT scan, which showed a possible early signs of wound infection. The patient was started on antibiotics and his fever resolved within 24 hours.

The patient continued to improve and was discharged home on postoperative day 10. He was advised to continue his pain medication and physical therapy regimen.

Follow-up appointment was scheduled for two weeks after discharge, and the patient was seen in the clinic at that time. His incision site was noted to be healing well, and he reported significant improvement in his pain and mobility.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgical outcome, with a minor postoperative complication that was effectively managed with antibiotics. He was able to return to his normal activities and reported significant improvement in his quality of life.
