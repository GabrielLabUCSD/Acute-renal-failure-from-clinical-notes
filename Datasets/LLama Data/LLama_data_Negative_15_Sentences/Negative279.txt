Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and numbness in the left leg. The patient has a history of lumbar spine surgery 10 years ago and has been experiencing increasing pain and weakness in the left leg over the past year. The patient has tried physical therapy and pain management without significant relief. The patient is currently taking Gabapentin and Hydrocodone for pain control. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, controlled with medication. The patient is a current smoker and has a history of alcohol use disorder, now in remission. The patient has no known allergies.

The patient will be undergoing a lumbar spine fusion surgery to address the chronic back pain and neurological symptoms. The patient will receive general anesthesia and will be closely monitored for any signs of neurological compromise during the procedure.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful surgery and was taken to the recovery room where he was closely monitored for any signs of neurological compromise. The patient had a satisfactory pain management and was able to ambulate independently with assistance on the first postoperative day. The patient had a minimal amount of drainage from the surgical site and was able to tolerate oral fluids without any issue.

The patient was transferred to the ward on the second postoperative day and continued to recover well. The patient had a few episodes of nausea and vomiting, but these were easily controlled with antiemetics. The patient was able to eat solid food and was up and about mobilizing without any assistance.

The patient was discharged home on the fourth postoperative day with a follow-up appointment in the clinic to check the progress and remove the sutures.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during the surgery or his postoperative course. The patient did have a mild urinary retention which was managed conservatively with bladder training and medication. The patient did not experience any wound infections, bleeding, or other complications. The patient's pain was well controlled and the patient was able to ambulate and mobilize without assistance a few days after surgery.

The patient will continue to be followed up in the clinic to monitor his progress and check for any signs of neurological compromise. Another appointment will be required to remove the sutures and the wound will be checked for any signs of infection.
