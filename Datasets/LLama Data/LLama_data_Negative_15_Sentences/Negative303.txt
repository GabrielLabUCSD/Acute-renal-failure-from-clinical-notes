Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and medication remedy without significant improvement. His MRI shows a large L4-L5 disc herniation and spinal stenosis. The patient decides to undergo a L4-L5 discectomy and fusion surgery.

The patient's past medical history includes hypertension and hyperlipidemia, for which he takes medication regularly. He has no known drug allergies and has never had a reaction to anesthesia. He is a nonsmoker and drinks alcohol occasionally.

The patient's postoperative course is uneventful. He experiences some expected postoperative pain and discomfort, which are managed effectively with pain medication. He follows his postoperative instructions carefully and begins mobilizing his legs and walking with assistance on the first postoperative day.

On the second postoperative day, the patient's wound is noted to be slightly red and swollen, but there are no signs of infection. His pain continues to improve, and he is able to ambulate independently with support.

On the third postoperative day, the patient develops a fever of 101.3 degrees Fahrenheit. Blood cultures are drawn, and broad-spectrum antibiotics are started. The fever resolves within 24 hours.

The patient continues to make a smooth recovery and is discharged home on postoperative day 4 with a follow-up appointment in the clinic for further assessment and suture removal.

Overall, the patient experiences a successful surgical outcome with minimal complications. He is advised to continue with physical therapy to optimize his recovery and prevent future recurrence of his symptoms.
