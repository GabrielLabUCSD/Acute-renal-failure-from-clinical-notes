Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and medication without significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he smokes one pack of cigarettes per day. He denies any significant cardiovascular or respiratory history. The patient underwent an MRI that showed a large L4-L5 disk herniation and spinal stenosis.

The patient underwent a left L4-L5 hemilaminectomy and diskectomy. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately two hours. The patient had a uneventful postoperative course and was able to ambulate independently the next day. He was discharged home on postoperative day two with a prescription for pain medication and instructions to limit heavy lifting and bending.

At the one-month follow-up appointment, the patient reported significant improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy. He denied any complications or adverse effects from the surgery. At the six-month follow-up, the patient reported continued improvement in his symptoms and had returned to his normal activities, including working and exercising. He denied any ongoing pain or discomfort.

The patient did not experience any complications during his postoperative course, including infection, nerve damage, or wound dehiscence. He did not require any additional surgical interventions and had a successful outcome from the surgery. The patient was pleased with the results and reported improved quality of life.
