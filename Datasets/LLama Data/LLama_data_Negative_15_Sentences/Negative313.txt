Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has been diagnosed with a lumbar disc herniation and has failed conservative management. He is a candidate for lumbar discectomy surgery.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. He has no known allergies and is not taking any medications that may interact with anesthesia.

The patient is a non-smoker and drinks alcohol occasionally. He has a healthy heart and lungs, with no history of cardiovascular disease or respiratory problems.

The patient has a body mass index (BMI) of 32, which may increase the risk of postoperative complications. However, he is otherwise healthy and a good candidate for surgery.

The patient will receive general anesthesia for the procedure, which carries a small risk of respiratory and cardiac complications. However, his overall health and lack of comorbidities reduce this risk.

The patient will be monitored closely throughout the procedure, with particular attention to his blood pressure and oxygen saturation levels.

The surgery is expected to take approximately two hours, and the patient will be discharged from the hospital the following day.

The patient will require pain management postoperatively, which will include a combination of opioids and non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs).

The patient will also require physical therapy to help him recover and regain strength in his back and leg.

There is a small risk of postoperative complications, including wound infection, nerve damage, and continued pain. However, these risks are minimized with proper wound care and close follow-up.

The patient will follow up with his surgeon within one week of discharge and will be seen by a physical therapist within two weeks.

Overall, the patient is a good candidate for surgery, and we expect a successful outcome with proper postoperative care and management.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a successful lumbar discectomy and was discharged from the hospital as planned.

He experienced some postoperative pain, which was managed effectively with opioids and NSAIDs.

The patient followed up with his surgeon within one week and reported improved pain and function.

He also started physical therapy within two weeks and has been making good progress.

There were no complications during the postoperative period, and the patient is expected to make a full recovery with continued therapy and proper wound care.
