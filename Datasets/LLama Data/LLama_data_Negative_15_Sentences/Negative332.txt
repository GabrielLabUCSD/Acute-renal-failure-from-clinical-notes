Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic low back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried physical therapy and medication without significant relief. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He is a nonsmoker and drinks occasionally. He denies any significant allergies. He is taking aspirin and thyroid medication.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative workup, including laboratory tests, imaging studies, and a cardiology consultation. His lab results showed mild anemia and elevated liver enzymes. His imaging studies demonstrated a lumbar spondylolisthesis and spinal stenosis. His cardiology consultation was negative for any significant cardiac pathology.

Surgical Procedure:

The patient underwent a lumbar decompression surgery, which included a laminectomy, facetectomy, and fusion. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately 3 hours.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He was transferred to the orthopedic floor and was monitored closely for any complications. He experienced some postoperative pain, which was managed effectively with pain medication. He was able to ambulate independently and had no difficulty with voiding or bowel movements.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during his postoperative course. He had some minor nausea and vomiting, which were managed with antiemetics. He also experienced some mild fatigue, which resolved with rest and hydration.

Follow-up:

The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon 2 weeks after the surgery. He reported improved pain and functional status. His incision site was healing well, and he was able to resume his regular activities. He was advised to continue physical therapy to improve his strength and flexibility.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, this patient had a successful lumbar decompression surgery and an uneventful postoperative course. He did not experience any significant complications and was able to resume his regular activities within a few weeks. His pain and functional status improved significantly, and he was advised to continue physical therapy for further improvement.
