Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has a history of lumbar spine degenerative disc disease and has failed conservative management. The patient is scheduled for a lumbar fusion surgery.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. The patient does not have a history of diabetes or any other chronic medical conditions.

The patient has a history of smoking but quit 5 years ago. The patient consumes alcohol occasionally but not excessively.

The patient has a history of allergy to penicillin and CEFOXITIN but has no known allergies to anesthesia.

The patient has a history of previous surgery, including a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair.

The patient's family history is significant for cardiovascular disease.

The patient's current medications include ibuprofen, gabapentin, and atorvastatin.

The patient's review of systems is otherwise unremarkable.

The patient's physical examination reveals a healthy male with normal vital signs.

The patient's neurological examination reveals decreased sensation to light touch and pinprick in the L4-S1 dermatomes.

The patient's musculoskeletal examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lumbar spine.

The patient's radiological studies, including an MRI and CT scan, reveal significant degenerative changes at L4-L5 and L5-S1.

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and the patient was discharged home on postoperative day 3.

The patient followed up with the surgeon at 2 weeks and 6 weeks postoperatively, and there were no complications noted.

The patient's pain and symptoms have significantly improved since the surgery.

The patient is able to ambulate without assistance and has returned to his normal activities.
