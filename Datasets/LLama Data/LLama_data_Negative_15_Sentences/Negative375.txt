Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has a history of smoking and has tried various conservative treatments without significant relief. The patient has been diagnosed with a herniated disc at L4-L5 and is planning to undergo a lumbar discectomy.

Preoperative medications include ibuprofen and gabapentin. The patient has no known allergies. The patient's surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a previous lumbar fusion.

Intraoperative course was uneventful. The patient had a standard anesthesia and was extubated in the operating room.

Postoperative course:

The patient had a routine postoperative recovery and was transferred to the floor on the first postoperative day. The patient had some nausea and vomiting but was managed with antiemetics. Pain was well controlled with patient-controlled analgesia (PCA). The patient was able to ambulate on the second postoperative day and was discharged home on postoperative day 3.

There were no complications during the patient's hospital stay. The patient follow-up appointment was scheduled for 2 weeks postoperatively.

At the follow-up appointment, the patient reported significant improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy. However, the patient experienced some numbness and tingling in the left lower extremity, which was managed with physical therapy.

Overall, the patient had an uncomplicated postoperative course and was satisfied with the outcome of the surgery.
