Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and pain medication without significant relief. He is a candidate for lumbar fusion surgery.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. He is a nonsmoker and drinks alcohol occasionally. He has no known allergies.

He denies any preoperative symptoms such as fever, chills, or difficulty urinating. His last bowel movement was this morning and he has no history of constipation. He has been NPO since midnight.

On examination, the patient's vital signs are stable, and he has normal heart sounds, without any murmurs or extra sounds. His lung fields are clear. He has tenderness to palpation over the lower back and positive straight leg raise. His neurological examination is normal.

The patient underwent lumbar fusion surgery under general anesthesia. The surgery went smoothly without any complications.

In the postoperative period, the patient's pain was managed with a patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) pump. He had some nausea and vomiting, which was controlled with antiemetics.

The patient had a urinary catheter inserted intraoperatively, which was removed on postoperative day 1. He had some urinary retention, which resolved with bladder training and conservative management.

The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 4 with a prescription for pain medication and instructions for wound care and rehabilitation.

On follow-up, the patient reported some discomfort at the incision site, which was managed with pain medication and wound care. He had no signs of infection and was satisfied with the results of the surgery.

The patient's neurological examination remained normal throughout the postoperative period. He has been advised to continue with rehabilitation and physical therapy to improve his back function.

Overall, the patient had an uneventful postoperative course and is recovering well from the surgery.
