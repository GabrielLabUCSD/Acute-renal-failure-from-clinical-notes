Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative joint disease. The patient has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient is a candidate for spinal fusion surgery to address the chronic pain and improve their quality of life.

Preoperative workup includes:

* Preoperative clearance by primary care physician
* Cardiology clearance due to history of hypertension
* Pulmonology clearance due to history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
* Anesthesia consultation
* Lab work, including blood count, electrolytes, and coagulation studies
* Imaging studies, including X-rays and MRI of the spine

Intraoperative course:

* The patient underwent spinal fusion surgery under general anesthesia.
* The procedure was uneventful, with no complications noted during the operation.

Postoperative course:

* The patient was transferred to the recovery room and monitored for vitals and pain management.
* The patient experienced some postoperative nausea and vomiting, which were managed with medication.
* The patient's pain was managed with a patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) pump.
* The patient was encouraged to ambulate and perform deep breathing exercises to prevent respiratory complications.
* The patient was discharged to home on postoperative day 3 with a follow-up appointment scheduled with the surgeon.

Complications:

* None noted during the intraoperative or postoperative period.

Follow-up:

* The patient followed up with the surgeon on postoperative day 7 and reported significant improvement in pain and mobility.
* The patient was advised to continue with physical therapy to further improve strength and flexibility.

Overall, the patient had an uncomplicated postoperative course and experienced significant improvement in pain and mobility following spinal fusion surgery.
