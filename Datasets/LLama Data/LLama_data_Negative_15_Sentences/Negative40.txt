Patient Summary:

He was recently diagnosed with lung cancer and is planning to undergo a lobectomy. His surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair.

Allergies:

The patient has a known allergy to penicillin.

Lab Results:

The patient's recent lab results show an elevated white blood cell count and a mild drop in hemoglobin. His liver function tests are within normal limits.

Imaging Studies:

A recent CT scan showed a 2-cm mass in the right lung, consistent with lung cancer.

The patient underwent a right upper lobectomy with a mediastinal lymph node dissection. The procedure was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately 3 hours to complete.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course. He experienced some mild postoperative pain, which was managed effectively with narcotic medication. Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during his postoperative course. However, he did develop mild postoperative nausea and vomiting, which responded well to antiemetic medication.

Follow-up:

The patient will follow up with his surgeon in 2 weeks to check the wound and remove the sutures. He will also follow up with his primary care physician to monitor his chronic conditions and adjust his medications as needed.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, this patient with a history of lung cancer, hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes mellitus underwent a successful lobectomy with a mediastinal lymph node dissection. His follow-up care will include monitoring by both his surgeon and primary care physician.
