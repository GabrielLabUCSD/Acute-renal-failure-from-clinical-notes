Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has a history of smoking and alcohol use. PMH: Hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and depression. The patient has tried physical therapy and pain management with mixed success. The patient is currently taking ASA and celecoxib without relief. The patient is allergic to PCN and has had an adverse reaction to sulfonamides. The patient"s BMI is 32.

The patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion with decompression on January 12, 2023. The patient"s postoperative course was uneventful, with minimal pain and nausea. The patient was discharged on postoperative day 3 with a prescription for oxycodone and instructions to continue celecoxib.

Follow-up appointment on January 26, 2023, revealed a well-healed incision with no signs of infection or dehiscence. The patient reported minimal back pain and improved mobility. The patient was advised to continue physical therapy and take ASA and oxycodone as needed.
