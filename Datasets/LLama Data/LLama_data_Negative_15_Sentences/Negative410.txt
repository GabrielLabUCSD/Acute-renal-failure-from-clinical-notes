Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presented to the clinic with a history of chronic low back pain and bilateral leg pain. The patient has been experiencing worsening pain over the past year, with pain radiating from the lower back to the buttocks and down the legs. The patient has tried physical therapy, chiropractic care, and medication without significant relief.

The patient has a history of smoking, with a pack-year history of 20. The patient has also been diagnosed with hypertension and hyperlipidemia. The patient"s family history is significant for cardiovascular disease.

On examination, the patient has limited mobility in the lower extremities and positive straight leg raise test. The patient"s neurological examination shows decreased sensation in the L5 and S1 dermatomes.

Imaging studies, including an MRI, reveal a large L4-L5 disc extrusion and spinal stenosis. The patient has been recommended for surgical intervention, including a laparoscopic decompression and fusion.

The patient"s postoperative course was uncomplicated. The patient was able to ambulate independently on their first postoperative day and had adequate pain control. The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 3.

Follow-up appointments at 2 weeks and 6 weeks showed adequate wound healing, and the patient reported significant improvement in pain and function. At 6 months, the patient was able to return to work without restrictions.

There were no complications during the postoperative course, including no infections, no deep vein thrombosis, and no pneumonia.

The patient was last seen in clinic 1 year after surgery, and the patient reported sustained improvement in pain and function. The patient was able to ambulate without assistance and had full range of motion in the lower extremities.

Conclusion:

Overall, this patient had a successful surgical outcome with no complications and significant improvement in pain and function. The patient"s history of smoking and hypertension were carefully monitored and controlled throughout the postoperative course.
