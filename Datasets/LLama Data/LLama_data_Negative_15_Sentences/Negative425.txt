Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and pain medication without significant relief. Imaging studies reveal a large L4-L5 disc herniation and spinal stenosis. The patient is scheduled for a L4-L5 decompression and fusion surgery.

Preoperative Medications:
The patient takes ibuprofen 800mg every 8 hours and oxycodone 10mg every 4-6 hours for pain management.

Preoperative Lab Results:
The patient's complete blood count and electrolytes are within normal limits. His blood type is A+.

Allergies:
The patient is allergic to penicillin, cephalosporins, and sulfa medications.

Medical History:
The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. He had a myocardial infarction 5 years ago and underwent a coronary artery bypass graft. He has also had a history of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease and uses an inhaler as needed.

Family History:
The patient's father died of a myocardial infarction at the age of 60. His mother has a history of osteoporosis.

Social History:
The patient is a retired construction worker and has a history of heavy smoking and alcohol use. He quit smoking 10 years ago and drinks occasionally. He lives with his wife and has two adult children.

Postoperative Course:
The patient had a successful L4-L5 decompression and fusion surgery. He had a thoracolumbar approach with instrumentation. His postoperative recovery was uneventful, and he was discharged home on postoperative day 5. He was advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for 6 weeks.

Complications:
The patient did not experience any complications during his postoperative course.

Follow-up:
The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon at 2 weeks and 6 weeks postoperatively. At 2 weeks, he reported some discomfort at the incision site, which was managed with pain medication. At 6 weeks, he reported significant improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy. He was allowed to gradually resume his activities.
