Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male, who presents with a history of chronic back pain and bilateral leg pain. He has been diagnosed with degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He is a candidate for a lumbar fusion surgery.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. He has no history of bleeding disorders or previous surgical complications. He is a non-smoker and drinks alcohol occasionally.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery uneventfully. His postoperative course was unremarkable, and he was discharged home on postoperative day 3. He followed up with his surgeon and primary care physician regularly and reported significant improvement in his pain.

At 6 weeks postoperative, the patient developed a wound infection, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some postoperative numbness and tingling in his legs, which gradually improved over time.

At 3 months postoperative, the patient reported a significant improvement in his pain and functional abilities. He was able to walk longer distances and perform daily activities with less discomfort.

At 6 months postoperative, the patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon, who reported that the fusion was solid and the patient was doing well. The patient continued to experience some numbness and tingling in his legs, but it was mild and did not affect his daily activities.

At 1 year postoperative, the patient reported a significant improvement in his overall health and quality of life. He was able to perform daily activities without pain and had returned to his preoperative level of functioning.

In summary, the patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery and had an unremarkable postoperative course, except for a wound infection and some postoperative numbness and tingling in his legs. He had a successful outcome and significant improvement in his pain and functional abilities at 1 year postoperative.
