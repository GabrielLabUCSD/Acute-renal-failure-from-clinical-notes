Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and medication without significant relief. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he is a current smoker. He denies any previous surgeries or significant medical history.

The patient underwent a lumbar decompression and fusion surgery. The surgery went well, and he was extubated in the operating room. He was transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) for recovery.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course. He was able to move his legs and feet immediately after surgery, and he had good muscle strength. He did experience some postoperative nausea and vomiting, which were managed with antiemetics. He had a patent airway and stable vital signs throughout his recovery. He was able to drink fluids on the first postoperative day and started on a soft diet the following day.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any complications during his postoperative course. He did not develop any signs of infection, such as fever, tachycardia, or purulent drainage. He did not have any respiratory or cardiac complications during his recovery. He did not experience any new neurological symptoms or deficits.

Discharge:

The patient was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 4. He was able to ambulate independently and had minimal pain managed with oral analgesics. He was instructed to continue with outpatient physical therapy to improve his strength and flexibility. He was also advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for the next 6 weeks.

Follow-up:

The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon 2 weeks after discharge. He reported minimal pain and was able to walk without assistance. He had some minor numbness and tingling in his legs, which was expected and will resolve over time. The surgeon was pleased with his progress and recommended continued physical therapy and home exercises.

In conclusion, the patient tolerated the surgery well and had a smooth postoperative course. He did not experience any significant complications during his recovery and was discharged from the hospital within a few days. He will continue with outpatient physical therapy to optimize his outcome.
