Patient Summary:

This patient is a 45-year-old male who presents for elective surgery. He has a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. The patient has a history of smoking but quit 5 years ago. He denies any respiratory symptoms and has normal spirometry results. The patient has a history of OSA and uses a CPAP machine nightly. His sleep study showed a moderate OSA with an AHI of 16. The patient has no history of cardiovascular disease, and his stress test showed normal results. His BMI is 35 kg/m2, and he has no history of previous abdominal surgery.

The patient's surgical plan is to undergo a laparoscopic sleeve gastrectomy. The patient understands the risks and benefits of the procedure and has realistic expectations about weight loss and improvements in health. Preoperative workup includes an upper GI series, which showed no evidence of ulcers or obstruction, and a colonoscopy that was negative for polyps or malignancy. The patient's blood work showed normal liver and kidney function.

Postoperative course:
The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He experienced some nausea and vomiting on the first postoperative day but was managed with antiemetics. He had minimal pain, which was well controlled with PCA morphine. The patient was able to tolerate sips of water on the second postoperative day and progressed to a full liquid diet by the fourth postoperative day. The patient was discharged home on the fifth postoperative day with instructions to continue a liquid diet for two weeks and then gradually transition to a pureed diet.

Complications:
The patient developed a wound infection on the seventh postoperative day, which was managed with antibiotics and local wound care. He also developed a seroma at the surgical site, which resolved on its own with conservative management. There were no respiratory or cardiac complications during the postoperative period. At two months follow-up, the patient had lost 20 pounds and reported improvement in his comorbidities.
