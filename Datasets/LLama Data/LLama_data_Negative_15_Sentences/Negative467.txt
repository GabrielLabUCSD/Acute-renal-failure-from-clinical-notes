patient summary

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and limited mobility. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medications, without significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he is currently taking medications for these conditions. He is a former smoker and has a history of moderate alcohol consumption.

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative evaluation, which included blood work, imaging studies, and a cardiac stress test. All results were within normal limits, except for mildly elevated liver enzymes. The patient was cleared for surgery by his primary care physician and anesthesiologist.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery, which was performed under general anesthesia. The surgery was uneventful, and the patient was transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit for recovery.

During the postoperative period, the patient experienced some expected discomfort, nausea, and vomiting. He was managed with pain medications, antiemetics, and fluids. He was able to ambulate independently and had a stable vital signs.

On the second postoperative day, the patient developed a fever of 101.3°F (38.5°C) and complained of increased pain in his surgical site. He was evaluated by his surgeon, who diagnosed a surgical site infection (SSI) and started him on antibiotics. The patient's fever resolved within 24 hours, and he continued to recover uneventfully.

The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 5, with instructions to continue his pain medications and wound care. He was also referred to physical therapy for rehabilitation and strengthening exercises.

The patient followed up with his surgeon 2 weeks after discharge and reported significant improvement in his pain and mobility. He was able to walk short distances without assistance and was working on his physical therapy exercises regularly.

The patient's postoperative course was otherwise uncomplicated, and he did not experience any further complications.

In conclusion, this patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery and had an uncomplicated postoperative course, except for a surgical site infection that was effectively managed with antibiotics.
