Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a 2-year history of persistent lower back pain. The pain is located in the lumbar region and radiates down to the left leg, with a severity of 8/10. The patient has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and pain medication, without significant relief.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and is currently taking medications for these conditions. They have no known allergies to medications and have never had a previous surgical procedure.

The patient is a current smoker and has a history of alcohol use, but denies any recent alcohol consumption. They work as a laborer and have been unable to perform their job duties due to the back pain.

The patient's physical examination reveals a limited range of motion in the lumbar spine, with tenderness to palpation over the lumbar facets. The straight leg raise test is positive at 40 degrees.

Imaging studies, including an MRI of the lumbar spine, reveal a L4-L5 disc herniation with mild spinal stenosis.

The patient has decided to undergo a lumbar discectomy and fusion surgery to alleviate the pain and improve their quality of life.

Postoperatively, the patient will be monitored closely for signs of neurological deficits, infection, and hardware failure. They will be encouraged to ambulate early and participate in physical therapy to regain strength and mobility.

The patient will be discharged home with a thoracolumbar brace and will follow up with the surgeon at 2 weeks and 6 weeks post-op.

The patient's postoperative course is expected to be uneventful, with no anticipated complications. They will be advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for the first 6 weeks after surgery.

The patient has been counseled on the potential risks and benefits of the procedure, including the risk of infection, nerve damage, and blood clots. They have also been advised of the importance of follow-up care and compliance with physical therapy.

In conclusion, the patient's medical history, physical examination findings, and imaging studies all support the diagnosis of a L4-L5 disc herniation with mild spinal stenosis. The patient has decided to undergo a lumbar discectomy and fusion surgery to alleviate their symptoms and improve their quality of life. A thorough postoperative plan has been outlined to ensure a smooth recovery and minimize the risk of complications.
