Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents for elective surgery. He has a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. He previously had a myocardial infarction 10 years ago and has been compliant with medications and lifestyle modifications since then. He has also been diagnosed with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) and uses an inhaler as needed. The patient has a history of lower back pain and has been smoking one pack per day for the past 20 years.

Preoperative Evaluation:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative evaluation, which included a physical examination, electrocardiogram, echocardiogram, and laboratory tests. His blood work showed mild anemia and elevated liver enzymes, which were attributed to his history of alcohol consumption. The patient's spirometry showed a forced expiratory volume in 1 second (FEV1) of 60% predicted, indicating moderate COPD. His echocardiogram showed a left ventricular ejection fraction of 50%.

Surgical Procedure:

The patient underwent a laparoscopic cholecystectomy for symptomatic gallstones. The procedure was performed uneventfully, and the patient was transferred to the postanesthesia care unit (PACU) for recovery.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course and was able to tolerate oral pain medication. He experienced some nausea and vomiting on the first postoperative day, which were managed with antiemetics. He also had some mild respiratory distress, which was managed with supplemental oxygen and bronchodilators. The patient was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 4.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during his postoperative course. However, he did develop a minor wound infection, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some constipation, which was managed with laxatives and stool softeners. The patient followed up with his surgeon and primary care physician within a week of discharge, and they both reported that his recovery was progressing well.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent a successful laparoscopic cholecystectomy and had a smooth postoperative course. He experienced some minor complications, including a wound infection and constipation, but they were managed effectively. The patient's history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and COPD was well-controlled during his hospitalization, and he was discharged home without any complications.
