Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative joint disease. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He is a candidate for lumbar fusion surgery to address his symptoms.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. He is a former smoker and has a history of mild obstructive sleep apnea.

The patient has no allergies and is taking no medications that may interact with anesthesia. He denies any history of bleeding disorders or previous anesthesia complications.

On physical examination, the patient has limited mobility in his lower back and left lower extremity, with a notable limp. His skin is warm and dry, with no signs of infection.

Preoperative lab work reveals a normal complete blood count, electrolyte panel, and coagulation studies. His creatinine kinase level is mildly elevated, consistent with his muscle strain.

The patient underwent lumbar fusion surgery under general anesthesia without any complications. His postoperative course was uneventful, with adequate pain management and no nausea or vomiting.

On the first postoperative day, the patient developed a mild fever, which responded promptly to antipyretic medication. He also experienced some postoperative nausea, which was managed with antiemetics.

The patient's wound healed well, with no signs of infection or dehiscence. He was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 4 and followed up with his surgeon in the clinic 2 weeks later.

At the 2-week follow-up, the patient reported significant improvement in his back pain and mobility. He was able to ambulate without assistance and had resumed some of his daily activities.

The patient's postoperative imaging studies demonstrated successful fusion of the lumbar spine. He will continue to follow up with his surgeon for further evaluation and management of his condition.

In summary, this patient underwent lumbar fusion surgery and had an uncomplicated postoperative course, with effective pain management and no major complications. His wound healed well, and he demonstrated significant improvement in his symptoms at the 2-week follow-up.
