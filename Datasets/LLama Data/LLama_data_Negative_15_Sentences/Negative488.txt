Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient has a history of smoking, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia. He has no known allergies and is taking no medications at present.

The patient underwent a lumbar decompression surgery three days ago. The surgery was uneventful, and the patient had a smooth postoperative course. He was managed with adequate analgesia and was able to mobilize independently on the first postoperative day. He did not have any complications during his hospital stay.

The patient was discharged home on the third postoperative day with instructions to continue his pain medication and to follow up with his surgeon in two weeks. He was also advised to restrictions on heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for the next six weeks.

The patient followed up with his surgeon as planned and reported significant improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy. He was able to gradually wean off his pain medication and resume his normal activities. He did not have any complications or recurrences of his symptoms during the follow-up period.

The patient is currently doing well and has returned to his usual daily activities. He has reported no new symptoms or concerns. He is satisfied with the outcome of the surgery and has expressed gratitude for the care provided.
