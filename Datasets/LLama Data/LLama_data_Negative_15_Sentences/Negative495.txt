Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and pain medication, without significant relief. The patient is a candidate for spinal fusion surgery to address his chronic pain and improve his quality of life.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative workup, including lab tests, imaging studies, and a cardiology evaluation. His blood work showed mild anemia and elevated inflammatory markers, consistent with his chronic pain. Imaging studies demonstrated multilevel degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis. The patient's cardiology evaluation was normal, and he was cleared for surgery.

Surgical Procedure:

The patient underwent a minimally invasive spinal fusion procedure, involving the insertion of a lumbar interbody fusion cage and instrumentation. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient tolerated it well.

Postoperative Course:

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful. He experienced some expected postoperative pain, which was managed effectively with pain medication. The patient was ambulating and breathing deeply within the first 24 hours after surgery. He was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 3 and was able to return to work 6 weeks after surgery.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during his postoperative course. He developed mild wound tenderness and some numbness in his left leg, which gradually resolved over time. The patient did not require any additional surgical interventions and did not experience any other complications.

Conclusion:

Overall, the patient had a successful outcome after spinal fusion surgery. His chronic back pain improved significantly, and he was able to return to work and resume his normal activities. The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and he did not experience any significant complications.
