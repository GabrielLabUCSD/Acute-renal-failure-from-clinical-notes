Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has been smoke-free for 5 years and has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. The patient has tried physical therapy and conservative management without significant relief. The patient is planing to undergo a lumbar fusion surgery.

The patient's surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair. The patient has no known allergies and is currently taking aspirin, lisinopril, and atorvastatin. The patient will be admitted on the day of surgery and is expected to stay for 3-4 days postoperatively.

The patient's postoperative course has been uneventful, with adequate pain management and no immediate complications. The patient was able to ambulate and perform deep breathing exercises without difficulty. The patient's wound appeared clean and well-healed during the follow-up appointment.

However, on the fifth postoperative day, the patient developed a fever and complained of increased pain in the surgical site. Blood cultures were drawn, and the patient was started on broad-spectrum antibiotics for suspected surgical site infection. The patient's fever resolved, and the patient was discharged home with a course of antibiotics.

Follow-up appointments showed no evidence of further infection or complications. The patient's pain and functional status improved significantly over time, and the patient was able to return to work and normal activities within 6-8 weeks after surgery.
