Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents for elective surgery. He has a medical history significant for hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He has been smoking one pack of cigarettes per day for the past 20 years. He denies any respiratory symptoms and has never had a respiratory infection. He has no allergies. He drinks moderately and denies any drug use. His family history is significant for coronary artery disease in his father and brother. He has had no previous surgeries. His current medications include lisinopril, simvastatin, and omeprazole. His last liver function test showed elevated liver enzymes. He has no history of bleeding disorders or previous blood transfusions. His platelet count and INR are within normal limits. He denies any recent weight loss or gain. He has no history of sleep apnea or renal disease. His current BMI is 32.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful surgery and was extubated in the operating room. He was transferred to the postanesthesia care unit (PACU) and monitored for several hours before being discharged to the ward. He was started on intravenous patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) for pain management. He experienced minimal postoperative nausea and vomiting and was treated with ondansetron. He had a satisfactory pain score and was able to ambulate and eat normally. He did not require blood transfusions.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any complications during his hospital stay. He did not develop any pneumonia or respiratory failure. His blood sugar levels were well controlled and he did not develop any signs of wound infection. He was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 5 and followed up with his surgeon in the clinic 2 weeks later.
