Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has been experiencing progressive worsening of symptoms over the past year, with pain and numbness in his left leg and foot, and difficulty ambulating. Physical examination reveals decreased sensation in the L5 and S1 dermatomes, and weakness in the left tibialis anterior muscle. MRI scan shows a large central herniated disc at L4-L5, with severe spinal stenosis and mild degenerative spondylolisthesis.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and is currently taking lisinopril and atorvastatin. He has no known allergies and has never had a surgical procedure. He is a former smoker, but quit 10 years ago. He denies any recent significant illnesses or injuries.

The patient"s postoperative course was uneventful, with no complications. He was discharged home on postoperative day 2, with instructions to rest and avoid heavy lifting or bending for 6 weeks. He was prescribed hydrocodone for pain management and instructed to follow up with his surgeon in 2 weeks.

On follow-up, the patient reported significant improvement in his pain and numbness, with some residual weakness in his left leg. He was able to ambulate without assistance and had returned to his normal activities. Physical examination showed improved sensation in the L5 and S1 dermatomes, and strength in the left tibialis anterior muscle had returned. The patient was pleased with the outcome of his surgery and was advised to continue with physical therapy to further improve his strength and mobility.

The patient"s postoperative recovery was otherwise uncomplicated, without any evidence of infection, nerve damage, or other complications. He was advised to continue with his medications and follow up with his surgeon as needed.
