Patient Summary: 

The patient is a 65-year-old male with a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin dependent diabetes mellitus. He has a history of smoking but quit 10 years ago. He has no known allergies. He takes several medications including lisinopril, atorvastatin, metformin, and aspirin. 

He is scheduled for an elective laparoscopic cholecystectomy. He had a coronary artery bypass graft 10 years ago and has been doing well since then. He has been complaining of worsening right upper quadrant abdominal pain for the past 6 months. He has tried multiple medications for this pain but they have not been effective. He had a CT scan that showed multiple stones in his gallbladder and a thickened gallbladder wall. 

He is otherwise healthy and active and walks 2 miles every day. He denies any recent weight loss, fever, or chills. He has no family history of gallbladder disease. 

Postoperative Course: 

The patient had an uneventful surgery and was extubated in the operating room. He had some nausea and vomiting in the recovery room but this resolved with medication. He was transferred to the floor and had a hydromorphone PCA for pain control. He had some minor sedation-related confusion but this resolved by the next morning. On the first postoperative day, he had some abdominal discomfort and was given acetaminophen and ibuprofen. He was able to eat solid food on the second postoperative day. On the third postoperative day, he developed a leak from one of his surgical sites and had to have it repaired. He was discharged on the fourth postoperative day with a prescription for hydrocodone-acetaminophen for pain control. 

Complications: 

The patient developed a leak from one of his surgical sites on the third postoperative day. This was repaired with alocal anesthetic in clinic. He did not have any other complications during his hospital stay. 

Follow-up: 

The patient was seen in clinic 2 weeks after discharge. He was doing well and had minimal pain. He was able to eat and drink without any issues. His incision sites were healing well. He was told to continue his medications and follow up in 6 weeks for further evaluation.
