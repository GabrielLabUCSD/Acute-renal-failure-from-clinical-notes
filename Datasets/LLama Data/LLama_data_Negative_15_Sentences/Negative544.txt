Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has tried conservative management including physical therapy and medications without significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. The patient is a current smoker and has a history of alcohol use disorder. The patient has no known allergies.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient underwent preoperative workup which included blood work, urinalysis, and imaging studies. The patient's blood work revealed anemia and elevated liver enzymes. The patient's urinalysis was positive for proteinuria and blood. The patient's imaging studies showed multilevel degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis.

Operative Course:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion surgery. The surgery included a two-level instrumented fusion with decompression of the spinal cord and nerve roots. The patient tolerated the procedure well and had no intraoperative complications.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course. The patient experienced some expected postsurgical pain and discomfort which was managed with pain medication. The patient had no respiratory or cardiac complications. The patient ambulated independently on postoperative day one and was able to perform basic activities of daily living without assistance.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any acute complications during the postoperative period. However, the patient did develop a wound infection which was managed with antibiotics. The patient also experienced some postoperative constipation which was treated with laxatives and stool softeners.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion surgery and had a smooth postoperative course. The patient experienced some expected postsurgical pain and discomfort which was managed with pain medication. The patient did not experience any acute complications during the postoperative period but did develop a wound infection and postoperative constipation which were managed appropriately. The patient was able to ambulate independently and perform basic activities of daily living without assistance.
