Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has been experiencing progressive worsening of symptoms over the past year, with limitations in ambulation and activities of daily living. The patient has a history of smoking and is currently taking naproxen and pregabalin for pain management.

Preoperative Diagnosis:

* Degenerative disc disease at L4-L5 and L5-S1
* Spondylolisthesis at L5-S1
* Left L5 nerve root compression

Proposed Surgery:

* L4-L5 and L5-S1 posterior lumbar fusion with instrumentation
* L5-S1 decompression

Postoperative Course:

The patient tolerated the procedure well and was extubated in the operating room. He was transferred to the intensive care unit for postoperative care. On the first postoperative day, the patient complained of some nausea and vomiting, which was managed with antiemetics. He also experienced some discomfort at the incision site, which was managed with pain medication.

On the second postoperative day, the patient had a temperature of 101.3°F and was started on antibiotics for possible surgical site infection. His white blood cell count was 12,000/mm3. He also developed a left posterior shoulder pain, which was managed with nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs.

On the third postoperative day, the patient's temperature was down to 99.6°F, and his white blood cell count was 10,000/mm3. He was able to ambulate with assistance and had improved pain control. The patient was transferred to the ward on this day.

On the fifth postoperative day, the patient was able to ambulate independently without assistance and was discharged home with a prescription for pain medication and instructions for postoperative care.

Complications:

* Surgical site infection (treated with antibiotics)
* Postoperative nausea and vomiting (managed with antiemetics)
* Left posterior shoulder pain (managed with nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs)
