Patient Summary:

The patient, a 45-year-old male, presents for surgery with a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He has a history of smoking but quit 5 years ago. He has no known allergies and takes no medications. The patient has a history of upper respiratory tract infections and has had multiple sinus surgeries in the past. He also has a history of degenerative joint disease and has undergone multiple joint replacements. The patient's most recent surgery was a total knee replacement 3 years ago.

The patient's current surgery is planned as a laparoscopic cholecystectomy for symptomatic gallstones. The patient is otherwise healthy and does not have any acute medical issues. His blood work and imaging studies are within normal limits.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful surgery and was transferred to the recovery room in stable condition. He experienced some postsurgical pain, nausea, and vomiting, which were managed with analgesics and antiemetics. The patient's blood pressure and tissue oxygenation remained stable throughout the postoperative period.

The patient was able to tolerate oral intake and had a steady recovery. He was discharged from the hospital on the second postoperative day with instructions to follow up with his surgeon in 2 weeks.

Complications:

The patient developed a wound infection on the fifth postoperative day, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some respiratory symptoms, including cough and shortness of breath, which were managed with bronchodilators and corticosteroids. The patient did not have any other complications during his postoperative course.

Conclusion:

The patient had a successful laparoscopic cholecystectomy and an uneventful postoperative course, except for a wound infection and some respiratory symptoms. He was discharged from the hospital on the second postoperative day and followed up with his surgeon 2 weeks later. The patient's postoperative course was remarkable for the absence of acute renal failure, a common complication in patients with a history of gallstones.
