Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and pain management, without significant relief. The patient is planning to undergo a lumbar fusion surgery to alleviate his symptoms.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. He is a non-smoker and denies any relevant allergies. His family history is unremarkable.

The patient's surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair. He has no history of previous back surgeries.

The patient's current medications include atorvastatin, lisinopril, and oxycodone. He denies any medication allergies.

The patient's review of systems is positive for persistent lower back pain, radiating leg pain, and numbness in his feet. He also reports occasional bilateral knee pain.

The patient's Physical exam reveals a straight back, no kyphosis or scoliosis, and full range of motion in his hips and lower extremities. His muscle strength is 5/5 throughout.

The patient's lumbar spine x-rays show multilevel degenerative disc disease, spondylosis, and a small annular tear at L4-L5.

The patient's MRI reveals a large central disc herniation at L4-L5 and moderate left neural foraminal stenosis.

The patient's postoperative course is expected to include a hospital stay of 3-4 days. He will require assistance with basic activities of daily living for 2-3 weeks after surgery.

The patient will have a follow-up appointment with his surgeon 2 weeks after surgery to check on his progress and remove sutures.

The patient will also have physical therapy appointments starting 2 weeks after surgery to help him regain strength and mobility in his lower back.

Complications that the patient may be at risk for include wound infection, nerve damage, and blood clots.

The patient will be closely monitored for signs of respiratory depression and urinary retention.

The patient's pain will be managed with a PCA pump and oral pain medication as needed.

The patient's family will be instructed on how to assist him with basic activities of daily living during his recovery.

The patient will be advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for at least 6 weeks after surgery.

The patient will be given a brace to wear for additional support during his postoperative recovery.
