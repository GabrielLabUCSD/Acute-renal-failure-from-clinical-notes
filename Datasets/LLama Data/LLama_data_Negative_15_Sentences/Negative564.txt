Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male, 5’9, 190 pounds, with a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus (NIDDM). He has been a smoker for 30 years but quit 2 years ago. He has no known allergies. He denies any recent illnesses or injuries.

On the fourth postoperative day, the patient’s incision site was dry, and he was able to eat a soft diet. He was discharged home with instructions to continue his pain medication and follow up with his surgeon in 1 week.
The patient had a postoperative follow-up appointment 1 week after discharge. He reported minimal pain and was able to eat a regular diet. His incision site was healing well. The patient was advised to continue his pain medication and follow up with his surgeon in 2 weeks.
