Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male with a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He has a history of smoking but quit 5 years ago. He has no known allergies and takes no medications. He presents with chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. Physical examination reveals decreased sensation in the L5-S1 dermatome and weakness in the left leg. Imaging studies show a herniated disc at L5-S1.

The patient underwent a lumbar discectomy and fusion. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia and took 2 hours. He had a uneventful postoperative course and was discharged on the third day.

Following discharge, he experienced some pain and discomfort at the surgical site which was managed with pain medication. He also had some constipation which was treated with laxatives.

On follow-up appointment, patient reported improvement in his symptoms and was able to walk without assistance. He also reported a significant decrease in his back pain and left leg radiculopathy.

However, patient developed a wound infection which was treated with antibiotics. He also had some urinary retention which was managed with catheterization.

Patient's postoperative course was otherwise uneventful and he was able to return to work after 6 weeks.

No acute renal failure was observed during the patient's postoperative course.

The patient's overall outcome was successful, with significant improvement of his symptoms and return to his normal activities.
