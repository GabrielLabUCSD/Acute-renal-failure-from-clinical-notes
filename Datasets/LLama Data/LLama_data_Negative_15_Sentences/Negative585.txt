Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has been diagnosed with a lumbar disc herniation and has failed conservative management. The patient is scheduled for a lumbar discectomy.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and is currently taking medications for these conditions. He has no history of bleeding disorders or allergies to anesthesia.

The patient has a history of smoking, but has quit within the past year. He drinks alcohol occasionally, but denies any recent excessive alcohol consumption.

The patient's surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair. He has no history of previous back surgeries.

The patient's family history is significant for his father having a history of coronary artery disease.

The patient's current medications include atorvastatin, lisinopril, and omeprazole.

The patient's physical examination reveals decreased sensation in the L5 and S1 dermatomes, and decreased strength in the left leg. His straight leg raise test is positive at 40 degrees.

The patient's postoperative course is uncomplicated. He experiences some pain and discomfort, but it is well managed with pain medication. He ambulates without assistance on the first postoperative day and is able to climb stairs independently by the second postoperative day.

The patient is discharged on the third postoperative day without any complications. He is advised to continue his medications as prescribed and to follow up with his surgeon in two weeks.

The patient follows up with his surgeon as directed and reports significant improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy. He is able to return to his normal activities without any restrictions.

The patient's follow-up examination reveals improved sensation in the L5 and S1 dermatomes and improved strength in the left leg. His straight leg raise test is now negative.

The patient's postoperative MRI scan shows a significant improvement in the disc herniation and no evidence of recurrent disc herniation or any other complications.
