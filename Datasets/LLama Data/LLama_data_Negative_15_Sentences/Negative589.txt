Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has been smoking for 20 years and has a history of hypertension. The patient has tried physical therapy and medication without significant relief. He is scheduled to undergo a lumbar fusion surgery.

Preoperative Course:

The patient's preoperative course was uneventful. He was optimized for surgery, and all his medical conditions were well-controlled. He was afebrile, and his blood work was normal.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He experienced some pain, and it was managed with pain medication. He was able to move around and sit up without any issues. He had a urinary catheter that was removed on the second postoperative day.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during his postoperative course. He had some nausea and vomiting that were managed with antiemetics. He did not have any respiratory or cardiovascular complications.

Discharge:

The patient was discharged home on the fifth postoperative day. He was advised to continue with pain medication and to gradually increase his mobilization. He was also advised to follow-up with his surgeon and primary care physician for further management.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the patient had a successful lumbar fusion surgery. He had a smooth preoperative and postoperative course, without any significant complications. The patient was discharged home, and he was advised to follow-up with his healthcare providers for further management.
