Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried physical therapy and medication without significant relief. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he smokes one pack of cigarettes per day. He denies any significant cardiovascular or respiratory history. He is allergic to penicillin and has a history of hypothyroidism. He has no history of previous surgeries.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery and was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 4. He experienced some nausea and vomiting on the first postoperative day, which was managed with medication. He had some pain in the wound area, which was controlled with pain medication. He ambulated independently on the second postoperative day and was able to eat a regular diet. He had a temporary decrease in urine output on the third postoperative day, but this resolved with fluid management. He had no complications during his hospital stay.

At the one-month follow-up appointment, the patient reported minimal back pain and significant improvement in his radiculopathy symptoms. He was able to walk without assistance and had returned to his normal activities. His hypertension and hyperlipidemia were well controlled on medication. He had stopped smoking and was advised to continue with a smoke-free lifestyle.

At the six-month follow-up appointment, the patient reported continued improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy symptoms. He had no significant complaints and was able to perform all activities without assistance. His hypertension and hyperlipidemia remained well controlled, and he had not experienced any complications related to his surgery.

Overall, the patient had a successful outcome from his lumbar fusion surgery, with significant improvement in his symptoms and minimal complications during his postoperative course. He was advised to continue with a smoke-free lifestyle and to follow up regularly with his surgeon to monitor his progress.
