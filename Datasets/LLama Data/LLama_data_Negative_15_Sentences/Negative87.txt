Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and decreased mobility. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient has a history of smoking but quit 5 years ago. He is currently taking aspirin and oxycodone for pain management.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery 10 years ago, which provided temporary relief but eventually, the pain returned. He also had a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, for which he takes medication regularly. The patient has no known allergies to medications and has not had any previous surgeries.

The patient's preoperative lab results revealed mild anemia and thrombocytopenia. However, these values were within acceptable limits, and the patient was deemed fit for surgery.

The patient underwent a thoracic spine fusion surgery, which lasted for 5 hours. The surgery was uneventful, and the patient had a smooth extubation. He was taken to the intensive care unit for close monitoring.

Postoperatively, the patient experienced some expected discomfort, which was managed with pain medication. He was able to sit up and take a few sips of water on the first day after surgery. The patient had some nausea and vomiting on the second postoperative day but tolerated oral medication well.

The patient's wound healed well, and there were no signs of infection. He was discharged from the hospital on the fifth postoperative day and advised to follow up with the surgeon and primary care physician.

During the follow-up appointment, the patient reported significant improvement in his back pain and mobility. He was able to walk short distances without assistance and could perform activities of daily living without much difficulty. The patient was also advised to continue with physical therapy and pain management medication as needed.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgery and postoperative course without any major complications. He benefited significantly from the surgery and was able to return to his normal activities with improved mobility and reduced pain.
