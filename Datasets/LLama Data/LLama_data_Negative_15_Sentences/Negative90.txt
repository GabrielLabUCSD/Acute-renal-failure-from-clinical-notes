Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and bilateral leg pain. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and medication without significant relief. He has a history of smoking but quit 5 years ago.

 His medical history includes hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and a history of non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. He had a myocardial infarction 10 years ago and has been compliant with his medications since. He denies any allergies.

 The patient has been thoroughly evaluated by a spine surgeon, who has recommended a lumbar fusion surgery. The patient understands the risks and benefits and has given informed consent.

The patient has been optimized preoperatively with medical clearance from his internist and cardiologist. His blood pressure and blood sugars have been well controlled. He has been instructed to stop aspirin and clopidogrel 7 days before surgery.

 On the day of surgery, the patient tolerated anesthesia well and had an uneventful intraoperative course. He had a lumbar fusion with instrumentation and bone grafting.

His postoperative course was complicated by a wound infection, which was treated with antibiotics. He experienced persistent pain and was managed with a multimodal pain regimen including narcotics, nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, and physical therapy.

The patient had a prolonged stay in the hospital due to the wound infection and required intravenous antibiotics for 6 weeks.

However, he did not require any further interventions and was eventually discharged home. He has since been following up with his surgeon and primary care physician regularly.

Despite the wound complication, the patient has shown significant improvement in his pain and mobility.

He is currently able to ambulate independently and has returned to his daily activities, including work.

The patient remains vigilant about his wound care and attends periodic follow-ups to ensure that the wound is healing properly.

In conclusion, this patient had a successful lumbar fusion surgery despite some postoperative complications. He has shown significant improvement in his pain and mobility and is gradually returning to his normal activities. He remains vigilant about his wound care and will continue to follow up regularly with his physicians.
