Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and medication without significant relief. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia and is currently taking medication for both conditions. The patient has no history of previous surgeries or significant medical complications.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery and was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 4. He experienced some expected postoperative pain and discomfort but was managing well with pain medication. He was advised to follow a strict rehabilitation protocol to ensure proper healing and prevent complications.

On postoperative day 7, the patient developed a fever and reported increased pain in the surgical site. He was evaluated by his surgeon and was found to have a surgical site infection. He was started on antibiotics and his pain management plan was adjusted. The patient remained hospitalized for further observation and monitoring.

On postoperative day 10, the patient had improved significantly and was discharged home. He continued to follow his rehabilitation protocol and had regular follow-up appointments with his surgeon.

The patient reported some persistent numbness and tingling in his left leg several weeks after surgery, but it gradually improved over time. He also experienced some mild, temporary weakness in his left leg but was able to ambulate without assistance.

The patient"s hypertension and hyperlipidemia were well controlled throughout the postoperative course. He did not experience any further complications and was satisfied with the outcome of his surgery.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgical outcome and made a full recovery. He was able to return to his normal activities without any significant limitations.
