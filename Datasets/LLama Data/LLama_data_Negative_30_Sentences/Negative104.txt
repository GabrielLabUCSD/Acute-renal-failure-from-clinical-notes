Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic low back pain and bilateral leg pain. He has been smoking for 20 years and has a history of hypertension. His surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair. He takes ibuprofen and oxycodone for pain management. He has tried physical therapy in the past without significant improvement. He also has a history of sleep apnea and uses a CPAP machine.

Pre-operative Work-up:

The patient underwent a thorough pre-operative work-up, which included:

* A thorough physical examination
* Lab tests, including a complete blood count, electrolytes, liver and kidney function tests, and coagulation studies
* Imaging studies, including an MRI of the lumbar spine and bilateral hips
* Anesthesia consultation
* Surgical consultation

Operative Course:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion surgery. The procedure was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient tolerated it well.

Post-operative Course:

The patient was transferred to the recovery room where he was closely monitored for any complications. He was started on IV pain medication and was later transitioned to oral pain medication as needed. He was also started on a prophylactic antibiotic regimen to prevent infection.

The patient experienced some post-operative discomfort, including pain, nausea, and constipation. These symptoms were managed with medication and resolved within a few days.

The patient was encouraged to ambulate early and often to prevent deep vein thrombosis and promote healing. He was also advised to follow a rehabilitation program that included physical therapy and exercises to strengthen his back muscles.

The patient was discharged from the hospital after a few days and was followed up in the clinic regularly. He reported significant improvement in his pain and mobility, and was able to return to work within a few weeks.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during his post-operative course. However, he did experience some minor side effects, including mild nausea and constipation, which were managed with medication and resolved within a few days.

Conclusion:

The patient tolerated the surgery well and had a successful outcome. He experienced some minor post-operative complications that were managed effectively. The patient was closely monitored and received appropriate medication and rehabilitation to promote healing and prevent further complications.
