Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and sciatica. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he is a current smoker. The patient has a history of Non-ST Segment Elevation Myocardial Infarction (NSTEMI) and has been compliant with his medications. He has no history of bleeding disorders or previous surgical procedures.

Preoperative Examination:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative examination, including an electrocardiogram (ECG), echocardiogram, and stress test, which showed no significant abnormalities. His blood work revealed a elevated white blood cell count and platelet count, which was attributed to his smoking history. The patient was optimize for surgery with optimization of his hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and smoking cessation.

Surgical Procedure:

The patient underwent a L4-L5 lumbar fusion with instrumentation. The procedure was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately 3 hours to complete. The patient had a uneventful intraoperative course and was transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) in stable condition.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a complicated postoperative course. He developed a wound infection on the third postoperative day, which was treated with broad-spectrum antibiotics. He also experienced significant postoperative pain, which was managed with a patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) pump. The patient had a difficult time mobilizing and was unable to ambulate independently. He required assistance with activities of daily living (ADLs) for the first week after surgery.

Complications:

The patient developed a seroma at the surgical site, which was managed with compression garments and elevation of the affected limb. He also experienced some degree of postoperative nausea and vomiting (PONV), which was controlled with antiemetics. The patient had a prolonged ileus, which was managed with a nasogastric tube and conservative management. He also developed a urinary tract infection (UTI), which was treated with antibiotics.

Recovery:

The patient's wound infection improved with antibiotics, and he was discharged from the hospital on the 10th postoperative day. He continued to experience some degree of pain, but it was managed with oral analgesics. The patient's seroma resolved with compression garments and elevation. He experienced some degree of numbness and weakness in his lower extremities, but it improved over time. The patient was able to ambulate independently and perform ADLs without assistance. He was advised to continue with smoking cessation and physical therapy to improve his mobility and reduce pain. The patient had a successful outcome and was satisfied with the surgical results.
