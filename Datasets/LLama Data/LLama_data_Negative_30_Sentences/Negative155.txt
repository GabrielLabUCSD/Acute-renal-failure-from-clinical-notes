Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has undergone multiple epidural injections and physical therapy without significant relief. He is currently smoking 1 pack of cigarettes per day and has a history of alcohol abuse. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, for which he takes medication. He denies any respiratory or cardiovascular problems. He has a history of previous abdominal surgery for a hernia repair.

On examination, the patient has restricted mobility in the lumbar spine and positive signs of nerve root compression. Straight leg raising test is positive on the left side. His sensory examination shows decreased sensation in the L5 and S1 dermatomes. Muscle strength is 4/5 in the left lower extremity.

Imaging studies reveal multi-level degenerative disc disease with spinal stenosis. MRI shows a large left-sided disc herniation at L4-L5, which is causing significant neural compression.

The patient underwent a left L4-L5 hemilaminectomy and discectomy. Intraoperative findings showed a large, hard, and adherent disc herniation that was pressing on the left L5 nerve root. The patient tolerated the procedure well and was extubated in the operating room.

Postoperative course:

 Day 1: The patient complains of mild pain, which is managed with scheduled pain medication. He ambulates with assistance and is able to move his knees and ankles.

 Day 2: The patient's pain is better controlled. He is able to sit on the edge of the bed and stand with assistance. He walks around the room with a walker.

 Day 3: The patient complains of mild nausea and vomits once. He denies any new neurological symptoms.

 Day 4: The patient's nausea and vomiting persist. He is unable to tolerate oral medications. He is started on IV fluids.

 Day 5: The patient's nausea and vomiting improve. He is able to tolerate sips of water and eat a small amount of soup. He is started on a clear liquid diet.

 Day 6: The patient is able to eat a full meal and tolerates it well. He ambulates around the room without assistance.

 Day 7: The patient is discharged home with a follow-up appointment in 2 days.

Complications:

* Postoperative nausea and vomiting (day 3-4)
* Inability to tolerate oral medications (day 4)

The patient did not experience any major complications such as infection, bleeding, or respiratory/cardiac problems. He had a successful postoperative course and was able to ambulate without assistance by day 6. The patient was discharged home with a follow-up appointment in 2 days.
