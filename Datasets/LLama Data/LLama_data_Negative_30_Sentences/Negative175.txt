Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and numbness in his legs. He has been diagnosed with spondylolisthesis and spinal stenosis. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and medication but has not found significant relief. He is now considering surgical intervention.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, for which he takes medication. He is a current smoker and has a history of alcohol use disorder, for which he has sought treatment.

The patient has had a previous cholecystectomy and appendectomy without complications.

On physical examination, the patient's vital signs are stable. He has limited mobility in his lower back and bilateral lower extremities. He has decreased sensation in his feet and diminished reflexes.

Imaging studies, including X-rays and MRI, confirm the diagnosis of spondylolisthesis and spinal stenosis.

The patient is scheduled for a posterior lumbar fusion with instrumentation. The surgical plan includes a posterior approach, bilateral decompression, and fusion with pedicle screws and rods.

The patient is at high risk for postoperative complications due to his history of smoking and alcohol use disorder. He is counseled on the importance of smoking cessation and strict wound care to minimize the risk of wound infection.

Postoperatively, the patient is closely monitored in the intensive care unit for the first 24 hours. He is then transferred to the surgical ward for continued recovery.

The patient experiences some postoperative pain, which is managed with pain medication. He also experiences some nausea and vomiting, which is treated with antiemetics.

The patient's wound heals uneventfully, and he is discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 7.

Following discharge, the patient attends regular follow-up appointments with his surgeon and physical therapist to monitor his progress and manage any postoperative complications.

Overall, the patient experiences a successful outcome from his surgical intervention and is able to return to his normal activities with improved pain and mobility.
