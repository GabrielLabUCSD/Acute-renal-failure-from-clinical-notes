Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and difficulty walking due to a herniated disc. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He has been recommended for surgery to treat his condition.

Medical History:

The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. He had a myocardial infarction three years ago and has been compliant with his medications and lifestyle modifications since then. He denies any history of bleeding disorders or previous surgeries.

Medications:

The patient is currently taking aspirin, lisinopril, atorvastatin, and metformin. He has no known allergies to medications.

Family History:

The patient's father died of a myocardial infarction at the age of 65. His mother has hypertension and hyperlipidemia.

Social History:

The patient is a retired smoker and denies any recent alcohol or illicit drug use. He lives alone but has a supportive family and social network.

Review of Systems:

The patient reports no significant changes in urination, bowel movements, or mood. He denies any recent weight loss or fever.

Physical Exam:

The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. His back examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lumbar spine with decreased range of motion. His neurological examination is normal.

Imaging Studies:

An MRI of the lumbar spine shows a large central disc herniation at L4-L5.

Laboratory Results:

The patient's complete blood count and electrolyte panel are within normal limits. His creatinine kinase level is mildly elevated.

Surgical Plan:

The patient is scheduled for a lumbar discectomy.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uncomplicated postoperative course. He was started on intravenous patient-controlled analgesia and later transitioned to oral pain medication. His pain was well-controlled, and he was able to ambulate independently within the first 24 hours. He experienced some nausea and vomiting on the first postoperative day but responded well to antiemetics. He was discharged home on the third postoperative day with a follow-up appointment in one week.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any complications during his hospital stay.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar discectomy and had an uncomplicated postoperative course. He was discharged home with proper wound care and pain management instructions. His follow-up appointment is scheduled in one week to monitor his progress and address any concerns or complications that may arise.
