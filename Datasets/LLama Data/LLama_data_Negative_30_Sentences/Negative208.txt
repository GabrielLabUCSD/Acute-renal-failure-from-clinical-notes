Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has a history of smoking and has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient has been diagnosed with degenerative disc disease and spondylolisthesis at L4-L5.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative workup, including laboratory tests, imaging studies, and consultations with a neurosurgeon and anesthesiologist. The patient's laboratory tests showed normal renal function, liver enzymes, and a low white blood cell count. The patient's imaging studies, including MRI and CT scans, confirmed the diagnosis of degenerative disc disease and spondylolisthesis at L4-L5. The patient's consultations with the neurosurgeon and anesthesiologist deemed the patient an appropriate candidate for surgical intervention.

Procedure:

The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 lumbar fusion with instrumentation. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient tolerated the procedure well.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. The patient was transferred to the recovery room, where vital signs were closely monitored. The patient experienced some postoperative pain, which was managed effectively with opioid medication. The patient was encouraged to ambulate and perform deep breathing exercises to prevent respiratory complications. The patient's wound site was clean and dry, with no signs of infection.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during the postoperative period. However, the patient did develop a mild case of postoperative nausea and vomiting, which was managed with antiemetics. The patient also experienced some constipation, which was relieved with laxatives.

Follow-up:

The patient had a follow-up appointment with the neurosurgeon one week after surgery. The patient was recovering well, and the wound site was healing properly. The patient was advised to continue pain management and physical therapy to improve mobility and strength. The patient was also advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for the next several weeks.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 lumbar fusion with instrumentation and had an uneventful postoperative course. The patient did not experience any major complications and was recovering well. The patient was advised to continue pain management and physical therapy and to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for the next several weeks.
