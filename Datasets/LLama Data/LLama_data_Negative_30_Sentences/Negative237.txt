Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has a history of smoking and hypertension. The patient has tried physical therapy and medication but has not had relief from symptoms. The patient is planning to undergo a lumbar fusion surgery.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient had a thorough preoperative workup, including an MRI of the lumbar spine, which showed a L4-L5 disc herniation and spinal stenosis. The patient also had a CT scan of the abdomen and pelvis, which showed a small stone in the left kidney. The patient had a cardiac evaluation, which showed normal cardiac function.

Intraoperative Course:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion surgery. Intraoperative fluids included 2 liters of lactated Ringer's solution and 1 gram of Ancef. The patient's blood pressure was maintained between 120-140 mmHg, and heart rate was maintained between 60-80 bpm.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. The patient was initially managed with an epidural catheter for pain control, which was removed on postoperative day 2. The patient was transitioned to oral pain medication and had adequate pain control. The patient was ambulatory on postoperative day 1 and was able to perform activities of daily living without assistance.

Complications:

The patient did not have any major complications during the postoperative course. However, the patient developed a mild cough on postoperative day 3, which was managed with over-the-counter cough medication. The patient also experienced some nausea and vomiting on postoperative day 4, which was managed with antiemetics.

Follow-up:

The patient had a follow-up appointment with the surgeon on postoperative day 7. The patient reported some residual back pain, but the incision site was well-healed. The patient was advised to continue with oral pain medication and to gradually resume activities. The patient was also referred to physical therapy for further management of back pain.

Overall, the patient had a successful lumbar fusion surgery and an uneventful postoperative course, with minimal complications.
