Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents for elective surgery. He has a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. The patient had a myocardial infarction three years ago and has been compliant with his medications and lifestyle modifications since then. He has a history of chronic lower back pain and bilateral knee osteoarthritis, for which he takes tramadol and celecoxib. The patient had a cholecystectomy ten years ago and has been asymptomatic since then.

The patient has a strong allergy to penicillin and has had an anaphylactic reaction in the past. He is allergic to sulfa medications and has a mild allergy to latex. The patient is an ex-smoker and stopped smoking three years ago. He drinks alcohol socially and in moderation.

The patient's physical examination is unremarkable, with the exception of mild swelling in his knees. His blood work reveals a normocytic anemia and mildly elevated liver enzymes. His serum creatinine level is 1.2 mg/dL. He has a negative urinalysis. An electrocardiogram and echocardiogram performed one week ago were normal.

The patient's postoperative course is uneventful. He wakes up from anesthesia without any complications and his vital signs are stable. He experiences some nausea and vomiting in the recovery room, which is treated with ondansetron. The patient's pain is well-controlled with tramadol and celecoxib. He is mobilized early and walks around the room without assistance. He is able to drink clear liquids two hours after surgery and advances to a regular diet the following day.

The patient experiences some constipation on the second postoperative day, which is treated with docusate sodium. He also develops a mild cough on the third postoperative day, which is thought to be related to an upper respiratory tract infection. This is treated with azithromycin.

The patient is discharged home on the fourth postoperative day in stable condition. At his one-week follow-up appointment, he reports minimal pain and is satisfied with his surgical outcome. He does not have any complications at one month, three months, or six months after surgery.

As for complications, the patient does not experience any acute renal failure, myocardial infarction, or stroke during his postoperative course. He does not require any blood transfusions or have any wound complications. There are no issues with his knee replacements or any signs of infection. The patient's course is uncomplicated, and he has a good outcome from his surgery.
