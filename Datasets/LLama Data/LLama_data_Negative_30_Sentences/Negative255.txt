Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has a history of smoking and alcohol use. The patient has tried physical therapy, chiropractic care, and pain management without significant relief. The patient has a history of degenerative disc disease, spondylolisthesis, and spinal stenosis.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery six months ago, which provided temporary relief but ultimately failed to alleviate the patient's symptoms. The patient has been informed that a revisional fusion surgery is necessary.

• Hemoglobin A1C of 7.5%
• Serum creatinine of 1.2 mg/dL
• Alanine transaminase of 25 U/L
• Aspartate transaminase of 30 U/L
• Alkaline phosphatase of 120 U/L
• Total bilirubin of 0.7 mg/dL

Preoperative Medications:

• Oxycodone 10 mg orally every 4-6 hours as needed for pain
• Gabapentin 300 mg orally twice a day
• Metformin 500 mg orally twice a day
• Atorvastatin 20 mg orally once a day
• Lisinopril 20 mg orally once a day
• Omeprazole 40 mg orally once a day
• Multivitamin orally once a day

Operative Course:

• The patient underwent a revisional lumbar fusion surgery under general anesthesia.
• Intraoperative fluids included lactated Ringer's solution and phenylephrine.
• The patient's blood pressure was maintained within normal limits throughout the procedure.
• The patient's temperature was maintained at 36.5°C (97.7°F) throughout the procedure.
• The patient had no postoperative complications.

Postoperative Course:

• The patient was transferred to the postanesthesia care unit (PACU) for observation.
• The patient experienced some nausea and vomiting in the PACU, which was managed with ondansetron.
• The patient's pain was managed with oxycodone and acetaminophen.
• The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 3 with a follow-up appointment in one week.

Complications:

• None.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent a successful revisional lumbar fusion surgery without any postoperative complications. The patient's pain was managed effectively with medication, and the patient was discharged home without any issues. Follow-up appointment was scheduled in one week to monitor the patient's progress and manage any potential complications.
