Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and bilateral leg pain. He has a history of degenerative disc disease, spondylolisthesis, and spinal stenosis. The patient has tried physical therapy and medication, but has not found sufficient relief. He is considering surgical intervention to address his symptoms.

The patient has no history of previous surgeries. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, but these conditions are well-controlled with medication. He is a non-smoker and drinks alcohol occasionally.

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative evaluation, which included blood work, imaging studies, and a cardiology consult. All results were within normal limits, and the patient was cleared for surgery.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery, which involved the insertion of a metal plate and screws to stabilize the spine. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient tolerated the procedure well.

Postoperatively, the patient experienced some pain and discomfort, which was managed with pain medication. He also experienced some constipation, which was treated with laxatives.

The patient was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 3, and he was able to ambulate independently. He was advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for the first 6 weeks after surgery.

During the postoperative period, the patient developed a wound infection at the incision site. This was treated with antibiotics, and the patient responded well to treatment.

The patient also developed a postoperative seroma, which was managed with compression garments and elevation of the affected limb.

At 6 weeks postoperative, the patient was seen in the clinic for a follow-up appointment. He reported significant improvement in his pain and mobility, and he was able to resume his normal activities.

At 12 weeks postoperative, the patient was seen again in the clinic, and he reported continued improvement in his symptoms. He was able to walk without assistance, and he had full range of motion in his back.

The patient did not experience any complications related to the surgery, such as nerve damage or urinary retention. He did not require any blood transfusions during or after the surgery.

In conclusion, the patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion surgery and experienced a straightforward postoperative course. He tolerated the procedure well, and he was able to ambulate independently within a few days of surgery.
