Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents for a routine laparoscopic cholecystectomy. He has a history of gallstones and symptomatic cholelithiasis, with persistent right upper quadrant pain and nausea. The patient has no known allergies and takes no medications. He is an experienced smoker with a 10-pack-year history, but quit smoking 5 years ago. His medical history is significant for hypertension and hyperlipidemia, for which he takes lifestyle modifications and medications, respectively. The patient denies any previous surgeries, significant illnesses, or hospitalizations. 

Family history is notable for a paternal grandfather who died from a myocardial infarction at age 60. The patient is married, works as an accountant, and has two adult children. He denies any recent travel, unusual exposures, or significant stressors. Review of systems reveals no weight loss, fever, chills, or night sweats. The patient has normal BMI and appetite. His exercise tolerance is moderate. He tolerates oral medications well, with no recent nausea or vomiting. He experiences occasional mild depression, for which he takes an SSRI, but reports no suicidal ideation or recent mood changes. 

Physical examination reveals a blood pressure of 140/80 mmHg and a heart rate of 78 beats per minute. Respiratory rate is 16 breaths per minute, with an oxygen saturation of 98% on room air. Body temperature is 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit. Head, eyes, ears, nose, and throat examination reveals no abnormalities. Cardiovascular, respiratory, and abdominal examination also reveals no abnormalities. The patient has a normal digital rectal examination. Neurological examination is significant for mild bilateral lower extremity paresthesias. Psychiatric evaluation reveals no evidence of acute psychosis or cognitive impairment. 

Laboratory results reveal a white blood cell count (WBC) of 10.2 K/uL, hemoglobin A1C of 6.8%, blood urea nitrogen (BUN) of 20 mg/dL, creatinine of 1.2 mg/dL, and a total bilirubin of 0.8 mg/dL. No imaging studies have been performed recently.

Plan:
The patient will undergo a laparoscopic cholecystectomy with an overnight stay in the hospital. The patient will receive general anesthesia, and the surgical team will consist of an attending surgeon, a resident, and an anesthesiologist. 

Postoperative Course:
The patient tolerated the procedure well and was extubated in the operating room. The patient was taken to the Post-Anesthesia Care Unit (PACU) for recovery, where he remained stable. He was transferred to the surgical floor and received pain management with patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) and adjunctive medications as needed. He tolerated oral hydration and a soft diet with no nausea or vomiting. The patient"s paresthesias improved over the next few days, and he was discharged home on postoperative day 2. 

Complications:
The patient did not experience any complications during his hospital stay. He was readmitted to the hospital 5 days after discharge with complaints of worsening right upper quadrant pain and fever. He was found to have a postoperative wound infection,which was treated with antibiotics. The patient was discharged home after a 3-day hospital stay, with complete resolution of symptoms.
