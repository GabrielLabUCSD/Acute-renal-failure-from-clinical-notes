Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has a history of smoking and has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medications, without significant relief. The patient has been diagnosed with a herniated disc and spinal stenosis.

Preoperative Course:

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery. The patient had a smooth intraoperative course and was transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit in stable condition.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a uneventful postoperative course. He experienced some pain and discomfort, which was managed with pain medication. The patient was able to ambulate and perform deep breathing exercises as instructed. The patient was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 3.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any acute complications during his hospital stay. However, he did develop a wound infection on postoperative day 7, which was managed with antibiotics and wound care. The patient was readmitted to the hospital for observation and treatment of the wound infection. He was discharged from the hospital after a 5-day stay.

Follow-Up:

The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon at 2 weeks post-discharge. At this visit, the wound infection was noted to be improving, and the patient was advised to continue with wound care and antibiotics. The patient was also advised to gradually resume his normal activities.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgery and a smooth postoperative course, except for the wound infection. The patient was managed appropriately for the complication and had a good outcome.
