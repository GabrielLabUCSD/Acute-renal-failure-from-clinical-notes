Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and bilateral leg pain. The pain has been persistent for the past 5 years and has been unresponsive to conservative management. The patient has a history of smoking and has a high body mass index (BMI).

Preoperative Diagnosis:

The patient has been diagnosed with degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis.

Operative Procedure:

The patient underwent a laparoscopic decompression and fusion of the lumbar spine.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He experienced some discomfort and pain in the immediate postoperative period, which was managed with pain medication. He was able to ambulate independently within the first 24 hours and was discharged from the hospital on the third postoperative day.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any complications during his postoperative course.

Follow-up:

The patient followed up with his surgeon at 2 weeks postoperative and reported significant improvement in his symptoms. He was able to walk without assistance and had a significant reduction in his pain levels.

At 6 weeks postoperative, the patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon and reported continued improvement in his symptoms. He was able to resume his normal activities and had no ongoing complications.

At 3 months postoperative, the patient had a final follow-up appointment with his surgeon and reported that his symptoms had fully resolved. He was able to return to his preoperative level of activity and had no ongoing pain or discomfort.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the patient underwent a laparoscopic decompression and fusion of the lumbar spine for degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis. He had an uneventful postoperative course and did not experience any complications. The patient had a successful outcome and was able to return to his preoperative level of activity.
