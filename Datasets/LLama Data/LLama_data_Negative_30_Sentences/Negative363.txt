Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has a history of smoking and has tried various conservative treatments including physical therapy and medication without significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. The patient works as a construction worker and has a history of heavy lifting.

Preoperative Evaluation:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative evaluation, including laboratory tests and imaging studies. The patient's white blood cell count, electrolytes, and liver function tests were within normal limits. The patient's creatinine kinase and troponin I levels were elevated, indicating possible muscle damage. The patient's chest X-ray and electrocardiogram were normal. The patient's MRI scan showed a large L4-L5 disc herniation with significant nerve root compression.

Surgical Procedure:

The patient underwent a L4-L5 discectomy and fusion surgery. The procedure was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient tolerated it well. The surgical site was stabilized with rods and screws.

Postoperative Course:

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful. The patient was able to ambulate on the first postoperative day and was discharged home on the second postoperative day. The patient was advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, and twisting for the first six weeks postoperatively. The patient was prescribed pain medication and muscle relaxants to control discomfort.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during his postoperative course. The patient did report some mild soreness at the surgical site, which was managed with pain medication. The patient's wound healed without any issues. The patient did not experience any infections, neurological deficits, or cardiovascular complications.

Follow-up:

The patient followed up with the surgeon at two weeks postoperatively. The patient reported significant improvement in his pain and was able to ambulate without assistance. The patient's incision site was well-healed, and the patient was advised to continue with his postoperative instructions. The patient was satisfied with the outcome of the surgery.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 discectomy and fusion surgery. The patient tolerated the procedure well and had an uneventful postoperative course. The patient did not experience any significant complications and reported significant improvement in his pain. The patient was satisfied with the outcome of the surgery, and the patient's postoperative recovery was uneventful.
