Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and bilateral leg pain. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient has a history of smoking, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia. He denies any previous surgeries or significant medical history.

Surgical History:

The patient is scheduled to undergo a lumbar decompression surgery to alleviate his chronic back pain.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful surgery and was taken to the recovery room in stable condition. He experienced some nausea and vomiting but was able to manage it with medication. He was able to ambulate independently on the first postoperative day and had minimal pain.

Complications:
The patient developed a wound infection on the third postoperative day, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some constipation but was able to manage it with laxatives. There were no other complications during his postoperative course.

Discharge and Follow-up:

The patient was discharged from the hospital on the fifth postoperative day with a follow-up appointment in two weeks. He was advised to continue taking his pain medication and to gradually increase his activity level over the next few weeks. Physical therapy was also recommended to help with his rehabilitation.

Current Status:

The patient is currently doing well and has minimal pain. He is adhering to his medication regimen and has been attending physical therapy sessions regularly. He has also been able to gradually resume his daily activities without any significant issues.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the patient underwent a successful lumbar decompression surgery and had an uneventful postoperative course. He developed a wound infection and constipation which were effectively managed with antibiotics and laxatives respectively. The patient is currently doing well and is following his postoperative instructions carefully. He is expected to make a full recovery over the next few weeks with continued physical therapy and medication.
