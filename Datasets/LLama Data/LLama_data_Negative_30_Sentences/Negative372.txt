Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and medication without significant relief. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and is currently taking atorvastatin and lisinopril. He is a former smoker and denies any recent alcohol or illicit drug use.

The patient's surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair. He has no known allergies to anesthesia or medications. His family history is significant for coronary artery disease and peripheral vascular disease.

On examination, the patient's vital signs are normal. He has mild tenderness to palpation over the lumbar spine and left lower extremity. His neurological examination shows decreased sensation to light touch in the L4 and L5 dermatomes. His muscle strength is 4/5 in the left lower extremity.

Imaging studies include an MRI of the lumbar spine, which shows a large central disc herniation at L4-L5 and moderate spinal stenosis. A left femoral nerve block was performed, which provided significant pain relief.

The patient's postoperative course was uneventful. He experienced some nausea and vomiting in the immediate postoperative period, which was managed with antiemetics. He was discharged from the hospital on the second postoperative day and was able to return to his regular activities within a week.

Follow-up appointments with the surgeon and primary care physician showed no evidence of complications. The patient's pain had significantly improved, and he was able to ambulate without assistance. His neurological examination showed improvement in sensation and muscle strength. The patient was started on physical therapy to improve his strength and flexibility.

After six months, the patient reported a recurrence of pain and numbness in his left lower extremity. An MRI showed a recurrent disc herniation at the same level. The patient underwent a second surgery, a minimally invasive decompression and fusion at L4-L5.

The patient's postoperative course was again uneventful. He experienced some discomfort and was managed with pain medication. He was discharged from the hospital on the third postoperative day and was able to return to his regular activities within two weeks.

Follow-up appointments showed improvement in the patient's pain and neurological symptoms. He was able to ambulate without assistance and had full recovery of sensation and muscle strength in his left lower extremity.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgical outcome and did not experience any complications. His postoperative course was uneventful, and he was able to return to his regular activities within a short period.
