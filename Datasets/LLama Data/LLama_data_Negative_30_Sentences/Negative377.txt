Patient Summary: 

The patient is a 45-year-old male, who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medications, but has not experienced sufficient relief. 

Past Medical History: 
The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulindependent diabetes mellitus. He had a myocardial infarction five years ago and underwent coronary artery bypass surgery. He has also had a history of recurrent migraines.

Surgical History: 
The patient has had a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair in the past. 

Current Medications: 
The patient takes lisinopril, simvastatin, metformin, and ibuprofen as needed.

Family History: 
The patient"s father died of a myocardial infarction at age 65. His mother has hypertension and hyperlipidemia. 

Social History: 
The patient is a heavy smoker and consumes alcohol occasionally. He works as a construction worker and has been unable to work due to his back pain. 

Review of Systems: 
The patient reports a prolonged history of back pain, left leg radiculopathy, and paresthesias. He also experiences occasional headaches and blurred vision. 

Physical Exam: 
The patient has limited mobility in his lower back and left lower extremity. His sensation is diminished over the L4-S1 dermatomes. Muscle strength is 4/5 in the left lower extremity. Reflexes are 2+ in the left knee and ankle. 

Imaging Studies: 
An MRI of the lumbar spine shows a large central herniated disc at L4-L5, with moderate spinal stenosis.

Plan: 
The patient is a candidate for a lumbar decompression surgery. The surgical procedure will involve a L4-L5 discectomy and lumbar fusion. The patient will be scheduled for a preoperative assessment to optimize his medical conditions before surgery. 

Postoperative Course: 
The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 discectomy and lumbar fusion. He tolerated the procedure well and did not experience any immediate complications. He was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day three with a plan to follow up with his surgeon in one week.

Follow-Up: 
The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon one week post-surgery. He reported some discomfort and incision pain but denied any significant complications. The incision site appeared well-healed. The patient was advised to continue with his postoperative recovery, including physical therapy and pain management. The patient was satisfied with the surgical outcome and reported significant pain relief.
