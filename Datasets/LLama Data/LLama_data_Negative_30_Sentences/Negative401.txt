Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has a history of smoking and has tried various conservative treatments without significant relief. The patient has been diagnosed with spondylolisthesis, spinal stenosis, and degenerative disc disease.

Preoperative Management:
The patient was optimize preoperatively with adequate pain management and nicotine replacement therapy. The patient underwent a preoperative evaluation, which revealed mild hypertension and a mildly elevated BMI. The patient was cleared for surgery by both the primary care physician and the anesthesiologist.

Operative Course:
The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 fusion with instrumentation. The procedure was uneventful, and the patient tolerated it well.

Postoperative Course:
The patient had a smooth postoperative course. He experienced some expected postoperative pain and discomfort, which was managed effectively with pain medication. The patient was ambulating and able to perform deep breathing exercises without any issues.

Complications:
The patient did not experience any acute complications during his postoperative course. He did develop some complications in the long-term, including recurrent back pain and decreased mobility. These complications were managed conservatively with physical therapy and pain management.

Follow-up:
The patient had regular follow-up appointments with his surgeon and primary care physician. His back pain gradually improved, and he was able to return to his usual activities. The patient was satisfied with the overall outcome of the surgery.
