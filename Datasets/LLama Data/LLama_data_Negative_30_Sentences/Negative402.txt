Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents for elective laparoscopic cholecystectomy. He has a history of gallstones and symptomatic cholecystitis. He has been experiencing worsening right upper quadrant abdominal pain and fever for the past week.

The patient has no significant medical history, except for hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. He has no history of previous surgeries or anesthesia complications.

The patient is allergic to penicillin and has a history of mild asthma. He denies any history of bleeding disorders or previous blood transfusions.

The patient is currently taking aspirin, lisinopril, and atorvastatin. He has no history of substance abuse or smoking.

On physical examination, the patient's vital signs are stable, with a temperature of 100.4°F (38°C). His abdomen is tender to palpation in the right upper quadrant, with guarding and rebound tenderness. There is no evidence of surgical scars or other abnormalities.

Preoperative lab results show a white blood cell count of 12,000 cells/mm3, hemoglobin A1C of 7.4%, and a serum bilirubin level of 2.5 mg/dL. The patient's liver function tests show an alanine transaminase (ALT) level of 55 U/L and aspartate transaminase (AST) level of 40 U/L.

The patient underwent laparoscopic cholecystectomy without any complications. He tolerated the procedure well and was transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) in stable condition.

Postoperative course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course. He was able to eat solid foods on the first postoperative day and was discharged on the second postoperative day.

He experienced some mild postoperative nausea and vomiting, which were managed with antiemetics. He also complained of mild abdominal discomfort, which was controlled with pain medication.

The patient's dressing was removed on the third postoperative day, and the incision site was clean and dry. He was advised to continue taking his medications as prescribed and to follow up with his surgeon in one week.

There were no complications during the postoperative period, and the patient made a full recovery.

The patient followed up with his surgeon one week after discharge, and the incision site was well-healed. He reported a significant improvement in his symptoms and was able to resume his normal activities without any restrictions.

The patient's postoperative lab results showed a white blood cell count of 9,000 cells/mm3, hemoglobin A1C of 7.2%, and a serum bilirubin level of 1.8 mg/dL. His liver function tests showed an ALT level of 30 U/L and AST level of 25 U/L.

The patient was very satisfied with the care he received and had no further questions or concerns.
