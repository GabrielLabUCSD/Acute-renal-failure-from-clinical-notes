Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and bilateral knee pain. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient is scheduled to undergo a lumbar fusion and bilateral knee replacement surgery.

Medical History:

The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He had a myocardial infarction 10 years ago and has been compliant with medications and lifestyle modifications since then. He has no history of bleeding disorders or previous surgeries.

Medications:

The patient is currently taking aspirin, atorvastatin, lisinopril, and metformin. He has no known allergies to medications.

Family History:

The patient's family history is positive for coronary artery disease and hypertension.

Social History:

The patient is a smoker and drinks alcohol occasionally. He works as a construction worker and is eager to return to work as soon as possible after surgery.

Review of Systems:

The patient reports no significant respiratory, cardiovascular, or neurological symptoms. He has some preoperative anxiety but is generally optimistic about the outcome of the surgery.

Physical Examination:

The patient's vital signs are stable, with a blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg and a heart rate of 75 bpm. His respiratory and cardiovascular examination is unremarkable. The patient has a mildly tender abdomen with no guarding or rebound tenderness. His knees are swollen and tender to palpation, with limited range of motion.

Laboratory Results:

The patient's complete blood count and electrolyte levels are within normal limits. His liver function tests show mild elevation in alanine transaminase and aspartate transaminase. His serum creatinine level is 1.2 mg/dL.

Imaging Studies:

The patient has undergone magnetic resonance imaging of the spine and knees, which shows multi-level degenerative disc disease and osteoarthritis.

Plan:

The patient will undergo a lumbar fusion and bilateral knee replacement surgery. He will be closely monitored for blood loss and fluid management during the surgery. He will receive appropriate prophylactic antibiotics and venous thromboembolism prevention medication.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful surgery and was transferred to the postanesthesia care unit for recovery. He had some nausea and vomiting in the early postoperative period but tolerated fluids and medications well. He was able to ambulate and perform deep breathing exercises as instructed. He was transferred to the surgical ward on the first postoperative day and continued to make a steady recovery.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during his postoperative course. He had some mild pain and discomfort, which was managed with medication. He was discharged home on the fifth postoperative day with instructions to continue with his medications, wound care, and rehabilitation.
