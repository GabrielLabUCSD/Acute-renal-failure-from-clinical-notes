Patient Summary:

The patient is a 65-year-old male with a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. He has a history of smoking and has quit for the past 10 years. He has a history of alcohol use but denies any recent consumption. He has been diagnosed with gallstones and is scheduled for a laparoscopic cholecystectomy.

Preoperative Medications:

The patient is currently taking lisinopril for hypertension, atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia, and metformin for non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. He has been advised to stop taking aspirin and non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) one week before surgery.

Allergies:

The patient has no known allergies to medications or materials.

Preoperative Lab Results:

The patient's complete blood count (CBC) and electrolyte panel were within normal limits. His liver function test (LFT) showed a mild elevation in alanine transaminase (ALT) and aspartate transaminase (AST). His serum bilirubin level was normal.

Preoperative Imaging:

An ultrasound of the gallbladder showed multiple gallstones with no evidence of cholecystitis or bile duct obstruction.

Surgical Procedure:

The patient underwent a laparoscopic cholecystectomy. The surgery was uneventful, and the patient was discharged from the hospital on the third postoperative day.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uncomplicated postoperative course. He experienced some discomfort and nausea on the first postoperative day, which was managed with pain medication and antiemetics. He was able to tolerate a liquid diet on the second postoperative day and was advanced to a soft diet on the third postoperative day.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any complications during his hospital stay. He was seen in the clinic for a follow-up appointment one week after discharge and reported no problems with his wound or any signs of infection.

Follow-up Plan:

The patient is scheduled for a follow-up appointment in three weeks to check on his progress and remove any sutures or staples. He will also have a repeat ultrasound of the gallbladder to confirm that all gallstones have been removed.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the patient underwent a laparoscopic cholecystectomy for symptomatic gallstones. The surgery was uneventful, and the patient had an uncomplicated postoperative course.
