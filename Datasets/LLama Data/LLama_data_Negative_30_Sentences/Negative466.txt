The patient's surgical procedure goes smoothly without any intraoperative complications. He is transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) for recovery and is closely monitored for any complications.

The patient's postoperative course is uneventful, and he is transferred to the surgical ward on the first postoperative day. He experiences some mild postoperative pain, which is managed with medication. He is advised to ambulate as soon as possible to prevent deep vein thrombosis.

The patient is discharged from the hospital on the fifth postoperative day, and he is advised to follow up with his surgeon in one week. He is also advised to continue his medications as prescribed and to monitor his wound for any signs of infection.

The patient does not experience any complications during his hospital stay. He is closely monitored for signs of surgical site infection, respiratory compromise, and cardiovascular instability, but none of these complications occur.
