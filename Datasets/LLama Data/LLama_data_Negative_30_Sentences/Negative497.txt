Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he smokes a pack of cigarettes per day. He denies any significant medical history, including cardiovascular disease, respiratory disease, or previous surgeries.

Preoperative Evaluation:
The patient underwent a thorough preoperative evaluation, including laboratory tests, imaging studies, and cardiovascular assessment. His blood work revealed normal electrolytes, renal function, and liver enzymes. His urinalysis was unremarkable. His chest x-ray and electrocardiogram were normal. An echocardiogram showed mild left ventricular hypertrophy, but no significant cardiac disease. A pulmonary function test revealed mild restrictive lung disease.

Operative Course:
The patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion surgery, with instrumentation and bone grafting. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient tolerated the procedure well. He remained stable throughout, with no significant hypotension or hypertension.

Postoperative Course:
The patient had a smooth postoperative course. He was transferred to the recovery room in stable condition and remained there for several hours for close observation. He experienced some postoperative pain, nausea, and dizziness, which were managed effectively with medication. He had a Foley catheter in place, which was removed on postoperative day 1. He was encouraged to ambulate early, and he was able to walk to the bathroom without assistance by postoperative day 2. He tolerated oral intake well and was advanced to a regular diet on postoperative day 3. He experienced no significant complications during his hospital stay.

Discharge and Follow-up:
The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 4, in stable condition. He was advised to continue with pain management and wound care, and to follow up with his surgeon in 2 weeks. He also underwent physical therapy to improve his mobility and reduce pain.

There were no significant complications during the patient's postoperative course, and he had a successful outcome from his lumbar fusion surgery.
