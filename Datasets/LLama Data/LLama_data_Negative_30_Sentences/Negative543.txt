Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male, smoker, and laborer who presents with a 2-year history of worsening left knee pain and stiffness, consistent with osteoarthritis. The pain has been limiting his mobility and ability to perform daily activities, including work. The patient has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medications, without significant relief.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, controlled with medication. He has no known allergies and has never had a surgical procedure before. The patient is currently taking aspirin and metformin.

On physical examination, the patient has tenderness and swelling of the left knee, with limited range of motion. There is no evidence of effusion or warmth. The patient has a normal neurovascular examination.

Imaging studies, including radiographs and MRI, confirm the diagnosis of osteoarthritis.

The patient has been scheduled for a total knee replacement surgery. The patient understands the risks and benefits of the procedure, including the potential for postoperative complications such as infection, bleeding, and nerve damage. The patient has been advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or strenuous activity for several weeks after the surgery.

Postoperative Course:
The patient had an uneventful general anesthesia and surgical procedure. He was transferred to the recovery room in stable condition and remained there for 2 hours before being transferred to the ward.

The patient's postoperative pain was adequately managed with opioids and nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs). The patient was able to ambulate with assistance on the first postoperative day and gradually increased his mobility over the next few days.

The patient experienced some postoperative nausea and vomiting, which were treated with antiemetics. The patient also developed a mild, controlled fever on the second postoperative day, which was treated with antibiotics.

The patient was discharged from the hospital on the fifth postoperative day, with instructions to continue his pain management regimen and to follow up with the surgeon in 2 weeks.

Complications:
The patient did not experience any significant complications during his postoperative course.

Conclusion:
The patient underwent successful total knee replacement surgery and had an uneventful postoperative course. The patient was discharged from the hospital without any significant complications and will follow up with the surgeon in 2 weeks.
