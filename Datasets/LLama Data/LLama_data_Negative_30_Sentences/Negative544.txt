Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents for elective laparoscopic cholecystectomy.
He has a history of chronic gallstone disease and recent onset ofright upper quadrant pain.
He has no allergies, smokes a pack of cigarettes per day, and drinks occasional alcohol.
The patient's medical history includes hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetesmellitus.
He takes lisinopril, atorvastatin, and metformin regularly.
His family history reveals a positive history of coronary artery disease in his father.
The patient has no history of previous abdominal surgeries.
His operated heart is full and regular, with no murmurs or extra sounds.
His lungs are clear to auscultation, with normal respiratory effort.
His abdominal exam reveals a positive Murphy's sign, guarding, and tenderness to palpation in the right upper quadrant.
His liver function tests show mild elevations in AST, ALT, and Alkaline phosphatase.
His serum amylase is elevated.
His blood count shows mild anemia and thrombocytopenia.
He has an elevated INR.
The patient has a history of recent weight loss, fever, and chills. 
He has no history of recent travel or sick contact.
His surgical site is clean, and his surgical risk is moderate.
The patient underwent uneventful laparoscopic cholecystectomy, with no reported complications during surgery.
Postoperatively, the patient had a smooth course, with adequate pain management on PCA.
He had no respiratory or cardiovascular complications.
He had no surgical site infections, bleeding, or hernias.
He tolerated oral intake well and did not require re-intubation.
He was discharged on the fifth postoperative day, with follow-up instructions to be seen in the clinic within one week.
At his follow-up, visit the patient reported some residual right upper quadrant pain, which was managed with oral analgesics.
He tolerated oral intake well, with no further complaints of nausea or vomiting.
He had no wound complications, and his incision sites looked healthy.
The patient was very satisfied with the overall outcome of his surgery.

As for long-term follow-up, the patient was seen in the clinic regularly, with close monitoring of his medical conditions and the surgical site.
He continued to smoke, but his smoking intensity decreased gradually over time.
His hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus were well-controlled with prescribed medications.
His liver function tests and amylase levels returned to normal.
He gained weight and maintained his weight loss during the follow-up period.
He did not report any further episodes of biliary colic pain or other surgical complications.
In conclusion, the patient had an uneventful postoperative course and good short-term and long-term outcomes following elective laparoscopic cholecystectomy.
