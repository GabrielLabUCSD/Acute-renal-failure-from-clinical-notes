Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative joint disease. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and pain medication, without significant relief. He is currently a smoker and has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. The patient has been scheduled for an elective lumbar fusion surgery to address his chronic back pain.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative workup, which included a physical examination, laboratory tests, and imaging studies. His blood work showed mild anemia and elevated liver enzymes, likely related to his smoking habit. A chest X-ray and electrocardiogram were also performed and showed no acute abnormalities.

Surgical Course:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion surgery, which involved the implantation of a metal rod and screws to stabilize his spine. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately three hours to complete.

Postoperative Course:

The patient was closely monitored in the postanesthesia care unit (PACU) for several hours after surgery. He experienced some postoperative nausea and vomiting, which was managed with antiemetic medication. The patient was able to ambulate and sit up without significant discomfort. He was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day two and was advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for several weeks.

Complications:

The patient developed a mild respiratory infection on postoperative day five, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some minor wound drainage, which resolved with dressing changes. The patient did not experience any significant postoperative complications, such as neurological deficits, bleeding, or infection.

Follow-Up:

The patient followed up with his surgeon on postoperative day 14 and reported significant improvement in his back pain. He was able to resume some of his daily activities, including light walking and gentle stretching. The patient was advised to continue his postoperative recovery and to follow up with his surgeon for further assessment and instructions.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion surgery and experienced a smooth postoperative course. He was discharged from the hospital without any major complications and followed up with his surgeon for further evaluation and recovery. The patient's postoperative recovery was uneventful, and he reported significant improvement in his back pain.
