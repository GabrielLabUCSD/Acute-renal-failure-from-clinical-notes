Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient has a history of smoking and hypertension. He is currently taking aspirin and ibuprofen for pain management.

Preoperative lab results reveal an elevated white blood cell count and platelet count, suggestive of mild inflammation. Imaging studies show multilevel degenerative disc disease with spinal stenosis and a left-sided L4-L5 herniated disc.

The patient underwent a left L4-L5 hemilaminectomy and decompression. Intraoperative monitoring showed no signs of neurological deficits or compromise. Estimated blood loss was minimal, and the patient received 2 units of intravenous tranexamic acid to reduce bleeding.

Postoperative day 1: The patient experiences some discomfort, which is managed with scheduled pain medication. He is encouraged to ambulate and perform deep breathing exercises to prevent respiratory complications.

Postoperative day 2: The patient's pain is better controlled, and he is able to walk around the hospital room without assistance. He is advised to continue with his postoperative exercises and to gradually increase his activity level.

Postoperative day 3: The patient is transferred to the rehabilitation unit for further recovery and physical therapy. He is making good progress with his exercises and experiences only mild, manageable pain.

Postoperative day 5: The patient reports feeling significantly better and is able to walk independently without any aid. He is discharged home with a prescription for pain medication and instructions to continue with his exercises.

Postoperative week 2: Follow-up appointment shows the patient is recovering well and experiencing minimal pain. He is advised to continue with his postoperative exercises and gradually return to his normal activities.

Postoperative week 4: The patient reports some numbness and tingling sensations in his left leg, which is managed with a change in his medication regimen. He is advised to continue with his exercises and follow-up in 2 weeks.

Postoperative week 6: The patient shows significant improvement in his symptoms and is able to resume his normal activities without restrictions.

Postoperative month 3: Follow-up MRI shows continued improvement in the patient's multilevel degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis. The patient experiences occasional mild pain but is able to manage it with over-the-counter medication.
