Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has been smoking for 20 years and has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He denies any past history of cardiovascular disease or pulmonary disease. He has tried various medications and physical therapy without significant relief. He has a history of depression and anxiety and is currently taking medication for both. He has no known allergies.

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery 10 years ago, which provided temporary relief but ultimately failed. He has been advised to undergo a revision surgery, which he is considering. He is concerned about the risks and complications associated with the surgery.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a uneventful postoperative course. He experienced some discomfort and pain in the immediate postoperative period, which was managed with pain medication. He was able to ambulate independently within the first 24 hours and was discharged home on postoperative day 3.

Complications:

The patient developed a wound infection on postoperative day 7, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some degree of urinary retention, which resolved with conservative management. He did not experience any respiratory or cardiovascular complications.

Recovery:

The patient's pain and discomfort gradually improved over the first 6 weeks postoperatively. He was able to return to work at 8 weeks postoperatively and was able to resume his normal activities, including driving, at 12 weeks postoperatively. He continued to experience some degree of numbness and tingling in his left leg, but it did not significantly impact his daily activities.

Follow-up:

The patient followed up with his surgeon at 6 weeks, 12 weeks, and 24 weeks postoperatively. He did not experience any significant complications during this time. His incisional scar appeared well-healed and he did not report any ongoing pain or discomfort.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the patient had a successful revision lumbar fusion surgery. He experienced a minor wound infection and urinary retention, but these complications were managed conservatively and did not significantly impact his postoperative course. The patient was able to return to work and resume his normal activities at 8 and 12 weeks postoperatively, respectively. He continued to experience some degree of numbness and tingling in his left leg, but this did not significantly impact his daily activities.
