Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and bilateral knee pain. The patient has a history of degenerative disc disease, osteoarthritis, and hypertension. The patient has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief.

Preoperative Studies:

The patient underwent preoperative studies, including an MRI of the lumbar spine, which showed severe degenerative disc disease at L4-L5 and L5-S1. The patient also had an arthrocentesis of the knee, which showed inflammation and degenerative changes.

Procedure:

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion at L4-L5 and L5-S1, as well as a total knee replacement of the bilateral knees. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately 4 hours.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a successful surgery and was transferred to the recovery room in stable condition. The patient experienced some postoperative pain, which was managed with pain medication. The patient was able to ambulate and perform deep breathing exercises without difficulty.

The patient was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 3 and was advised to follow a rehabilitation program to optimize function and minimize the risk of complications. The patient was also advised to follow a pain management plan, which included medication and alternative therapies.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during the postoperative period. However, the patient did experience some minor complications, including:

* Mild nausea and vomiting on postoperative day 1, which was managed with antiemetics.
* Mild urinary retention on postoperative day 2, which was managed with bladder catheterization.
* Mild wound infection at the knee surgery site, which was managed with antibiotics.

Follow-up:

The patient attended follow-up appointments at 2 weeks, 6 weeks, and 12 weeks postoperatively. At the 12-week follow-up, the patient reported significant improvement in back and knee pain, and was able to ambulate without difficulty. The patient was also able to perform activities of daily living without assistance.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, the patient underwent successful lumbar fusion and total knee replacement surgery. The patient had a smooth postoperative course, with minor complications that were managed effectively. The patient was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 3 and was able to return to normal activities at 12 weeks postoperatively.
