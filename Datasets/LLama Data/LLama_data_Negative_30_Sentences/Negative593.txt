Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male with a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes mellitus. He has a history of smoking and alcohol use. He presents with a new diagnosis of adenocarcinoma of the colon, stage III, and is scheduled for a colon resection.

Preoperative workup:
The patient had a complete blood count, electrolyte panel, and liver function tests, which were all within normal limits. A colonoscopy was performed, and the tumor was visualized and biopsied. Imaging studies, including a computed tomography (CT) scan of the chest, abdomen, and pelvis, and a positron emission tomography (PET) scan, showed no evidence of metastasis.

Operative course:
The patient underwent a laparoscopic anterior resection of the colon with anastomosis. The surgery was completed without any complications, and the patient was transferred to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) for recovery.

Postoperative course:
The patient had a smooth postoperative course and was discharged from the hospital on the fifth postoperative day. He was advised to follow a low-fiber diet for 4 weeks and to avoid heavy lifting or exercise for 6 weeks. He was also prescribed pain medication and instructed to follow a wound care regimen.

Complications:
The patient did not experience any complications during his postoperative course. He had no fever, infection, or wound issues and did not require any unplanned hospital readmissions. He followed up with his surgeon and primary care physician regularly and had no concerns or issues during the postoperative period.
