Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who has been scheduled for a laparoscopic cholecystectomy to remove his gallbladder. He has a history of gallstones and has experienced recurrent episodes of biliary colic over the past year. The patient has no allergies and is not taking any medications. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, for which he takes medication. The patient is a current smoker and has a history of alcohol use disorder, for which he has received treatment.

Preoperative Evaluation:

The patient underwent a preoperative evaluation, which included a thorough physical examination, laboratory tests, and imaging studies. His blood work revealed elevated liver enzymes, consistent with biliary colic. A computed tomography (CT) scan of the abdomen and pelvis showed multiple gallstones in the gallbladder. The patient"s ECG and echocardiogram were normal.

Operative Course:

The patient underwent a laparoscopic cholecystectomy under general anesthesia. The procedure was uneventful, and the gallbladder was successfully removed. The patient tolerated the procedure well and was transferred to the postanesthesia care unit (PACU) for recovery.

Postoperative Course:

The patient experienced some discomfort in the abdomen and shoulder pain, which was managed with pain medication. He had a moderate amount of drainage from the surgical site, which was expected and resolved on its own. The patient was able to tolerate a clear liquid diet on the first postoperative day and progressed to a full liquid diet by the second day. He was discharged from the hospital on the third postoperative day.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during his postoperative course. However, he did have some minor issues, including nausea and vomiting, which were managed with antiemetics. He also experienced some constipation, which was treated with laxatives. The patient did not develop any wound infections or other complications related to the surgical site.

Follow-up:

The patient followed up with his surgeon one week after discharge. He reported some mild discomfort in the abdomen, which was managed with pain medication. The patient was advised to continue taking pain medication as needed and to follow a soft diet for the next few days. He was also advised to avoid heavy lifting or strenuous activities for several weeks. The patient"s wound was healing well, and there were no signs of infection.

In conclusion, the patient underwent a successful laparoscopic cholecystectomy and had a smooth postoperative course. He experienced some minor complications, such as nausea and constipation, which were managed effectively. The patient followed up with his surgeon and was advised to continue taking pain medication and to follow a soft diet for a few days. He was also instructed to avoid heavy lifting or strenuous activities for several weeks to ensure proper healing.
