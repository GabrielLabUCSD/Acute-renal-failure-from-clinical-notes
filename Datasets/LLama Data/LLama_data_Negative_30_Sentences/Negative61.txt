Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and decreased mobility. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medications, without significant relief. The patient is a candidate for lumbar fusion surgery to address his chronic pain and improve his quality of life.

Preoperative History:

* The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and sleep apnea.
* He is currently taking aspirin, atorvastatin, and CPAP for his sleep apnea.
* The patient has a history of smoking but quit 5 years ago.
* He has a history of alcohol use but denies any recent alcohol consumption.
* The patient has no known allergies to medications.
* He has a history of previous abdominal surgery but denies any previous back surgeries.

Intraoperative Course:

* The patient underwent lumbar fusion surgery under general anesthesia without any complications.
* The procedure included the insertion of instrumentation and bone grafting.
* The patient's vital signs remained stable throughout the procedure.

Postoperative Course:

* The patient was transferred to the postoperative care unit in stable condition.
* He experienced some postoperative pain, which was managed with narcotic medication.
* The patient was able to ambulate independently within the first 24 hours after surgery.
* He experienced some minor nausea and vomiting but did not require any antiemetic medication.
* The patient's wound site appeared clean and dry, with no signs of infection.
* He was discharged from the hospital 3 days after surgery.

Follow-up:

* The patient followed up with his surgeon 2 weeks after discharge.
* He reported minimal pain and had resumed his normal activities.
* The patient was satisfied with the outcome of the surgery and reported improved mobility and reduced pain.
* He was advised to continue physical therapy to further improve his mobility and strength.

Complications:

* The patient did not experience any complications during his postoperative course.
