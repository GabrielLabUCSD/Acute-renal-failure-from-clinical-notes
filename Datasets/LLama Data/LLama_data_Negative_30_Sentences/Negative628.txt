The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, but has not found sufficient relief. He is a candidate for lumbar fusion surgery.

The patient's surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair. He has no history of bleeding disorders or previous surgical complications.

On physical examination, the patient has tenderness to palpation over the lumbar spine and positive straight leg raise on the left side. He has decreased sensation to light touch and bilateral Hoffmann's reflexes.

Preoperative lab tests reveal a normal complete blood count, electrolyte panel, and coagulation studies. His creatinine level is 1.2 mg/dL, and his estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) is 85 mL/min/1.73m2.

The patient underwent lumbar fusion surgery, which consisted of a posterior approach with instrumentation and bone grafting. The surgery was uneventful, and the patient tolerated it well.

Postoperatively, the patient was transferred to the intensive care unit for observation. He had a stable vital signs and was able to move his lower extremities. He did not require any blood transfusions.

On the first postoperative day, the patient developed a fever of 101.3°F (38.5°C) and complained of worsening lower back pain. His wound was clean and dry, and there was no evidence of surgical site infection.

The patient's fever persisted on the second postoperative day, and he was started on broad-spectrum antibiotics. His back pain improved slightly, and he was able to ambulate with assistance.

By the third postoperative day, the patient's fever had resolved, and he was able to ambulate independently. His back pain had improved significantly, and he was able to sit up and walk without assistance.

The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 4 with a follow-up appointment in 2 weeks. He was advised to continue his medications as prescribed and to gradually increase his activity level over the next few weeks.

The patient did not experience any complications during his hospital stay. He had a uneventful postoperative course and was able to recover without any significant issues.
