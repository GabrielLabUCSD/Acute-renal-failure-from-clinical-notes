Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and decreased mobility. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient has a history of smoking and alcohol use. He also has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, for which he takes medication. The patient"s family history is significant for cardiovascular disease.

Preoperative Evaluation:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative evaluation, including laboratory tests, electrocardiography, and imaging studies. His blood work showed mild anemia and elevated liver enzymes. An electrocardiogram (ECG) showed evidence of left ventricular hypertrophy. Imaging studies demonstrated significant spinal stenosis and degenerative changes.

Operative Course:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar decompression surgery. Intraoperatively, the patient had stable vital signs and did not experience any major complications.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course. He experienced some expected postoperative pain, nausea, and vomiting, which were managed with medication. The patient also developed a mild respiratory infection, which was treated with antibiotics.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during his hospital stay. However, he did develop a mild urinary retention, which was managed with bladder catheterization. The patient also experienced some mild postoperative delirium, which resolved with supportive care.

Discharge and Follow-up:

The patient was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 4. He was advised to continue his medications and follow up with his surgeon in 2 weeks. The patient also attended a follow-up appointment with his primary care physician and reported significant improvement in his back pain and mobility.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgery and a smooth postoperative course. He was discharged home without any major complications and had a positive outcome.
