Patient Summary: 

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents for a preoperative evaluation in preparation for a laparoscopic cholecystectomy. He has a history of cholelithiasis with symptoms of abdominal pain and nausea. His medical history also includes hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. He takes medication for these conditions and has been compliant with his treatment regimen. He has no known allergies to medications.

The patient has never had any previous surgeries and is concerned about the risks associated with anesthesia. He has been a smoker for the past 10 years but has decreased his smoking habit recently. He drinks alcohol occasionally but not excessively. He has a healthy diet and exercises regularly.

During the preoperative evaluation, the patient's vital signs were within normal limits. His blood work showed a slightly elevated white blood cell count and mildly elevated liver enzymes. His electrolyte levels were normal. A chest X-ray and an ECG were also normal.

The patient's surgical procedure went smoothly, and he tolerated the anesthesia well. He had a uneventful postoperative course and was discharged from the hospital on the second postoperative day.

During the follow-up appointment, the patient reported some mild discomfort at the surgical site, which was managed with pain medication. He also reported a slight increase in urinary frequency, which resolved within a few days. He did not experience any complications such as respiratory, cardiac, or renal problems.

Overall, the patient had an uncomplicated postoperative course and is recovering well from his surgery.
