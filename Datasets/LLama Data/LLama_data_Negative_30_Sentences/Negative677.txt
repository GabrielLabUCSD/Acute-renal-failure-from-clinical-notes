Patient Summary: 

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy.
He has tried physical therapy, chiropractic care, and pain management without significant relief.
MRI revealed a large lumbar disc herniation, which is likely causing his symptoms. 
After a thorough discussion of risks and benefits, the patient elected to undergo a lumbar decompression surgery. 
Preoperative examination and lab results were within normal limits, except for a slightly elevated BMI. 
The patient had a uneventful surgery, lasting approximately 2 hours under general anesthesia.
The patient was extubated in the operating room and transported to the post-anesthesia care unit (PACU) for recovery. 
He experienced some nausea and vomiting in the PACU, which was managed with antiemetics. 
The patient was discharged from the hospital the following day on postoperative day 1 (POD1). 
He was advised to rest and avoid heavy lifting or bending for 6 weeks.
He was prescribed pain medication, muscle relaxants, and physical therapy for postoperative management. 
On POD 7, the patient reported increased pain and swelling at the incision site. 
He was evaluated by his surgeon, who diagnosed him with a superficial surgical site infection (SSI). 
The patient was started on antibiotics and underwent a course of wound care. 
He required a brief hospitalization for iv antibiotics and wound care. 
On POD 14, the patient was noted to have a decreased sensation in his left leg. 
A neurological consult was done, and EMG/NCS revealed a new left common peroneal nerve palsy. 
The patient was started on conservative management with physical therapy and brace. 
On POD 21, the patient reported significant improvement in his pain and function. 
He was able to ambulate without assistance and resumed his normal activities. 
On POD 28, the patient reported minimal discomfort at the incision site and full recovery of his left leg function. 
He was grateful for the surgical intervention and the outcome. 
The patient had a postoperative follow-up appointment with his surgeon on POD 35, where he was noted to have a full recovery. 
He was advised to continue with physical therapy and maintain good posture to prevent recurrence of his symptoms. 
Overall, the patient had a successful surgical outcome and minimal complications related to his surgery.
