Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and decreased mobility. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He has a history of smoking and alcohol use but reports that he has quit smoking for the past year. He has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, for which he takes medication. He denies any history of bleeding disorders or previous surgeries.

Preoperative Examination:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative examination, including blood work, urinalysis, electrocardiogram (ECG), and chest X-ray. His blood work revealed a hemoglobin level of 14 g/dL, platelet count of 250 K/uL, and international normalized ratio (INR) of 1.2. His urinalysis was unremarkable. His ECG showed a normal sinus rhythm. His chest X-ray revealed a clear lung field.

Surgical Procedure:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar decompression and fusion surgery. The surgical procedure involved a posterior approach, and the patient received general anesthesia. The surgery lasted for 4 hours, and the patient received 2 units of packed red blood cells intraoperatively for blood loss.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course. He was extubated immediately after surgery and was transferred to the recovery room. He remained stable and was able to move his lower extremities immediately postoperatively. He was administered intravenous analgesics for pain management. His blood work on the first postoperative day showed a hemoglobin level of 12 g/dL, platelet count of 200 K/uL, and INR of 1.1.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any complications in the immediate postoperative period. He did not develop any signs of infection, such as fever, tachycardia, or purulent drainage from the wound. His wound healed well, and he was discharged from the hospital on the fifth postoperative day.

Follow-up:

The patient followed up with his surgeon 2 weeks after discharge. He reported mild back pain, which was managed with oral analgesics. He was able to ambulate independently and had improved mobility. His wound was well-healed, and he did not report any signs of infection.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, this patient had a successful lumbar decompression and fusion surgery. He had a smooth postoperative course and did not experience any complications. His wound healed well, and he was able to ambulate independently within 2 weeks after surgery. He will continue to follow up with his surgeon for further evaluation and management of his back pain.
