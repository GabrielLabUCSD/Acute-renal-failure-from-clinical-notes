Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left lower extremity radiculopathy. The pain has been persistent for the past year, worsening over the past few months, and has been described as an 8/10 in severity. The patient has tried physical therapy and conservative management without significant improvement. He denies any prior history of back surgery.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled on medication. He is a former smoker, quit 5 years ago, and drinks alcohol occasionally. He works as a construction worker and is able to perform light duties, but the pain has significantly impacted his ability to perform his job.

On examination, the patient has decreased lumbar lordosis, limited flexion, and positive straight leg raise on the left side. His neurological examination shows decreased sensation to light touch in the L5 and S1 dermatomes on the left lower extremity. Muscle strength is 4/5 in the left knee and ankle, and there is a mild left-sided sciatic notch tenderness.

Imaging studies reveal a large central disc protrusion at L4-L5, with severe spinal canal stenosis. There is also evidence of facet joint arthropathy and mild degenerative spondylolisthesis.

The patient underwent a left L4-L5 hemilaminectomy and facetectomy for decompression of the spinal cord and nerve roots. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He was discharged home on the second postoperative day with a lumbar brace and instructions to gradually resume his activities.

The patient followed up with his surgeon at 2 weeks and reported significant improvement in his pain, which was now a 2/10. He was able to ambulate without assistance and had resumed light activities. His neurological examination showed improved sensation in the L5 and S1 dermatomes, and his muscle strength had improved to 5/5.

At 6 weeks, the patient reported continued improvement in his pain, which was now a 0/10. He was able to perform his job without restrictions and had resumed all of his preoperative activities. His neurological examination continued to show improvement, with full sensation and normal muscle strength in the left lower extremity.

The patient had a satisfactory postoperative course and did not experience any complications. His pain and neurological symptoms significantly improved, and he was able to resume his normal activities without restrictions.
