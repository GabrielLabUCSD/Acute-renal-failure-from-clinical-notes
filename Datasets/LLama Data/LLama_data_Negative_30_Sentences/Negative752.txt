Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He has a history of smoking, hypertension, and hyperlipidemia. The patient has no known allergies and has a negative family history of anesthesia complications.

Preoperative evaluation includes a thorough physical examination, blood work, and imaging studies, which reveal a herniated disc at L4-L5 with left-sided sciatica. The patient is classified as ASA Class III due to his smoking habit and medical comorbidities.

The patient undergoes a successful L4-L5 discectomy and fusion surgery under general anesthesia. Intraoperative course is unremarkable, with stable vital signs and minimal blood loss.

Postoperative course is complicated by a respiratory complication, requiring supplemental oxygen therapy and diuretic medication. The patient also experiences some postoperative nausea and vomiting, which is managed with antiemetics.

Patient is discharged on postoperative day 3 with a surgical drain in place and instructions for wound care and pain management. He follows up with his surgeon and primary care physician without any complications.

At 6-week follow-up, the patient reports significant improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy. He has maintained a regular exercise regimen, including physical therapy, and has gradually weaned off his pain medication.

At 12-month follow-up, the patient has achieved full recovery and resumes all his preoperative activities without any restrictions. He reports excellent satisfaction with the surgical outcome and encourages others to consider the same procedure for similar symptoms.

Complications:

* Respiratory complication (Requiring supplemental oxygen therapy and diuretic medication)
* Postoperative nausea and vomiting (Managed with antiemetics)

No acute renal failure or other serious complications occurred during the patient's postoperative course.
