Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents for elective surgery. He has a medical history of hypertension, diabetes mellitus, and hyperlipidemia. The patient had a myocardial infarction 10 years ago and has been compliant with medications and lifestyle modifications since then. He has a history of chronic lower back pain and has been smoking 10 cigarettes per day for the past 20 years. The patient has a body mass index of 35 kg/m2.

Preoperative Examination:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative examination, including an electrocardiogram, echocardiogram, and stress test, which showed no evidence of cardiac ischemia. His blood work showed normal liver and kidney function, and his hemoglobin A1c was 7.8%. The patient was cleared for surgery by his primary care physician and anesthesiologist.

Surgical Procedure:

The patient underwent a laparoscopic cholecystectomy for symptomatic gallstones. The surgery lasted for 2 hours, and the patient received general anesthesia.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course. He was extubated immediately after surgery and was able to drink fluids within 2 hours. He experienced minimal postoperative pain and was able to ambulate on the first postoperative day. The patient was discharged on the second postoperative day with a prescription for pain medication and instructions to follow a clear liquid diet for 1 week.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any complications during his hospital stay. He followed his postoperative instructions carefully and did not develop any wound infections or other complications.

Follow-up:

The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon 2 weeks after discharge. He reported minimal pain and was able to eat a normal diet. His wound was well-healed, and he was able to resume his normal activities without any restrictions. The patient was satisfied with the outcome of his surgery and was grateful for the care he received.
