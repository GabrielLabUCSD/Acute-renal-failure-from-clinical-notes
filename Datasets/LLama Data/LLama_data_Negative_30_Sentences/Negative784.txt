Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male, who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has been diagnosed with a lumbar herniated disc and is planned for a lumbar discectomy.

Medical History:

The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and smoking. He had a myocardial infarction 10 years ago and has been compliant with medications and lifestyle modifications since then. He also has a history of non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus and hypothyroidism.

Surgical History:

The patient has had a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair in the past.

Current Medications:

The patient is currently taking lisinopril, atorvastatin, metformin, and levothyroxine.

Allergies:

The patient is allergic to penicillin and sulfa medications.

Family History:

The patient's father died of a myocardial infarction at the age of 60. His mother has a history of breast cancer.

Social History:

The patient is married and works as an accountant. He has two adult children who live independently. He smokes a pack of cigarettes per day and drinks alcohol occasionally.

Review of Systems:

The patient reports frequent lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy, which worsens with bending, lifting, and walking. He also experiences numbness and tingling sensations in his left leg and foot. He denies any bowel or bladder dysfunction, fevers, or chills.

Physical Examination:

On examination, the patient's vital signs are stable. His back examination reveals tenderness and spasm in the lower back. He has decreased sensation in his left leg and foot. His neurological examination is otherwise normal.

Imaging Studies:

MRI revelas a large lumbar disc herniation at L4-L5.

Lab Results:

The patient's complete blood count, electrolyte panel, and liver function tests are within normal limits. His serum creatinine level is elevated at 1.8 mg/dL.

Plan:

The patient will undergo a lumbar discectomy. He will be closely monitored for signs of postoperative complications such as infection, bleeding, or neurological deficits. He will also receive aggressive pain management and physical therapy postoperatively.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He was discharged on the third postoperative day with a prescription for pain medication and instructions to gradually resume his activities.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any postoperative complications.

Follow-up:

The patient will follow up with his surgeon in one week and with his primary care physician in two weeks. He will also attend physical therapy sessions to improve his lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy.
