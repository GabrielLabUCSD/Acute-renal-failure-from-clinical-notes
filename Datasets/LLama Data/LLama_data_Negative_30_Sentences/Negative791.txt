Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and sciatica. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He has a history of smoking but quit 5 years ago. The patient has no known allergies and is currently taking no medications.

Preoperative Evaluation:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative evaluation, including laboratory tests and imaging studies. His blood work showed normal liver and kidney function, and his blood count was within normal limits. A preoperative electrocardiogram (EKG) and echocardiogram were also performed and showed no abnormalities.

Surgical History:

The patient underwent a lumbar decompression and fusion surgery. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately 3 hours to complete. The patient received a total of 2 units of blood during the procedure.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course. He was transferred to the recovery room where he remained stable and alert. He was started on intravenous pain medication and was able to tolerate a liquid diet.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during his postoperative course. He did develop a mild wound infection, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some nausea and vomiting, which were managed with antiemetics.

Discharge:

The patient was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day 5. He was advised to continue his pain medication and to gradually increase his activity level. He was also instructed to follow up with his surgeon within 2 weeks for a postoperative appointment.

Follow-up:

The patient followed up with his surgeon as instructed and reported significant improvement in his back pain. He was able to return to his normal activities, including work, within 6 weeks after surgery.
