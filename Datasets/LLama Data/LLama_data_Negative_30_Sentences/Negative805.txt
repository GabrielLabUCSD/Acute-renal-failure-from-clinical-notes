Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male with a medical history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. He has a history of smoking but quit 5 years ago. He has no known allergies and takes medications for his conditions as prescribed. He has a history of coronary artery disease and had a coronary artery bypass graft 10 years ago. He also has a history of left knee osteoarthritis and has been using a cane for ambulation for the past year.

The patient will be undergoing a left total knee arthroplasty (TKA) to alleviate his knee pain and improve his mobility. The patient is aware of the risks and benefits of the procedure and has given informed consent.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful surgery and was transferred to the postanesthesia care unit (PACU) for recovery. He was extubated and had stable vital signs. He was noted to have some delayed gastric emptying, but it resolved within 24 hours. He was started on patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) for pain management.

On postoperative day (POD) 1, the patient was transferred to the orthopedic floor. He was able to ambulate with assistance and had minimal pain. Physical therapy was started to help with mobility and range of motion exercises.

On POD 2, the patient had a sudden onset of left calf pain and swelling. He was evaluated by the surgical team and was found to have a large hematoma in his calf. The patient was taken back to the operating room for evacuation of the hematoma. The patient tolerated the procedure well and had no complications.

On POD 3, the patient was continued on PCA for pain management. He was able to ambulate independently and had minimal pain. His wound was clean and dry.

On POD 4, the patient was discharged home with a prescription for pain medication and instructions for wound care and physical therapy. He was advised to follow up with the surgeon within a week.

Complications:

The patient had a postoperative hematoma that required evacuation.

Conclusion:

The patient tolerated the surgery well and had a smooth postoperative course. He had one complication, a postoperative hematoma, but it was managed successfully. The patient was discharged home and advised to follow up with the surgeon within a week.
