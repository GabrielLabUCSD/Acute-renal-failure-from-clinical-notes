Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left lower extremity radiculopathy. The patient has a history of smoking and alcohol use. The patient has tried physical therapy and medications without relief. The patient is scheduled for a lumbar fusion surgery.

Preoperative Assessment:

The patient's vital signs are within normal limits. The patient's neurological exam reveals decreased sensation to light touch in the L4-S1 dermatomes and weakness in the left lower extremity. The patient's straight leg raise test is positive at 45 degrees. The patient has normal reflexes and normal muscle strength in the right lower extremity.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful intraoperative course. The patient experienced some postoperative nausea and vomiting which were managed with medication. The patient's pain was managed with patient-controlled analgesia. The patient was able to ambulate and sit in a chair on the first postoperative day. The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 3.

Complications:

The patient developed a wound infection on postoperative day 7 which was managed with antibiotics. The patient also developed urinary retention which was managed with a foley catheter. The patient's pain was managed with oral narcotics. The patient had a slow recovery and was unable to return to work for 6 weeks.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery for chronic back pain and radiculopathy. The patient had an uneventful intraoperative course and developed wound infection and urinary retention as complications. The patient had a slow recovery and was unable to return to work for 6 weeks.
