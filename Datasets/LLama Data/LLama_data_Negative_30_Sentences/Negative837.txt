Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The pain is described as a constant, dull ache that worsens with activity and is accompanied by numbness and tingling in the left leg.

The patient has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and is currently taking medication to control these conditions.

The patient has no known allergies.

The patient has a history of smoking, but quit 5 years ago.

The patient denies any recent significant weight loss or gain.

The patient's family history is significant for coronary artery disease and cancer.

The patient's social history includes occasional alcohol consumption and no illicit drug use.

The patient's review of systems is positive for recent worsening of back pain, left leg radiculopathy, and occasional difficulty with urinary retention.

The patient's surgical history includes a cholecystectomy 10 years ago.

The patient's current medications include atorvastatin, lisinopril, and gabapentin.

The patient is currently working as a machinist and denies any recent changes in his work environment.

The patient's physical examination reveals decreased sensation to light touch and pain in the left L5-S1 dermatome.

The patient's straight leg raise test is positive at 40 degrees.

The patient's lumbar spine MRI shows a large central disc herniation at L5-S1 with significant spinal canal stenosis.

The patient's neurological consultation recommends surgical intervention.

The patient is scheduled for a L5-S1 lumbar microdiscectomy.

The patient's postoperative course is uneventful.

The patient experiences significant improvement in back pain and left leg radiculopathy.

The patient is able to return to work as a machinist 6 weeks postoperatively.

The patient's follow-up MRI at 6 weeks shows significant improvement in the disc herniation and spinal canal stenosis.

The patient has no complications during the postoperative period.
