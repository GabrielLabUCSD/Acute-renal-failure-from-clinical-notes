Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male, who presented with a 2-day history of worsening abdominal pain, nausea, and vomiting. He has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease. The patient had an unremarkable physical exam, with the exception of tenderness to palpation in the upper right quadrant of his abdomen. Laboratory tests showed a mild elevation in liver function tests and a positive lipase. An abdominal ultrasound revealed a gallstone in the gallbladder. The patient underwent a laparoscopic cholecystectomy.

The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He experienced some discomfort in the abdomen and shoulders, which was managed with pain medication. On the first postoperative day, he developed a fever, which resolved with antibiotics. He was able to tolerate a regular diet and liquids without any issues. The patient was discharged on the second postoperative day and advised to follow up with his surgeon in one week.

At the one-week follow-up appointment, the patient reported some persistent discomfort and swelling in the abdomen. On examination, there was mild tenderness in the abdomen, and the incision site looked healthy. The patient was advised to continue with pain medication and to follow up in two weeks.

At the two-week follow-up appointment, the patient reported significant improvement in his symptoms and was able to resume all his activities without any restrictions. The incision site was completely healed, and there were no signs of infection. The patient was advised to continue with his medications for hypertension and hyperlipidemia and to follow up with his surgeon in six weeks.

At the six-week follow-up appointment, the patient reported no further issues and was able to tolerate a normal diet and liquids without any difficulty. The patient was advised to continue with his medications and to follow up with his surgeon in three months.

At the three-month follow-up appointment, the patient reported no issues and was able to perform all activities without any restrictions. The patient's hypertension and hyperlipidemia were well-controlled, and there were no signs of recurrent gallstones. The patient was advised to continue with his medications and to follow up with his surgeon in six months.

Overall, the patient had an uncomplicated postoperative course and made a full recovery after the laparoscopic cholecystectomy.
