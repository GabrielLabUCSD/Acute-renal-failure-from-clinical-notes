Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and sciatica. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He is a candidate for lumbar fusion surgery.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. He is a non-smoker and denies any significant medical history.

Preoperative workup includes:

* Complete Blood Count (CBC) within normal limits
* Electrolyte panel within normal limits
* Lipid profile within normal limits
* Urinalysis within normal limits
* Chest X-ray within normal limits
* Electrocardiogram (ECG) within normal limits

On the day of surgery, the patient is brought to the operating room and placed under general anesthesia. The surgery proceeds uneventfully, and the patient is taken to the recovery room in stable condition.

Postoperative course:

* The patient experiences some discomfort and pain, which is managed with adequate analgesia.
* He is encouraged to ambulate and perform deep breathing exercises to prevent respiratory complications.
* He is started on a blood thinner to prevent deep vein thrombosis.
* He is monitored closely for any signs of surgical site infection or neurological deficits.

Complications:

* The patient develops a mild fever on the first postoperative day, which is treated with antibiotics.
* He experiences some nausea and vomiting, which is managed with antiemetics.
* He has a temporary increase in pain, which is managed with additional analgesia.
* He has some constipation, which is treated with laxatives and stool softeners.

The patient is discharged from the hospital on the fifth postoperative day, in stable condition, with instructions for wound care and follow-up appointments.

At three-month follow-up, the patient reports significant improvement in his back pain and sciatica, and is able to ambulate without assistance. He is satisfied with the outcome of the surgery.
