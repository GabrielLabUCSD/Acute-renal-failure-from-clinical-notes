Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic low back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried conservative management including physical therapy and medication but has not had adequate relief. He is a candidate for lumbar fusion surgery.

Medical History:

The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes mellitus. He had a myocardial infarction 10 years ago and has been compliant with medications and lifestyle modifications since then. He does not smoke or drink alcohol.

Surgical History:

The patient has had a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair in the past.

Medications:

The patient is currently taking lisinopril, atorvastatin, metformin, and pregabalin.

Family History:

The patient's father died of a myocardial infarction at age 55. His mother has hypertension and hyperlipidemia.

Social History:

The patient is married with two adult children. He works as an accountant and is able to perform his job duties with some limitations due to his back pain.

Review of Systems:

The patient reports no unusual symptoms other than back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has been compliant with his medications and follow-up appointments.

Physical Examination:

The patient's vital signs are normal. He has a mildly elevated blood pressure. His cardiovascular examination reveals a regular rhythm with no murmurs or extra sounds. His respiratory examination is unremarkable. His abdominal examination shows no tenderness or guarding. His musculoskeletal examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lumbar spine with decreased range of motion. His neurological examination shows decreased sensation in the L4-S1 dermatomes and decreased strength in the left lower extremity.

Imaging Studies:

The patient has had multiple imaging studies, including X-rays, CT scans, and MRI scans, which show multi-level degenerative changes in the lumbar spine with spondylolisthesis and stenosis.

Lab Results:

The patient's lab results show normal complete blood count, electrolytes, and renal function. His lipid profile shows elevated triglycerides and low HDL.

Plan:

The patient will undergo lumbar fusion surgery with instrumentation. He will be closely monitored for blood sugar control and cardiac complications. He will be advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending or twisting for 6 weeks after surgery. He will be prescribed pain medication and will follow up with the surgeon in 2 weeks.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uncomplicated surgery and was discharged on the 3rd postoperative day. He did well in the early postoperative period but developed a wound infection on the 7th day. He was treated with antibiotics and wound care and made a full recovery. He was able to return to work 6 weeks after surgery with some limitations. He had a follow-up appointment with the surgeon 2 months after surgery and reported significant improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy symptoms.

Complications:

The patient developed a wound infection but did not have any other complications.
