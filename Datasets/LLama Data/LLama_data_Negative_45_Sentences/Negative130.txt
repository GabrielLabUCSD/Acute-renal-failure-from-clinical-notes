Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with neck, back, and radicular pain. He has a history of smoking and obesity. The patient has been diagnosed with cervical spondylosis, lumbar spondylosis, and spondylolisthesis. He has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and pain management, without significant improvement.

The patient's medical history includes hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes mellitus. He has a history of previous surgery, including a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair. He takes medications including aspirin, lisinopril, and metformin. He has allergies to penicillin and sulfa.

The patient's physical examination shows limited range of motion in the neck and back, and positive signs of nerve root compression. His sensory examination is intact, and his muscle strength is 5/5. His reflexes are 2+ and symmetrical.

Imaging studies, including an MRI, show significant stenosis at C3-4 and L4-5, and a large central disc protrusion at C5-6.

Surgical Plan:
The patient is scheduled for a C5-6 anterior cervical discectomy and fusion with a titanium cage and plate.

Postoperative Course:
The patient's postoperative course was uneventful. He experienced some pain and discomfort, which was managed with pain medication. He was able to return to his normal activities within 6 weeks. He had no complications during his hospital stay or after discharge.

Follow-up:
The patient had a follow-up appointment 6 weeks post-op, and imaging studies showed good fusion and no evidence of graft subsidence. He reported significant improvement in his neck and back pain, and was able to resume all his previous activities without any restrictions. He continued to follow up with his surgeon and primary care physician for ongoing management of his medical conditions.

The patient had no complications during the postoperative period, including no signs of infection, wound dehiscence, or neurological deficits. He did not develop any new symptoms or have any unplanned hospitalizations. His wound healed properly, and he had no issues with his surgical site. He had adequate pain control and no evidence of respiratory or cardiovascular complications. He had a successful outcome and was satisfied with the results of his surgery.
