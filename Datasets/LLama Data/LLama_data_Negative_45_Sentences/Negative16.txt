Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male, who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The pain started after a work-related injury 10 years ago and has been gradually worsening over time. The patient has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medications, but has not found significant relief.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and has been smoking 1 pack of cigarettes per day for the past 20 years. He has also been diagnosed with obstructive sleep apnea and uses a continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) machine at night.

The patient underwent a lumbar MRI, which revealed a large central disc herniation at L4-L5, with severe spinal stenosis and bilateral neural foraminal stenosis. The patient was referred to a spine surgeon and was recommended to undergo a decompression surgery.

The patient underwent a decompression surgery, which included a L4-L5 laminotomy and facetectomy, as well as a bilateral L4-L5 foraminotomy. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately 2 hours to complete.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a uneventful postoperative course. He was monitored in the recovery room for several hours before being transferred to the ward. He was started on a PCA pump for pain management and was able to control his pain effectively.

The patient was able to ambulate independently on the first postoperative day and was able to go home on the second postoperative day. He was advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending or twisting for the first 6 weeks after surgery.

The patient followed up with his surgeon 2 weeks after surgery and reported significant improvement in his pain and function. He was able to walk longer distances without pain and was able to perform his daily activities without any significant limitations.

The patient had a follow-up MRI 6 weeks after surgery, which revealed significant improvement in the spinal stenosis and neural foraminal stenosis. The patient was able to discontinue his pain medications and was able to return to work 8 weeks after surgery.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during his postoperative course. He had some minor discomfort and swelling at the incision site, which resolved with time. He also experienced some mild numbness in his legs, which improved over time.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgery and was able to achieve significant pain relief and improvement in his function. He was able to return to his normal activities and was satisfied with the outcome of his surgery.
