Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has been experiencing worsening pain over the past year, with difficulty ambulating and performing daily activities. The patient has a history of smoking and alcohol use.

The patient"s medical history includes hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and a history of non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus. The patient has been advised to undergo surgery to address the root cause of the pain, which is believed to be a herniated disc.

On examination, the patient has a reduced range of motion in the lumbar spine and left lower extremity weakness. The patient also reports numbness and tingling sensations in the left lower extremity.

Preoperative Diagnostic Testing:

The patient has undergone MRI and CT scans, which have revealed a herniated disc at L4-L5. The patient has also undergone an EMG study, which has confirmed the diagnosis of left L5 nerve root compression.

Operative Course:

The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 discectomy and fusion surgery. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient tolerated the procedure well.

The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. The patient was monitored in the intensive care unit for the first 24 hours post-surgery and was then transferred to the ward. The patient was started on pain management medication and physical therapy to improve the range of motion and strength in the lower back and left lower extremity.

The patient was advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending or twisting for the first three months after surgery. The patient was also advised to avoid smoking and to continue with physical therapy exercises to improve the outcomes.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any complications during the postoperative period. The patient"s pain improved significantly, and the patient was able to ambulate and perform daily activities without difficulty.
