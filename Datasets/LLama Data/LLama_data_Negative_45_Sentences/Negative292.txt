Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he is a current smoker. He denies any previous surgeries or significant medical history.

Preoperative Evaluation:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative evaluation, including laboratory tests and imaging studies. His bloodwork revealed elevated inflammatory markers and electrolyte imbalances. An MRI of the lumbar spine showed a large extruded disc at L5-S1 with significant nerve root compression.

Operative Course:

The patient underwent a successful L5-S1 discectomy and fusion. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient tolerated it well. The operative report noted good exposure, adequate decompression, and proper fusion.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a uneventful postoperative course. He was started on intravenous pain medication and gradually transitioned to oral medications. He experienced some nausea and vomiting in the immediate postoperative period, but it was well-controlled with antiemetics. The patient was ambulatory on the first postoperative day and was able to sit and walk without significant pain. He was discharged home on postoperative day 2.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications in the immediate postoperative period. He did develop a mild wound infection, which was treated with oral antibiotics. He also experienced some numbness and tingling in his feet, which resolved gradually over time. There were no signs of neurological deficits or delayed wound healing.

Follow-up:

The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon at 2 weeks and 6 weeks postoperatively. At both visits, he reported significant improvement in his pain and function. He was able to return to work at 8 weeks postoperatively, and he reported improved mobility and reduced discomfort.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, this patient's surgical course was uneventful, with effective pain management and a smooth postoperative recovery. The patient did not experience any significant complications, and he was able to return to his normal activities with improved symptoms. Further follow-up appointments will be necessary to monitor his progress and ensure that the fusion is solid.
