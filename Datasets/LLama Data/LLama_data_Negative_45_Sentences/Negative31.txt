Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has been diagnosed with a lumbar herniated disc and is scheduled for a lumbar discectomy.

Medical History:

The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes mellitus. He has been smoking for the past 10 years and has a history of alcohol use disorder. He has no known allergies and has never had a previous surgical procedure.

Medications:

The patient is currently taking lisinopril for hypertension, atorvastatin for hyperlipidemia, and metformin for diabetes mellitus. He is also taking hydrocodone for pain management.

Surgical History:

The patient has never had a previous surgical procedure.

Family History:

The patient's father died of a myocardial infarction at the age of 55. His mother has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia.

Social History:

The patient is married with two children. He works as a construction worker and has been unable to work for the past 6 months due to his back pain. He denies any recent travel or exposure to illness.

Review of Systems:

The patient reports a 7/10 pain level in his back and left leg. He also reports numbness and tingling in his left foot and toes. He has difficulty ambulating and has been using a cane for assistance. He denies any bowel or bladder issues.

Physical Exam:

On physical exam, the patient's vital signs are within normal limits. He has decreased sensation to light touch in his left foot and toes. He has a positive straight leg raise test on the left side. His muscle strength is 4/5 in his left lower extremity.

Imaging Studies:

An MRI of the lumbar spine reveals a large central herniated disc at L4-L5.

Preoperative Course:

The patient was seen in the clinic for preoperative evaluation. His blood work and imaging studies were reviewed, and he was deemed a suitable candidate for surgery. He was instructed to stop taking his medications 24 hours prior to surgery and to avoid eating or drinking after midnight the night before surgery.

Surgical Procedure:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar discectomy. The surgical site was clean and dry, and there were no intraoperative complications.

Postoperative Course:

The patient tolerated the procedure well and was able to ambulate within a few hours of surgery. He experienced some postoperative pain, which was managed with hydrocodone. He was discharged home the following day with instructions to rest and avoid heavy lifting or bending.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any complications during his postoperative course.

Follow-up:

The patient was seen in the clinic for follow-up 2 weeks after surgery. He reported a significant improvement in his pain level and was able to ambulate without assistance. His wound was clean and dry, and he was instructed to continue taking his medications as prescribed. He was also referred to physical therapy for further rehabilitation.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent a successful lumbar discectomy for a herniated disc. He tolerated the procedure well and did not experience any complications during his postoperative course. He was discharged home with instructions to rest and avoid heavy lifting or bending. Follow-up appointment was made for 2 weeks after surgery, and he was referred to physical therapy for further rehabilitation.
