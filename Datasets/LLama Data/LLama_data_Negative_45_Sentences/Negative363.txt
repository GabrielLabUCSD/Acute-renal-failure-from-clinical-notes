Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and lower extremity radiculopathy. The patient has a history of lumbar degenerative disc disease and has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and pain management, without significant relief. The patient is considering surgical intervention and has been referred to our office for evaluation.

Medical History:

The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. He had a myocardial infarction 10 years ago and has been compliant with medications and lifestyle modifications since then. He has no history of bleeding disorders or previous surgical interventions.

Medications:

The patient is currently taking aspirin, lisinopril, atorvastatin, and omeprazole.

Allergies:

The patient has no known allergies to medications or latex.

Family History:

The patient's father had a history of coronary artery disease, and his mother had a history of breast cancer.

Social History:

The patient is a current smoker and drinks alcohol occasionally. He works as a truck driver and has a high school education. He lives with his wife and two adult children.

Review of Systems:

The patient reports constant lower back pain (8/10) and bilateral lower extremity radiculopathy (5/10). He also experiences occasional numbness and tingling in his feet. The patient has no significant change in bowel or bladder function.

Physical Exam:

On examination, the patient has a decreased lumbar lordosis and bilateral lower extremity weakness (4/5). His reflexes are diminished, and he has decreased sensation to light touch and pinprick in his feet.

Diagnostic Tests:

The patient has had multiple imaging studies, including X-rays, CT scans, and MRI studies, which have shown lumbar degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis.

Plan:

Based on the patient's history, physical exam, and diagnostic tests, it is recommend that the patient undergo a lumbar decompression surgery, including a lumbar laminectomy and fusion. The patient will be closely monitored for postoperative complications, including infection, bleeding, and neurological deficits.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He was discharged home on postoperative day 3 and had follow-up appointments with his surgeon at 2 weeks and 6 weeks. He reported significant improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy at his 6-week follow-up appointment.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during his postoperative course.

Conclusion:

The patient presented with a history of chronic back pain and lower extremity radiculopathy. He had a thorough evaluation, including imaging studies, and was found to have lumbar degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis. The patient underwent a successful lumbar decompression surgery and had an uneventful postoperative course. He reported significant improvement in his symptoms at his 6-week follow-up appointment.
