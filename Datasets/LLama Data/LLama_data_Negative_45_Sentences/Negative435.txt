Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic low back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He is a candidate for lumbar fusion surgery.

Medical History:

The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and chronic kidney disease (CKD) stage 3. He had a myocardial infarction 10 years ago and has been compliant with his medications and follow-up appointments. He has no history of bleeding disorders or previous surgeries.

Medications:

The patient is currently taking lisinopril, atorvastatin, and aspirin. He has no known allergies to medications.

Family History:

The patient's mother died of breast cancer at the age of 60. His father had a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia.

Social History:

The patient is a retired construction worker and leads a sedentary lifestyle. He has a history of smoking but quit 10 years ago. He drinks alcohol occasionally.

Review of Systems:

The patient reports chronic low back pain (8/10) and left leg radiculopathy (6/10) with numbness and tingling in his toes. He also experiences occasional headaches and fatigue. He has no history of vomiting, fever, or recent weight loss.

Physical Exam:

On examination, the patient's blood pressure is 140/80 mmHg, pulse rate is 70 bpm, and respiratory rate is 16 breaths/min. His temperature is 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit. He has a mildly tender abdomen with no guarding or rebound tenderness. His lower extremities show mild weakness (4/5) with decreased sensation in his left leg.

Lab Results:

The patient's complete blood count, electrolytes, and renal function tests are within normal limits. His creatinine kinase level is mildly elevated at 150 IU/L. His chest X-ray and electrocardiogram are normal.

Imaging Studies:

The patient's MRI scan shows a L4-L5 disc herniation with moderate spinal stenosis.

Plan:

The patient will undergo lumbar fusion surgery. He will be monitored closely for blood pressure and fluid management during the perioperative period. He will receive standard anesthesia and surgical protocols.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful surgery and was transferred to the recovery room. He was monitored closely for the first 24 hours and had stable vital signs. He experienced some nausea and vomiting, which were managed with medication. He was able to ambulate independently on the first postoperative day and had minimal pain. He was discharged home on the second postoperative day with a follow-up appointment in 2 weeks.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any significant complications during his hospital stay. He had some minor wound discomfort, which was managed with pain medication. He was advised to avoid heavy lifting and bending for 6 weeks.

Follow-up:

The patient followed up with his surgeon 2 weeks after discharge. He reported some intermittent lower back pain but was otherwise doing well. His wound was healing properly, and he was advised to continue his postoperative course. He was also referred to physical therapy for rehabilitation.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent successful lumbar fusion surgery and had an uneventful postoperative course. He did not experience any significant complications and was discharged home within 2 days. His follow-up appointment showed satisfactory progress, and he was advised to continue his postoperative course and physical therapy.
