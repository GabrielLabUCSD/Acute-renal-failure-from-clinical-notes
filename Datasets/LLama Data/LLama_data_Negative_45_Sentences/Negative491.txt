Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and decreased mobility. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and pain management, without significant relief. The patient has a history of smoking and has been advised to quit smoking before surgery.

Surgical History:

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery 10 years ago, which provided temporary relief but ultimately failed. He has had multiple subsequent surgeries for hardware revisions and fusions, including a revision of the previous fusion surgery 5 years ago.

Medical History:

The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obstructive sleep apnea. He had a myocardial infarction 5 years ago and has been compliant with medications and lifestyle modifications since then.

Medications:

The patient is currently taking aspirin, atorvastatin, lisinopril, and oxycodone. He has been advised to discontinue aspirin and oxycodone 7 days before surgery.

Allergies:

The patient is allergic to codeine and has a history of anaphylaxis.

Social History:

The patient lives with his wife and has two adult children. He works as an accountant and has taken 6 weeks off work for recovery. The patient has a history of alcohol abuse but has been sober for the past 5 years.

Postoperative Course:
The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He was transferred to the step-down unit on postoperative day 2 and was discharged home on postoperative day 5. He had some nausea and vomiting on the first postoperative day, which was treated with ondansetron. The patient had some pain on the second postoperative day, which was managed with oxycodone. He did not have any complications during his hospital stay.

Follow-up:
The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon 2 weeks after discharge. He reported some lingering back pain but was generally satisfied with his progress. The surgeon recommended continued physical therapy and pain management. The patient had a further follow-up appointment 6 weeks after discharge, at which point he was progressing well and had returned to work.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any complications during his hospital stay or follow-up period.

Conclusion:

The patient had a successful surgery and uneventful postoperative course. He was discharged home without any complications and had a satisfactory follow-up. The patient was advised to continue physical therapy and pain management for ongoing symptoms.
