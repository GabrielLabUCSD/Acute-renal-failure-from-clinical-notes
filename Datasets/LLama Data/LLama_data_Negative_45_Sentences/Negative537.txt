Patient Summary:

The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and bilateral knee pain. He has a history of degenerative disc disease, spinal stenosis, and bilateral knee osteoarthritis. The patient has tried conservative management including physical therapy, pain medication, and injections without significant relief. He is planning to undergo a lumbar fusion and bilateral knee replacement surgery.

The patient's medical history includes hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and diabetes mellitus. He has a history of smoking but quit 5 years ago. He denies any significant cardiovascular or respiratory history. He takes aspirin, metformin, and atorvastatin regularly.

The patient's surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair. He has no history of previous back or knee surgeries.

The patient's family history is significant for cardiovascular disease and hyperlipidemia. His mother died of a myocardial infarction at the age of 65, and his father had a history of hyperlipidemia.

The patient's social history includes a history of heavy alcohol use but denies any recent alcohol use. He is a retired construction worker and has a history of heavy lifting and bending. He lives alone and has a support system consisting of his wife and two adult children.

The patient's review of systems is significant for bilateral knee pain, chronic back pain, and numbness and tingling in his lower extremities. He denies any recent weight loss, fevers, or chest pain. He has a history of sleep apnea and uses a continuous positive airway pressure machine.

The patient's physical examination shows limited mobility in his knees and lower back. He has tenderness to palpation over his lumbar spine and bilateral knees. His neurological examination shows decreased sensation in his lower extremities.

 The patient's laboratory results show a hemoglobin A1C of 7.5%, a total cholesterol of 220 mg/dL, and a high-sensitivity C-reactive protein of 12 mg/L. His blood work also shows normal renal function and electrolyte levels.

The patient's radiological studies show multilevel degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis. His bilateral knee X-rays show advanced osteoarthritis.

The patient's surgical course was uneventful. He tolerated the procedure well and had minimal postoperative pain. He was discharged home on postoperative day 3.

The patient's postoperative course was complicated by a wound infection. He developed fevers and chills on postoperative day 5 and was readmitted to the hospital for intravenous antibiotics. His wound healed after a course of antibiotics, and he was discharged home on postoperative day 10.

The patient's follow-up appointment showed improved mobility and decreased pain. His wound was well-healed, and there were no signs of infection. His hemoglobin A1C was 6.8%, total cholesterol was 180 mg/dL, and high-sensitivity C-reactive protein was 6 mg/L.

The patient is currently doing well and has returned to his usual activities. He denies any significant pain or discomfort and is grateful for the surgical intervention.
