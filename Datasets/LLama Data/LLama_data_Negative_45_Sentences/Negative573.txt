Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has been experiencing worsening symptoms over the past year, with difficulty ambulating and performing daily activities. The patient has tried physical therapy and medication without significant relief.

The patient's medical history includes hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and a history of smoking. The patient has no known allergies and has not had any previous surgeries. The patient's family history is positive for cardiovascular disease and cancer.

The patient's physical examination reveals limited mobility in the lower back and left leg, with positive findings for sciatic nerve root compression. The patient's neurological examination shows decreased sensation and strength in the left lower extremity.

Imaging studies, including an MRI, reveal a herniated disc at L4-L5 and spinal stenosis. Electromyography and nerve conduction studies confirm the diagnosis of sciatic nerve root compression.

The patient is scheduled for a lumbar decompression surgery, which includes a lumbar laminectomy and fusion. The patient is aware of the risks and benefits of the surgery, including potential complications such as infection, nerve damage, and blood clots.

The patient's postoperative course is uneventful, with adequate pain management and improvement in mobility. The patient is able to ambulate independently within the first few days after surgery and experiences significant improvement in symptoms.

The patient's follow-up appointment at six weeks postoperative shows continued improvement in symptoms and mobility. The patient is able to return to work and resume normal activities without any restrictions.

At one year follow-up, the patient reports continued satisfaction with the outcome of the surgery and has not experienced any complications or recurrence of symptoms. The patient's neurological examination shows full recovery of sensation and strength in the left lower extremity.

In conclusion, this patient's medical history and postoperative course demonstrate a successful outcome from lumbar decompression surgery for the treatment of sciatic nerve root compression.
