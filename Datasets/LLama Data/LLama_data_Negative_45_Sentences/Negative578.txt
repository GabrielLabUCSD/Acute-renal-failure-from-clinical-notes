The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, but has not found adequate relief. He is now considering surgery to address his condition.

Imaging studies, including X-rays and MRI, confirm the presence of degenerative disc disease and spinal stenosis.

After surgery, the patient will be closely monitored in the intensive care unit for the first 24 hours. He will receive intravenous pain medication and will be encouraged to breathe deeply and cough frequently to help prevent respiratory complications.

The patient is at low risk for complications related to surgery. However, as with any surgical procedure, there are potential risks and complications, including infection, bleeding, and nerve damage. In addition, there is a small risk of developing a pulmonary embolism or deep vein thrombosis.
