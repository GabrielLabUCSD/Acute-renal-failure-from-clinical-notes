The patient is a 45-year-old male, who presents with a history of chronic back pain and bilateral knee pain. The patient's current symptoms include chronic back pain, which radiates to his right leg, and bilateral knee pain. He also experiences occasional numbness and tingling in his right hand.

On examination, the patient has limited mobility in his back and knees. He has tenderness to palpation over the lumbar spine and bilateral knees. There is no evidence of neurological deficit or motor weakness.

The patient's laboratory results show elevated levels of inflammatory markers, including erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR) and C-reactive protein (CRP). His lipid profile shows elevated levels of cholesterol and triglycerides.

Procedure:
The patient is scheduled to undergo a total knee replacement and spinal fusion surgery.

The patient had an uneventful postoperative course. He was able to ambulate with assistance on the first postoperative day and was able to walk independently by the third postoperative day.  Follow-Up:
The patient's follow-up appointments showed that his pain had significantly improved, and he was able to ambulate without assistance.
