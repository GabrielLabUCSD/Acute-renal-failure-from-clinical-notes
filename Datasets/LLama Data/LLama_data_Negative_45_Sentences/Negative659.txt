Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a 2-year history of right knee pain. The pain is described as a dull ache that worsens with activity and improves with rest. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. He has tried physical therapy, bracing, and analgesics without significant relief. The patient has a history of smoking but has abstained for the past 5 years. He drinks alcohol occasionally and denies any illicit drug use.

On examination, the patient has full range of motion in the right knee without evidence of effusion or warmth. There is mild tenderness to palpation over the medial tibial plateau. The patient's blood work reveals a normal white blood cell count, platelet count, and electrolyte levels. His serum creatinine is elevated at 1.6 mg/dL. The patient's ECG shows sinus rhythm with a heart rate of 70 beats per minute. His echocardiogram is normal.

The patient underwent an arthroscopic medial meniscectomy. The surgical course was uncomplicated, and the patient was discharged on the first postoperative day with instructions to participate in physical therapy.

Postoperative Course:
The patient's postoperative pain was well-controlled with oral pain medication. He was able to ambulate without assistance and had no evidence of complications. The patient was advised to limit weight-bearing activities for 6 weeks and to participate in physical therapy to regain strength and range of motion in the right knee.

Complications:
The patient did not experience any complications during his postoperative course.

Conclusion:
This patient with a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia presented with chronic right knee pain that was unresponsive to conservative management. He underwent an arthroscopic medial meniscectomy, and his postoperative course was uneventful. The patient's serum creatinine level was elevated, but this did not pose a significant concern for renal dysfunction. Considering the patient's history of smoking and hypertension, it is imperative to monitor his renal function closely in the postoperative period. The patient's postoperative pain was well-controlled, and he was able to ambulate without assistance. The patient will continue with physical therapy to regain strength and range of motion in the right knee.
