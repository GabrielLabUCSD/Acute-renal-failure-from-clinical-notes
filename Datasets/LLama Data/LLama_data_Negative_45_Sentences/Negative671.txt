Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male, who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has been diagnosed with a lumbar disc herniation and spinal stenosis. The patient has tried conservative management, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He is now considering surgical intervention.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he is currently taking medication to manage these conditions. He has no known allergies and has never had a previous surgical procedure.

The patient"s surgical plan includes a lumbar decompression and fusion. The procedure will involve an incision in the lower back, and the surgeon will remove the herniated disc and bone spurs that are pressing on the nerves. The spine will then be stabilized with metal hardware.

Postoperative Course:

The patient tolerated the procedure well and was transferred to the recovery room in stable condition. He was monitored closely for vital signs and neurological status. He experienced some discomfort and pain in the lower back, which was managed effectively with pain medication.

The patient was encouraged to ambulate within the first 24 hours post-surgery, and he was able to do so without difficulty. He was discharged from the hospital after a two-day stay, and he was advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for several weeks.

Complications:

The patient developed a wound infection at the incision site, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some numbness and tingling in the left leg, which resolved within a few weeks. There were no other complications during the postoperative period.

Follow-up:

The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon six weeks post-surgery. At that time, he reported significant improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy. He was able to walk without assistance and had resumed his normal activities. The surgeon was pleased with his progress and recommended continued physical therapy to further improve his strength and flexibility.

Overall, the patient had a successful surgical outcome and a smooth postoperative course. He was able to manage his pain effectively and did not experience any significant complications.
