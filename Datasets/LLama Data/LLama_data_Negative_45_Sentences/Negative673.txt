Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The pain has been worsening over the past year, and the patient has tried various conservative treatments without relief. He has a history of smoking and has a positive family history of degenerative disc disease. The patient has been diagnosed with lumbar spondylolisthesis and disk herniation at L4-L5.

The patient's medical history includes hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and obesity. The patient has a history of non-insulin-dependent diabetes mellitus and has been taking metformin for the past five years. He has had no significant surgical history and has no known allergy.

The patient's current medications include:

* Metformin 1000mg orally twice a day
* Lisinopril 20mg orally once a day
* Simvastatin 40mg orally once a day
* Naproxen 500mg orally twice a day

The patient's review of systems is significant for:

* Chronic lower back pain (8/10)
* Left leg radiculopathy (5/10)
* Paresthesias in the left foot and toes
* Difficulty ambulating long distances
* Difficulty standing for long periods
* Difficulty bending or twisting
* No history of recent significant weight loss, fever, chills, or night sweats

The patient's physical examination reveals:

* Blood pressure: 140/90 mmHg
* Pulse: 80/min
* Respiratory rate: 16/min
* Temperature: 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit
* Height: 5 feet 10 inches
* Weight: 250 lbs
* Body Mass Index (BMI): 35.2
* Spinal examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the lumbar spine, especially at L4-L5
* Decreased sensation to light touch in the left foot and toes
* Weakness in the left leg (grade 4/5)
* Positive straight leg raise test

Imaging studies include:

* MRI of the lumbar spine revealing lumbar spondylolisthesis and disk herniation at L4-L5
* CT scan of the lumbar spine revealing no significant stenosis or osteological abnormalities

The patient is scheduled for a lumbar decompression surgery. The patient has been educated about the risks and benefits of the procedure, and he has signed an informed consent. He is planned to have a postoperative course as follows:

* Admission to the hospital for 2-3 days
* Intravenous pain medication for the first 24 hours
* Switch to oral pain medication as needed
* Gradual mobilization and physical therapy as tolerated
* Follow-up appointment with the surgeon in 2 weeks

The patient is advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for the first 6 weeks postoperatively. He is also advised to avoid smoking and to continue his current medications as prescribed.

Postoperative complications:

* Wound infection
* Delayed wound healing
* Increased back pain
* Neurological deficits
* Respiratory complications

The patient is encouraged to contact the surgeon's office if he experiences any of these complications or if he has any concerns about his postoperative course.
