Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The patient has a history of smoking and hypertension. The patient had a lumbar MRI that showed lumbar spondylosis and a left L5-S1 herniated disc. The patient failed conservative management and is planned for a left L5-S1 hemilaminectomy and diskectomy. The patient is allergic to iodine and has a history of bleeding disorders.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a successful left L5-S1 hemilaminectomy and diskectomy. The patient had a smooth extubation and was transferred to the recovery room in stable condition. The patient had some nausea and vomiting postoperatively but quickly responded to antiemetics. The patient had a mild sore throat and dry mouth but did not have any significant respiratory or cardiovascular complications.

The patient was monitored closely for signs of neurological compromise, and none were observed. The patient was able to move his legs and feet appropriately, and sensation was intact in the L5 and S1 dermatomes. The patient had some mild postoperative pain but quickly responded to pain medication.

The patient was discharged from the hospital on postoperative day #2 and was advised to follow up with his surgeon in 2 weeks. The patient was also advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending or twisting for the next 6 weeks.

Complications:

The patient did not have any complications during or after surgery.
