Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He is a candidate for lumbar fusion surgery.

The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, which are well-controlled with medication. He is a non-smoker and has a healthy appetite.

Preoperative lab work shows a normal complete blood count, electrolyte panel, and coagulation profile. Urinalysis is normal except for a trace amount of protein.

The patient underwent lumbar fusion surgery without any complications. He had a thorough postoperative course, with adequate pain management and appropriate wound healing.

On the second postoperative day, the patient experienced some nausea and vomiting, which was managed with antiemetics.

On the fifth postoperative day, the patient developed a fever of 101.3°F (38.5°C), which was treated with antibiotics. Blood cultures were drawn, and the patient was started on broad-spectrum antibiotics.

The patient's fever resolved within 24 hours, and he continued to make a full recovery. He was discharged home on the seventh postoperative day.

Follow-up appointments with the surgeon and primary care physician showed no signs of complications or infection. The patient was able to resume his normal activities, including work and exercise, without any limitations.

There were no instances of acute renal failure or any other major complications throughout the patient's postoperative course.
