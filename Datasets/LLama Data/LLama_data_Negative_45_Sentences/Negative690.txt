The patient is a 55-year-old male who presents with a long history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, but has not experienced significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and has been smoke-free for 10 years. He denies any recent weight loss or change in bowel habits.

The patient's surgical history includes a cholecystectomy 10 years ago, and he has no known allergies to medications or anesthesia. He is currently taking aspirin, lisinopril, and atorvastatin. His last colonoscopy was 5 years ago and was normal. The patient has no history of bleeding disorders or previous surgical complications.

The patient's physical examination reveals a temperature of 98.6 degrees Fahrenheit, blood pressure of 130/80 mmHg, heart rate of 70 beats per minute, and an oxygen saturation of 98% on room air. His respiratory and cardiovascular examinations are normal, and there is no evidence of neurological deficits. The patient's abdominal examination reveals a surgical scar from the previous cholecystectomy, and there is no tenderness or guarding. The patient's left leg radiculopathy is evident, with decreased muscle strength and reflexes compared to the right leg.

The patient's postoperative course is uncomplicated, and he is discharged home on the second postoperative day. He is advised to avoid heavy lifting, bending, or twisting for 4 weeks, and is prescribed hydrocodone for pain management. The patient follows up with his surgeon 2 weeks after discharge, and reports significant improvement in his back pain and radiculopathy. He is gradually weaned off the hydrocodone over the next 2 weeks, and is advised to continue with physical therapy to improve his strength and mobility.

There are no complications during the postoperative period, and the patient's wound heals without any issues. He is advised to continue with his usual medications, and follows up with his primary care physician for ongoing management of his hypertension and hyperlipidemia. The patient is satisfied with the outcome of his surgery and reports significant improvement in his quality of life.
