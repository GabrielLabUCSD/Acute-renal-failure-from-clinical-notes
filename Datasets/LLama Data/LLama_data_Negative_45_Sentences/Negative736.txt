Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic lower back pain and left leg radiculopathy. The pain started after a fall from a height 10 years ago and has been gradually worsening over time. The patient has tried various treatments including physical therapy, pain medication, and injections without significant relief.

The patient has a history of hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and smoking. He has a family history of cardiovascular disease and his mother died of a myocardial infarction at the age of 60.

On physical examination, the patient has limited mobility in the lower back and left leg, with tenderness to palpation over the lumbar spine. His straight leg raise test is positive on the left side. His neurological examination shows decreased sensation to light touch and vibration in the L5 and S1 dermatomes.

Imaging studies show a large L4-L5 disc herniation with compression of the left L5 nerve root. An MRI scan also shows mild spinal stenosis and facet joint degeneration.

The patient's surgical plan includes a L4-L5 decompression and discectomy. The patient is concerned about the risks of surgery, especially in relation to his smoking history and cardiovascular risk factors.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had an uneventful procedure and was extubated in the operating room. He was transferred to the intensive care unit for close monitoring and pain management.

On the first postoperative day, the patient had some nausea and vomiting, which responded well to antiemetics. His pain was managed with patient-controlled analgesia and he was able to ambulate with assistance.

On the second postoperative day, the patient's pain had improved significantly, and he was able to walk independently. His wound was clean and dry, and there were no signs of infection.

On the third postoperative day, the patient developed a fever and tachycardia. Blood cultures were drawn, and the patient was started on broad-spectrum antibiotics. The fever resolved within 24 hours, and the patient continued to recover uneventfully.

The patient was discharged home on the fifth postoperative day with a follow-up appointment in the clinic. He was advised to continue with pain medication and physical therapy to improve his mobility and strength.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during his postoperative course. However, he did develop a mild wound infection on the seventh postoperative day, which was treated with oral antibiotics. The patient also experienced some constipation, which was managed with laxatives and stool softeners.

Conclusion:

The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 decompression and discectomy, with an uneventful postoperative course. He experienced some minor complications, including a mild wound infection and constipation, but these were managed effectively. The patient's pain and mobility improved significantly after surgery, and he was able to return to his normal activities.
