Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. He is currently smoking 1 pack of cigarettes per day and has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia. The patient's surgical history includes a cholecystectomy and a hernia repair.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient underwent a preoperative workup, which included a thorough physical examination, laboratory tests, and imaging studies. His blood work showed mild anemia and elevated inflammatory markers. A CT scan of the spine revealed severe degenerative disc disease at L4-L5 and L5-S1, with significant spinal stenosis and bilateral facet joint degeneration.

Operative Course:

The patient underwent a lumbar fusion surgery, which involved the removal of the degenerated disc and the fusion of the L4-L5 and L5-S1 vertebrae. The surgery was performed under general anesthesia and took approximately 3 hours to complete.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course, with minimal pain and discomfort. He was able to ambulate independently on the first postoperative day and was discharged home on the second postoperative day. He was prescribed pain medication and muscle relaxants to manage his discomfort.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during his postoperative course. However, he did develop a mild wound infection, which was treated with antibiotics. He also experienced some numbness and tingling in his legs, which resolved within a few weeks.

Follow-up:

The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon 2 weeks after his discharge. At that time, his wound was healing well, and he had minimal pain and discomfort. He was advised to continue with his pain medication and to gradually resume his normal activities.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, this patient with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease underwent a lumbar fusion surgery and had a smooth postoperative course. Although he developed a mild wound infection and some numbness and tingling in his legs, these complications were managed effectively, and he was able to resume his normal activities within a few weeks.
