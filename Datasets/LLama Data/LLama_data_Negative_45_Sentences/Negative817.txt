Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and left leg radiculopathy. He has tried various conservative treatments, including physical therapy and medication, without significant relief. The patient has a history of hypertension and hyperlipidemia, and he is a former smoker.

Preoperative Workup:

The patient underwent a thorough preoperative workup, including an MRI of the lumbar spine, which revealed a large L4-L5 disc herniation. His blood work showed normal liver and kidney function, and his INR was 1.2. The patient's ECG showed a normal sinus rhythm.

Operative Course:

The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 discectomy and decompression. The procedure was performed under general anesthesia, and the patient tolerated it well. The surgical site was clean, and there were no intraoperative complications.

Postoperative Course:

The patient had a smooth postoperative course. He experienced some expected postoperative discomfort, which was managed with pain medication. He was ambulating and doing well on the first postoperative day. The patient was discharged home on postoperative day 2 with a prescription for pain medication and instructions for wound care and physical therapy.

Complications:

The patient did not experience any major complications during his postoperative course. However, he did develop a mild wound infection that was treated with antibiotics. The infection was caught early, and the patient responded well to treatment. There were no signs of nerve damage or neurological deficits during the postoperative period.

Conclusion:

Overall, the patient had a successful surgical outcome, with adequate pain relief and no major complications. The patient's postoperative course was uneventful, and he was able to return home and resume his normal activities with minimal discomfort. The patient will continue to follow up with his surgeon and primary care physician to monitor his progress and manage any ongoing issues.
