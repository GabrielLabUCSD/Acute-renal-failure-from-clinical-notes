Patient Summary:

The patient is a 45-year-old male who presents with a history of chronic back pain and degenerative disc disease. He has been smoking for 20 years and has a history of hypertension. The patient has tried physical therapy and pain management without significant relief. He decides to undergo a lumbar fusion surgery to alleviate his symptoms. 

Preoperative Workup: 
The patient underwent a thorough preoperative workup, including blood tests, imaging studies, and a physical examination. His blood work revealed elevated blood pressure and a low serum albumin level. The patient's imaging studies showed advanced degenerative disc disease at L4-L5 and L5-S1 with canal stenosis.

Surgical Procedure: 
The patient underwent a successful L4-L5 and L5-S1 lumbar fusion surgery. The procedure included a posterior approach with instrumentation and bone grafting.

Postoperative Course: 
The patient had a uneventful postoperative course. He was extubated immediately after surgery and was able to breathe independently. He did not experience any significant pain or discomfort and was able to ambulate independently within the first 24 hours. The patient was discharged from the hospital on the third postoperative day. 

Complications: 
The patient did not experience any significant complications during his postoperative course. He had some minor wound drainage on the second postoperative day, but it resolved quickly with dressing changes. The patient did not develop any signs of infection, neuropraxia, or other complications.

Follow-up: 
The patient had a follow-up appointment with his surgeon one week after discharge. He reported some minor discomfort at the incision site, but it was manageable with pain medication. The patient was able to ambulate independently and showed significant improvement in his back pain. His follow-up appointments were uneventful, and the patient continued to improve.

Conclusion: 
In conclusion, the patient underwent a successful lumbar fusion surgery and had an uneventful postoperative course. He did not experience any significant complications and showed significant improvement in his back pain. The patient was able to ambulate independently and had a smooth recovery. The patient's outcome was optimal, and he was able to return to his normal activities.
