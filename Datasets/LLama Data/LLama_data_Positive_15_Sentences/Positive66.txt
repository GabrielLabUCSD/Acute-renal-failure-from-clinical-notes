Patient Summary:

1. The patient is a 65-year-old male.
2. He has a history of hypertension and diabetes.
3. He has been experiencing worsening knee pain over the past year.
4. He has tried physical therapy and medication with little relief.
5. He has been diagnosed with severe osteoarthritis of the knee.
6. He is planning to undergo a total knee replacement surgery.
7. He has a history of smoking but quit 10 years ago.
8. He has a history of moderate alcohol consumption.
9. He has no known allergies.
10. He is currently taking aspirin and metformin.
11. He has a history of kidney stones.
12. His last known creatinine level was 1.5 mg/dL.
13. He has been advised to stop taking aspirin and metformin 72 hours before surgery.
14. He will be admitted to the hospital on the day of surgery.
15. The surgical team has been informed of his history of renal stones and risk of acute renal failure.

Complication:

1. The patient developed acute renal failure post-surgery.
2. He experienced decreased urine output and oliguria.
3. His serum creatinine level increased to 2.5 mg/dL.
4. He was started on intravenous fluids and vasopressors.
5. A kidney ultrasound revealed mild hydronephrosis.
6. The patient was transferred to the intensive care unit for close monitoring.
7. He was treated with antibiotics for possible urinary tract infection.
8. His renal function gradually improved over 48 hours.
9. He was able to return to his surgical ward and continue recovering from surgery.
10. He will have close follow-up with his nephrologist and primary care physician for ongoing management of his renal disease.
