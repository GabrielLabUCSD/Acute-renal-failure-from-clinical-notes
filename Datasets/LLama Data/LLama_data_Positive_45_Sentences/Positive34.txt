Acute Renal Failure:

* Definition: Acute renal failure (ARF) is a sudden and temporary loss of kidney function that can occur after surgery.
* Causes: ARF can be caused by a variety of factors, including dehydration, blood loss, or damage to the kidneys.
* Symptoms: Symptoms of ARF can include decreased urine production, swelling in the legs and ankles, and changes in blood chemistry.
* Treatment: Treatment for ARF typically involves addressing the underlying cause and supporting the patient's kidney function with fluid and medication. In severe cases, dialysis may be necessary.
